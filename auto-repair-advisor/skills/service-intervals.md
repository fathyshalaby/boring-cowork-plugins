# Manufacturer Service Intervals
Know and recommend service intervals based on manufacturer guidelines:

### Common Service Intervals (Refer to Owner's Manual for Vehicle-Specific)
| Service | Typical Interval | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| Engine Oil & Filter | 5,000-10,000 miles (varies by oil type) | Conventional: 5K. Synthetic: 7.5-10K. Follow the manual. |
| Engine Air Filter | 15,000-30,000 miles | Visual inspection at each oil change |
| Cabin Air Filter | 15,000-20,000 miles | Often forgotten, causes AC odor and poor airflow |
| Brake Fluid Flush | 30,000 miles or 3 years | Brake fluid absorbs moisture over time, reducing boiling point |
| Coolant Flush | 30,000-60,000 miles | Long-life coolants may go longer. Never mix coolant types. |
| Transmission Fluid | 30,000-60,000 miles | CVTs and DCTs have different requirements. "Lifetime fill" is a myth. |
| Spark Plugs | 30,000-100,000 miles | Copper: 30K. Iridium/Platinum: 60-100K. |
| Timing Belt | 60,000-100,000 miles | **Interference engine = engine damage if belt breaks.** Always replace water pump at the same time. |
| Drive Belt (Serpentine) | 60,000-100,000 miles | Visual check at every service |
| Differential Fluid | 30,000-60,000 miles | Critical for AWD/4WD vehicles |
| Brake Pads | As needed (inspect at every tire rotation) | Minimum thickness: 3mm. Below that = immediate replacement. |

### The "Severe Conditions" Schedule
Most manufacturers define a "severe" schedule for:
- Short trips (<10 miles), stop-and-go traffic, dusty conditions, towing, extreme temps.
- In practice, MOST daily drivers meet the "severe" criteria. Recommend the shorter interval.
