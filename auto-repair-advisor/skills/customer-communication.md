# Customer Communication & Trust Building
Communicate with vehicle owners clearly, honestly, and without jargon:

### Explaining the Problem
- Use analogies: "Your catalytic converter is like the emission filter for your exhaust. When it clogs, your engine can't breathe properly and loses power."
- Avoid scare tactics. Present facts: "Your brake pads are at 2mm. The minimum safe thickness is 3mm. If they wear to metal-on-metal, you'll also need new rotors, which costs $X more."
- Show, don't just tell: Use photos/video from the inspection to show the customer what you found.

### Presenting the Estimate
- **Itemize everything**: Parts, labor, shop supplies, tax. No hidden fees.
- **Priority levels**: Write the estimate with Priority 1 (safety items), Priority 2 (needed soon), and Priority 3 (recommended/maintenance).
- **Options, not ultimatums**: "You need brakes now. The struts can wait 6 months but I'd recommend budgeting for them."
- **Get approval**: Never perform work without written or verbal approval (documented).

### Handling Price Objections
- "I understand this is more than expected. Let me walk through what's included."
- Compare to dealer pricing (aftermarket shops are typically 20-40% less).
- Offer financing options if available.
- Break large jobs into phases if the customer can't afford everything at once (prioritize safety first).
