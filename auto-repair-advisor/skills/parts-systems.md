# Vehicle Systems & Parts Knowledge
Maintain comprehensive knowledge of major vehicle systems:

### Engine Management System
- **ECM/PCM**: The computer that controls fuel injection, ignition timing, emissions, and transmission shifting.
- **MAF/MAP Sensor**: Measures air intake for fuel metering. A dirty MAF causes drivability issues. Clean with MAF sensor cleaner (never touch the wire).
- **Oxygen Sensors (O2/AF)**: Upstream (before cat) measures combustion efficiency. Downstream (after cat) monitors catalytic converter health.
- **Fuel System**: Fuel pump → fuel filter → fuel rail → fuel injectors. Modern GDI (Gasoline Direct Injection) systems operate at 2,000+ PSI and require specialized tools.

### Brake System
- **Disc brakes**: Caliper, pads, and rotor. Inspect pad thickness and rotor condition at every tire rotation.
- **Drum brakes**: Wheel cylinder, shoes, and drum. Common on rear axles of economy vehicles.
- **ABS (Anti-Lock Braking System)**: Modulates brake pressure to prevent wheel lockup. ABS faults set a dashboard warning light and require scan tool diagnosis.
- **Brake fluid**: Hygroscopic—absorbs moisture from the atmosphere. Moisture lowers the boiling point, causing brake fade. Flush every 2-3 years.

### Steering & Suspension
- **Struts/Shocks**: Dampen spring oscillation. Signs of failure: bouncing, nose-diving on braking, tire cupping.
- **Ball Joints/Tie Rod Ends**: Pivot points in the steering/suspension. Play = safety concern. Check by jacking the vehicle and testing for movement.
- **Alignment**: Check whenever tires show uneven wear, after suspension work, or after hitting a significant pothole.
