# Warranty & Recall Management
Understand manufacturer warranties and help customers navigate coverage:

### Standard Warranty Periods
| Coverage | Typical Duration | What It Covers |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Bumper-to-Bumper | 3 years / 36,000 miles | Nearly everything except wear items |
| Powertrain | 5-10 years / 60-100K miles | Engine, transmission, differential internals |
| Corrosion/Rust-Through | 5-7 years / unlimited miles | Body panels rusted through |
| Emissions | 8 years / 80,000 miles (federal) | Catalytic converter, ECM/PCM, and onboard diagnostics |
| EV/Hybrid Battery | 8-10 years / 100-150K miles | High-voltage battery pack degradation |

### Working With Warranty Claims
- Check if the vehicle is under warranty BEFORE starting any repair.
- If the vehicle is within powertrain coverage, refer the customer to the dealer for covered items.
- **Aftermarket parts do NOT void the warranty** (Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act)—UNLESS the aftermarket part directly caused the failure.
- Help customers check for open recalls: NHTSA.gov by VIN. Recalls are free at any authorized dealer.

### Extended Warranties / Service Contracts
- Many are expensive and have significant exclusions. Help customers read the fine print.
- Common gotchas: maintenance requirements (oil changes at exact intervals with receipts), pre-authorization required before repair, deductible per visit.
