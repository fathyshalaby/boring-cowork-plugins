# Electric & Hybrid Vehicle Basics
Understand EV and hybrid vehicle fundamentals as the market shifts:

### Hybrid Types
| Type | Battery | Electric Motor | Plug-In? | Electric-Only Range |
|------|---------|---------------|----------|-------------------|
| Mild Hybrid (MHEV) | 48V | Start/stop + assist | No | None |
| Full Hybrid (HEV) | ~1-2 kWh | Drive + regen | No | 1-2 miles |
| Plug-In Hybrid (PHEV) | 8-18 kWh | Drive + regen | Yes | 20-50 miles |
| Battery Electric (BEV) | 40-100+ kWh | Primary drive | Yes | 200-350+ miles |

### Safety Considerations
- **High-voltage systems**: HEV/PHEV/BEV systems operate at 200-800V. This is LETHAL. Only trained and certified technicians should work on high-voltage components.
- **Orange cables**: High-voltage cables are always orange. NEVER cut, splice, or handle them without proper PPE and training.
- **De-energization procedure**: Before any service on or near high-voltage components, the system must be de-energized per the manufacturer's procedures. This typically involves removing a service disconnect plug and waiting 5-10 minutes.

### Service Differences
- **Brake wear**: Regenerative braking significantly extends brake pad life (80K+ miles is common). Don't sell brake jobs before they're needed.
- **Oil changes**: BEVs don't need them. HEV/PHEVs still do (they have gas engines).
- **Coolant**: EVs have multiple cooling loops (battery, power electronics, cabin). Use the correct coolant type per manufacturer specs.
- **Tires**: EVs are heavier and have higher torque. They wear tires faster. Recommend EV-specific tires (lower rolling resistance, designed for higher weight).
