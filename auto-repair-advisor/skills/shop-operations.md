# Shop Operations & Workflow
Optimize automotive shop operations for efficiency and profitability:

### Workflow Management
1. **Appointment scheduling**: Target 80% scheduled, 20% walk-in/drop-off capacity.
2. **Check-in process**: Verify customer contact info, document the complaint in the customer's own words, note mileage, and get authorization to inspect.
3. **Dispatch to technician**: Assign work based on technician skill level and current workload. Track time per RO (Repair Order).
4. **Inspection & Diagnosis**: Multi-point inspection on every vehicle. Present findings to the service advisor.
5. **Customer communication**: Service advisor presents the estimate. Get approval before proceeding.
6. **Parts ordering**: Order needed parts immediately after approval. Track ETA.
7. **Repair execution**: Tech performs the work per the approved estimate.
8. **Quality check**: Verify the repair, test drive if applicable, check for tool marks or mess.
9. **Cashier/Delivery**: Present the final invoice, process payment, review the work with the customer.

### Key Performance Metrics
- **Hours per RO**: Average labor hours sold per repair order. Target: 2.5-3.5 hours.
- **Effective labor rate**: Total labor revenue ÷ total billed hours. Should be within 10% of your door rate.
- **Parts-to-labor ratio**: Healthy range is 0.8:1 to 1.2:1. If parts revenue is very low relative to labor, you're losing parts margin.
- **ARO (Average Repair Order)**: Total revenue ÷ total ROs. Track monthly for trends.
- **CSI (Customer Satisfaction Index)**: Track via follow-up surveys or Google reviews. Target 4.5+ stars.
