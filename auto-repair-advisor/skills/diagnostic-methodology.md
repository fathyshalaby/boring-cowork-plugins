# Systematic Vehicle Diagnostics
Apply a structured diagnostic approach to vehicle problems:

### Diagnostic Sequence
1. **Verify the customer complaint**: Test drive or operate the vehicle to reproduce the symptom. "Check engine light on" could mean thousands of different things.
2. **Retrieve DTCs (Diagnostic Trouble Codes)**: Connect a scan tool and pull codes. Record all pending, current, and historical codes.
3. **Research the code**: Codes indicate a CIRCUIT or SYSTEM fault, not a specific part. P0301 = "Cylinder 1 Misfire Detected"—could be spark plug, coil, injector, compression, or wiring.
4. **Follow manufacturer diagnostic procedures**: Use the OEM service manual or AllData/Mitchell for the specific vehicle's diagnostic flowchart.
5. **Test, don't guess**: Use a multimeter, oscilloscope, fuel pressure gauge, or compression tester to confirm the root cause before recommending parts.
6. **Verify the repair**: After the fix, clear codes, test drive, and confirm the fault does not return.

### Common Misdiagnosis Traps
- **Replacing oxygen sensors for a rich/lean code**: The sensor is REPORTING the condition. Find the vacuum leak, fuel pressure problem, or MAF sensor issue causing the rich/lean condition.
- **Replacing a battery for a no-start**: Test the starter, alternator, and charging system. A dead battery is often a symptom of a failed alternator.
- **Catalytic converter replacement for P0420**: Verify there isn't an exhaust leak, engine misfire, or coolant consumption damaging the converter first. The converter is expensive and may be the victim, not the cause.
