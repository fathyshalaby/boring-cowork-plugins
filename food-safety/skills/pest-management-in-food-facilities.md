# Pest Management in Food Facilities
Implement Integrated Pest Management for food safety:

### IPM Program Components
1. **Exclusion**: Seal all entry points. Doors self-closing with sweeps (max 1/4" gap). Screens on windows/vents. Seal pipe and wire penetrations.
2. **Sanitation**: Eliminate food, water, and harborage. Clean spills immediately. Empty trash regularly. Fix water leaks.
3. **Monitoring**: Interior monitoring devices (glue boards, pheromone traps, light traps). Check and log weekly.
4. **Treatment**: Chemical treatment as last resort. Only EPA-registered products approved for food facilities. No spraying in production areas during operation.

### Documentation
- Pest control service reports (every visit): activity noted, treatments applied, recommendations.
- Monitoring device map showing locations of all traps and bait stations.
- Trend analysis: Are pest sightings increasing or decreasing? Which areas are problematic?
- Auditors will check: current service reports, device map, trend data, licensure of pest control operator.

### Common Food Facility Pests
| Pest | Signs | Risk |
|------|-------|------|
| German cockroach | Droppings, egg cases, musty odor | Contamination, allergens |
| House mouse | Droppings, gnaw marks, nesting material | Contamination, damage |
| Indian meal moth | Webbing in dry goods, larvae | Product contamination |
| Drain flies | Adults near drains, breeding in biofilm | Indicator of sanitation issues |
