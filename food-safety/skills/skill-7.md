# Supplier Management & Receiving
Manage food suppliers and receiving to prevent contaminated ingredients from entering:

### Supplier Approval Program
- **Risk assessment**: Categorize ingredients by risk level. High-risk (raw animal products, allergens, imported goods) require more stringent supplier evaluation.
- **Supplier questionnaire**: Request information on food safety certifications, HACCP plans, allergen controls, recall procedures.
- **Third-party audits**: Require GFSI-benchmarked certification (SQF, BRC, FSSC 22000) for high-risk suppliers.
- **COAs (Certificates of Analysis)**: Request with each shipment for critical specs (micro testing, allergens, chemical residues).
- **Approved supplier list**: Maintain and review annually. Only purchase from approved suppliers.

### Receiving Inspection
1. Check temperature of refrigerated/frozen items immediately. Reject if: refrigerated >41°F, frozen shows thawing.
2. Inspect packaging: intact, no damage, no pest evidence, proper labeling.
3. Check dates: receiving within shelf life, date codes legible.
4. Check documentation: matches PO, correct quantities, COA if required.
5. Log receiving inspection results. Note any rejections with reason and supplier notification.
