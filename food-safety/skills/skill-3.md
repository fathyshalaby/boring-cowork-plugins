# Temperature Control & Monitoring
Master time-temperature control for food safety:

### Critical Temperatures
| Temperature | Significance |
|------------|-------------|
| 0°F (-18°C) | Frozen storage (stops bacterial growth, doesn't kill bacteria) |
| 32-41°F (0-5°C) | Refrigeration (slows bacterial growth significantly) |
| 41-135°F (5-57°C) | **DANGER ZONE** — bacteria multiply rapidly |
| 135°F (57°C) | Minimum hot holding temperature |
| 145°F (63°C) | Minimum cooking temp for whole cuts of meat, fish, eggs (for immediate service) |
| 155°F (68°C) | Minimum cooking temp for ground meat, injected meats, eggs for hot holding |
| 165°F (74°C) | Minimum cooking temp for poultry, stuffing, reheated leftovers |
| 212°F (100°C) | Boiling point of water |

### Cooling Requirements
- **Two-stage cooling**: 135°F → 70°F within 2 hours. Then 70°F → 41°F within 4 additional hours. Total: 6 hours max.
- Cooling methods: Shallow pans (≤4" depth), ice bath, ice paddle, blast chiller.
- **Date marking**: Ready-to-eat TCS food held >24 hours must be date-marked. Use within 7 days (including preparation day).

### Monitoring Equipment
- Calibrate thermometers at least daily (ice point: 32°F ± 2°F, or boiling point).
- Use data loggers for continuous monitoring of coolers, freezers, and hot-holding units.
- Infrared thermometers for receiving checks (surface temp only — not for cooking verification).
