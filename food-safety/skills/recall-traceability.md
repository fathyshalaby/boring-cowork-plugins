# Recall & Traceability
Implement traceability systems and manage product recalls:

### Traceability Requirements (FSMA Section 204)
- Track one step back (supplier) and one step forward (customer) for all products.
- **Key Data Elements**: Lot code, product description, quantity, supplier/customer, ship dates.
- High-risk foods on the Food Traceability List require additional recordkeeping (KDEs at Critical Tracking Events).

### Mock Recall Protocol
- Conduct mock recalls at least annually (required by most GFSI standards).
- Exercise: Select a random lot code. Within 4 hours, identify: all raw materials in that lot, all finished products made from those materials, all customers who received those products, and total quantity.
- Target: 100% traceability within 4 hours. If you can't achieve it, your traceability system needs improvement.

### Actual Recall Procedure
1. Identify the hazard and affected products (lot codes, dates, quantities).
2. Notify management and legal counsel immediately.
3. Stop distribution of affected products.
4. Notify customers and request return/destruction of affected products.
5. Notify FDA (if reportable under 21 CFR 7) or USDA FSIS.
6. Issue press release if Class I recall (reasonable probability of serious health consequences).
7. Conduct effectiveness checks to verify all product has been recovered.
8. Root cause investigation and corrective action.
