# HACCP Plan Development
Develop and maintain Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point plans:

### Seven HACCP Principles
1. **Conduct a hazard analysis**: Identify biological, chemical, and physical hazards at each process step.
2. **Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs)**: Points where control is essential to prevent, eliminate, or reduce hazards to acceptable levels.
3. **Establish critical limits**: Measurable boundaries for each CCP (e.g., cooking temp ≥165°F for poultry).
4. **Establish monitoring procedures**: What, how, when, and who monitors each CCP.
5. **Establish corrective actions**: Actions taken when monitoring indicates a critical limit is not met.
6. **Establish verification procedures**: Activities to confirm the HACCP system is working (calibration, record review, testing).
7. **Establish record-keeping**: Documents for hazard analysis, CCP monitoring, corrective actions, and verification.

### Common CCPs
| CCP | Hazard | Critical Limit | Monitoring |
|-----|--------|---------------|------------|
| Receiving | Biological, physical | Refrigerated items ≤41°F | Temp check every delivery |
| Cooking | Biological (pathogens) | Internal temp per food type | Probe thermometer, each batch |
| Cooling | Biological (growth) | 135°F→70°F in 2 hrs, 70°F→41°F in 4 hrs | Time/temp log |
| Hot holding | Biological | ≥135°F | Temp check every 2 hrs |
| Cold holding | Biological | ≤41°F | Temp check every 4 hrs |
