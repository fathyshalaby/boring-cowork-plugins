# Command: /mobility:a1-triage

Analyze a cross-border scenario to determine which EU Regulation Article applies.

## Output Format
```
A1 SCENARIO TRIAGE

SCENARIO ANALYSIS: [e.g., German resident working 2 days at home, 3 days at employer HQ in Austria]

1. CATEGORY IDENTIFICATION
- Is this a temporary posting on behalf of an employer (Max 24 months)? [YES/NO]
- Or is this a regular, ongoing pattern in multiple states? [YES - Multi-state]
Category: Article 13 - Persons working in two or more Member States.

2. THE 25% RESIDENCE TEST
- Country of Residence: [Germany]
- Expected working time in Residence Country: [40% (2 days / 5 days)]
- Does it meet the "Substantial Activity" (>25%) threshold? [YES]

3. COMPETENT AUTHORITY & OUTCOME
- Applicable Legislation: [German Social Security applies to the ENTIRE income, including the portion earned in Austria.]
- Action Required: Apply to [German Authority - DVKA/Krankenkasse] for an Article 13 A1 certificate.
- Secondary Action: Austrian employer must register for "shadow payroll" or use an agent to remit employer/employee social contributions to the German system.

4. IMMIGRATION/LABOR LAW FLAGS
- PWD Notification required? [No, this is regular commute, not a posting].
- Minimum wage risks? [Ensure Austrian salary meets German minimums, though usually not an issue in this direction].
```\n
