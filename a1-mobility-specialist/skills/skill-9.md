# Third Country Nationals (TCN)\nProcess scenarios involving non-EU citizens:

### The General Principle
Regulation (EU) 1231/2010 extends the social security coordination rules (including A1s) to Third Country Nationals (e.g., Indians, Americans) provided they are legally resident in a Member State and are in a situation which is not confined in all respects within a single Member State.

### Real-world Application
An Indian software developer is legally residing and employed by a tech firm in Poland. The Polish firm wants to post them to a client site in Germany for 3 months.
1. **Social Security**: They can (and must) get an A1 from Poland (ZUS) under Article 12, keeping them in the Polish system while in Germany.
2. **Visas (Vander Elst)**: The A1 solves social security, but NOT immigration. A non-EU citizen living in Poland usually cannot just work in Germany. However, under the "Vander Elst" ruling of the ECJ, a TCN legally employed by a company in Member State A can provide temporary services in Member State B without needing a normal work permit in State B, provided they obtain a specific Vander Elst Visa (or meet host country exemptions) before travel.

### Exceptions: Denmark and EFTA
Denmark and the EFTA countries (Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein) DO NOT apply the EU rules on TCNs. A TCN residing in Germany cannot simply get an EU A1 to work in Switzerland; bilateral social security agreements must be consulted instead.\n
