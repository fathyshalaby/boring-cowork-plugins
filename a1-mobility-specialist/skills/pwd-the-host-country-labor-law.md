# PWD & The Host Country Labor Law\nDistinguish Social Security (A1) from Labor Law (Posted Workers Directive):

### The Crucial Distinction
A common, dangerous misconception is that obtaining an A1 certificate is all you need to send a worker abroad. It's not. 
- **The A1** deals ONLY with Social Security (where do I pay health/pension contributions?).
- **The PWD (Posted Workers Directive)** deals with Labor Law (what minimum wage, holiday pay, and working hours must I guarantee the worker?).

### Host Country Minimum Standards
When you post a worker from a low-wage country (e.g., Romania) to a high-wage country (e.g., Denmark), you keep paying Romanian *social security* (via the A1), but you MUST pay the employee at least the statutory minimum wage or collective bargaining agreement minimum of the host country (Denmark) for the duration of the posting.

### The PWD Notification System
Before the worker boards the plane, the sending employer must:
1. Guarantee the "core nucleus" of host country employment rights (pay, maximum work periods, health/safety, gender equality).
2. Submit a formal **Posting Declaration/Notification** to the host country's labor authority (e.g., the SIPSI portal in France, the Meldeportal-Mindestlohn in Germany). This is SEPARATE from the A1 application.
3. Appoint a local representative/liaison person in the host country to interact with labor inspectors.
4. Keep payslips and timesheets translated into the host country language available on-site.\n
