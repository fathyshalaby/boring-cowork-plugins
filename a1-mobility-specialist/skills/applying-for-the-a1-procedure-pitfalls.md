# Applying for the A1: Procedure & Pitfalls\nManage the execution of A1 applications:

### The Competent Authority
You apply in the country whose social security system you want to *keep*. 
- For a posted worker from Italy to Spain, apply to INPS in Italy.
- For a multi-state worker residing in Germany working 30% in DE and 70% in PL, apply to the DVKA in Germany.

### Application Timing
Applications should be submitted *before* the start of the activity. However, in emergency dispatch situations (e.g., a critical machine breakdown requiring next-day travel), you must submit the application prior to travel, even if the actual certificate takes weeks to issue. The "proof of application" usually suffices during an inspection, provided it was submitted before the start date.

### Common Rejection Reasons
1. **Lack of Substantial Prior Insurance**: For Article 12 (Posting), the worker must have been subject to the sending country's social security legislation for at least 1 month prior to the posting.
2. **Replacing another posted worker**: If Worker A finishes their 24-month posting, you cannot immediately send Worker B to do the exact same job under an A1. (There is usually a 2-month cooling-off period required for the position itself).
3. **Shell Companies**: The sending employer doesn't do real business in the home country (e.g., an Irish IT firm where 100% of employees are permanently posted to client sites in Germany). Usually, authorities look for 25% of the company's turnover/staff to be domestic.
4. **Incorrect Article**: Applying for an Art 12 Posting when the pattern is clearly Art 13 Multi-state work.\n
