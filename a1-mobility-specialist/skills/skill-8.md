# Special Rules: Transport Sector\nManage the highly complex rules for international drivers:

### The Mobility Problem
Truck drivers cross borders daily, making standard posting rules unworkable.

### Social Security (A1) for Drivers
International drivers are almost always classified as Article 13 (Multi-state workers).
- If they reside in State A and the employer is in State A: A1 in State A.
- If they reside in State B, but spend <25% of time driving in State B, and work for an employer in State A: A1 in State A.

### The Mobility Package (Labor Law)
The EU introduced specific rules (Lex Specialis) for calculating minimum wage for drivers to prevent social dumping.
- **Bilateral Transport (Point A to Point B)**: E.g., loading in Poland, unloading in Germany. Host country (German) minimum wage rules do NOT apply. Home country rules apply.
- **Transit (Driving through without loading/unloading)**: Minimum wage rules of transit countries do NOT apply.
- **Cabotage (Domestic transport within a host country)**: E.g., a Polish truck loads in Berlin and unloads in Munich. German minimum wage rules strictly apply from day one.
- **Cross-trade (Point B to Point C but not home state)**: Minimum wage of the host countries generally applies.

### IMI Portal
Employers of international drivers must post their declarations not via national portals, but via a specialized central EU portal (the IMI system) specifically designed for the road transport sector.\n
