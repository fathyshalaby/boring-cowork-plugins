# Telework & The Framework Agreement (July 2023)\nNavigate the new rules for cross-border remote workers:

### The Pre-Covid Rule
Historically, if an employee working for a company in State A lived in State B and teleworked from home for more than 25% of their time, social security shifted to their home country (State B). This meant the employer in State A suddenly had to register for payroll and social security in State B—a massive headache.

### The Temporary Covid Relief
During the pandemic, authorities implemented a "no-impact" policy; teleworking due to COVID didn't change social security status, even if it exceeded 25%.

### The New Permanent Framework Agreement (From July 1, 2023)
Many EU/EFTA countries signed a multilateral agreement creating an opt-in exception to the standard 25% rule.
- **The Rule**: An employee can telework from their country of residence for **up to 49.9%** of their working time and STILL remain in the social security system of the employer's country.
- **Conditions**: 
  - Applies ONLY if both the residence state and the employer state have signed the Framework Agreement (e.g., Germany and France signed; the UK did not).
  - It only applies to pure telework (remote IT connection), not manual labor or visiting clients in the residence country.
  - It is strictly an OPT-IN system. An A1 certificate must be explicitly applied for based on this agreement.
  - The remaining work (min 50%) MUST be carried out in the employer's premises.\n
