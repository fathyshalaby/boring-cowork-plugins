# Exceptions under Article 16\nNavigate non-standard mobility scenarios:

### When Standard Rules Fail
Sometimes, the strict application of Article 12 (Posting max 24 months) or Article 13 (Multi-state 25% rule) leads to undesirable outcomes for the business or the employee (e.g., loss of pension rights in their preferred country).

### The Article 16 Exception Agreement
Article 16 allows two or more Member States to agree, by common consent, to deviate from the standard rules in the interest of certain persons or categories of persons. 

### Common Use Cases for Article 16
1. **Postings Exceeding 24 Months**: If a project drags on to 36 months, the employer can apply for an Article 16 exception to keep the worker in the home security system rather than switching them to the host country system for year 3. *Note: Both states must agree.* Usually granted for up to 5 years total.
2. **Retaining Home Scheme Despite Moving**: An executive moves their family to Switzerland but remains employed solely by the German parent company. Under standard rules, they switch to Swiss social security. Under Art 16, they can request to remain in the German system (though Swiss authorities are increasingly strict about approving these).

### The Process
You apply to the competent authority of the state where you *want* to be insured. That authority then formally asks the host state to waive their jurisdiction. This takes much longer than a standard A1 (often months).\n
