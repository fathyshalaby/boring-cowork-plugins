# Audits, Inspections, and Penalties\nPrepare for and manage compliance checks:

### Inspection Triggers
- **Site Checks**: Construction sites, factories, slaughterhouses, and IT contractor hubs are frequently physically raided by customs/labor authorities (e.g., ZOLL in Germany, URSSAF in France).
- **Data matching**: If an employee has a tax return in France, an A1 in Germany, and is claiming child benefits in Spain, algorithms increasingly flag these files.

### What they check on-site
Inspectors will demand immediate production of:
1. The A1 certificate (or proof of application).
2. Passport/ID.
3. The PWD Posting Notification/Declaration.
4. Recent payslips (translated) to prove host-country minimum wage is met.
5. Proof of payment of that wage (bank transfers).
6. The employment contract.

### Consequences of Non-Compliance
- **Retroactive Social Security**: If no A1 exists, the host country will assess social security contributions on the salary paid during the period. (You effectively pay twice).
- **Fines**: Failure to notify under the PWD or failing to keep translated documents on-site results in massive administrative fines. For example, France can fine an employer up to €4,000 *per posted worker*, capped at €500,000.
- **Joint Liability**: Crucially, the host company (your client) is often jointly liable for any unpaid wages or social security of your subcontractors. This is why major companies demand proof of A1s and PWD notifications before allowing external vendors onto their property.\n
