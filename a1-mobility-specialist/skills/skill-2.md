# Posted Workers vs. Multi-State Workers\nDistinguish between the two main categories of mobile workers under EU Regulations:

### Article 12: Posted Workers
- **Definition**: A person employed in Member State A, sent by their employer to perform work in Member State B on that employer's behalf.
- **Duration Limit**: The anticipated duration of the work cannot exceed **24 months**.
- **Key Criteria**: 
  - There must be a direct relationship between the sending employer and the employee throughout the posting.
  - The sending employer must normally carry out substantial activities in the home country (not just a shell company hiring people solely to post them).
  - The employee cannot be sent to replace another posted worker whose term has expired.
- **Result**: They remain insured in the home country (State A) for max 24 months.

### Article 13: Persons Working in Two or More Member States
- **Definition**: A person who normally pursues an activity as an employed person in two or more Member States.
- **Key Criteria**: They must perform a **"substantial part"** (defined as at least 25%) of their working time or remuneration in their Member State of *residence*.
- **Result**: 
  - If they meet the 25% threshold at home: They are insured in their country of residence.
  - If they do NOT meet the 25% threshold at home: They are insured in the country where their employer has its registered office/place of business.
- **Duration Limit**: A1s under Article 13 are usually issued for 1-2 years, but the status can theoretically continue indefinitely as long as the multi-state working pattern remains.\n
