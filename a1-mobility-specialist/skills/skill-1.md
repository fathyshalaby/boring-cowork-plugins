# A1 Certificate Fundamentals\nUnderstand the core purpose of the A1 Portable Document in the EU:

### What is an A1 Certificate?
The A1 certificate proves which country's social security system applies to an individual while working in another EU/EEA country or Switzerland. It is the definitive proof that an employer/employee is paying social contributions in their home country, exempting them from paying them simultaneously in the host country.

### The Problem it Solves
Without an A1, under EU rules (Regulation 883/2004), an employee is generally subject to the social security legislation of the country *where they physically work*. If an engineer from Germany goes to install a machine in France for one week, France could legally demand social security contributions on that week's salary. The A1 prevents this double taxation and immense administrative burden.

### The Golden Rule
You MUST have an A1 BEFORE the cross-border work begins. In practice, many host countries (like France, Austria, Switzerland) conduct strict site inspections (especially on construction sites or factories). If an external worker is found without an A1, severe fines are levied on both the sending employer and the host company.

### Who Needs It?
- **Posted workers**: Sent temporarily by their employer to another Member State.
- **Multi-state workers**: Regularly working in two or more Member States (e.g., international truck drivers, executives splitting time between offices).
- **Self-employed**: Temporarily providing services in another Member State.\n
