# The 25% Rule (Substantial Activity)\nCalculate substantial activity for multi-state workers (Art. 13):

### The Threshold
To remain in the social security system of their country of residence, a multi-state worker must perform a "substantial part" of their activity there. The benchmark is **25%**.

### How is 25% measured?
Authorities look at the anticipated working pattern for the upcoming 12 months. It can be measured by:
1. **Working Time**: e.g., working 2 days a week (40%) at home, 3 days abroad.
2. **Remuneration**: e.g., 30% of their income is generated through tasks performed in the home country.

### Documentation is Key
Social security authorities often demand proof of this expected pattern. You must provide:
- Employment contracts detailing the split location arrangement.
- Working time schedules or calendars.
- In some cases, historical data (flight logs, hotel receipts) from previous years to prove the pattern is genuine.

### The Trap
If an employee lives in Belgium, works for a Dutch company, and spends 80% of their time in the Netherlands and only 20% working from home (telework) in Belgium:
- They fail the 25% test in their residence state (Belgium).
- Result: They are subject to the social security system of the employer's state (the Netherlands).
- The employer does *not* need to register for Belgian payroll, but they STILL need an A1 issued by the Netherlands to prove this status to Belgium.\n
