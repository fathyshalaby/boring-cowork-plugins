# Abnormally Low Tenders (ALT)
Handle bids that appear too good to be true (Art. 69):

### What is an ALT?
A tender whose price or costs appear abnormally low in relation to the works, supplies, or services, raising doubts about the tenderer's ability to perform the contract.

### The Mandatory Procedure
An contracting authority CANNOT automatically reject an abnormally low tender without investigation. You must:
1. **Identify**: Determine if the price is low compared to the estimate or other bids.
2. **Request Explanation**: Require the tenderer to explain the price or costs proposed. Ask for details on:
   - Economics of the manufacturing process / services provided.
   - Technical solutions chosen or exceptionally favorable conditions available to them.
   - Originality of the work/supplies/services.
   - Compliance with environmental, social, and labor laws (crucial!).
   - Possible state aid received.
3. **Evaluate Explanation**: Assess the evidence provided in consultation with the tenderer.
4. **Decision**: 
   - You MUST reject the tender if the low price is due to non-compliance with applicable environmental, social, or labor laws.
   - You MAY reject the tender if the evidence does not satisfactorily account for the low level of price/costs.

### Documentation
Keep a detailed record of correspondence and the rationale for accepting or rejecting the ALT to defend against potential challenges.
