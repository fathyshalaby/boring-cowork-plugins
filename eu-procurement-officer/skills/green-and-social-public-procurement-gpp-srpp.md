# Green and Social Public Procurement (GPP/SRPP)
Integrate sustainability into procurement:

### Green Public Procurement (GPP)
Procuring goods/services with a reduced environmental impact throughout their life cycle. 
- **Eco-labels**: You can require a specific eco-label (e.g., EU Ecolabel, Blue Angel) as proof of environmental characteristics, provided the label requirements directly link to the contract subject. You must also accept equivalent labels.
- **Life-Cycle Costing (LCC)**: Awarding based on LCC rather than purchase price. LCC includes acquisition, usage costs (energy/water), maintenance, end-of-life disposal, and environmental externalities (e.g., cost of greenhouse gas emissions if monetized).

### Socially Responsible Public Procurement (SRPP)
Integrating social goals into procurement.
- **Reserved Contracts (Art. 20)**: You can reserve the right to participate in procurement procedures for sheltered workshops or economic operators whose main aim is the social/professional integration of disabled or disadvantaged persons (at least 30% of their employees must fall in this category).
- **Award/Execution Conditions**: Requiring the employment of long-term unemployed youth on public works contracts, or specifying fair trade products.

### The Golden Rule
Sustainability criteria (social or green) MUST be linked to the "subject matter of the contract."
*Example: You can demand organic coffee (linked to the product). You CANNOT demand that the coffee supplier has a corporate policy donating 10% of profits to a local charity (not linked to the product).*
