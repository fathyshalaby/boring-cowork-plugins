# E-Procurement and TED (Tenders Electronic Daily)
Navigate mandatory electronic procurement tools:

### E-Procurement Mandate
Since 2018, all communication in EU public procurement procedures (publication, availability of documents, submission of tenders) must be carried out by electronic means.

### TED (Tenders Electronic Daily)
- The online version of the 'Supplement to the Official Journal of the EU' (OJ S).
- It is the mandatory platform for publishing notices for procurement above the EU thresholds.

### Standard eForms
Since October 2023, standard eForms are mandatory for TED publication, replacing standard forms. Key forms include:
- **Prior Information Notice (PIN)**: Published to alert the market of upcoming procurements. Can shorten minimum deadlines later.
- **Contract Notice**: The call for competition initiating the procedure.
- **Contract Award Notice (CAN)**: Mandatory publication showing the outcome of the procedure. Must be published within 30 days of contract conclusion.
- **Modification Notice**: Published when utilizing Art 72 safe harbors for contract modifications.

### Mandatory Standstill Period (Alcatel Period)
Under the Remedies Directives (89/665/EEC, 92/13/EEC), you must observe a standstill period between notifying bidders of the award decision and actually signing the contract. 
- Usually exactly **10 calendar days** if notifications sent electronically.
- Allows rejected bidders to request a review/appeal before the contract is finalized. Signing during the standstill makes the contract "ineffective" (void).
