# Exclusion Grounds & Selection Criteria
Assess supplier suitability (The ESPD and Qualification phase):

### The European Single Procurement Document (ESPD)
- A standard, self-declaratory form used across the EU to prove that a supplier fulfills exclusion and selection criteria.
- Drastically reduces administrative burden. Only the winning bidder needs to submit the actual supporting certificates (e.g., tax records) via e-Certis.

### 1. Exclusion Grounds (Art. 57)
- **Mandatory Exclusions**: Corruption, fraud, terrorism, money laundering, child labor, human trafficking. Also: failure to pay taxes or social security contributions (proven by a binding judicial/administrative decision).
- **Discretionary Exclusions**: Bankruptcy/insolvency, grave professional misconduct, significant prior contract failures (early termination/damages), conflicts of interest, distorting competition.
- **Self-Cleaning**: An excluded supplier CAN participate if they prove they have taken sufficient measures to demonstrate reliability (e.g., paying fines, firing guilty personnel, structural changes).

### 2. Selection Criteria (Art. 58)
Criteria to prove the bidder is *capable* of performing the contract. Must be proportionate and strictly related to the contract subject.
- **Suitability to pursue professional activity**: e.g., enrolled in a relevant trade register.
- **Economic and financial standing**: e.g., minimum yearly turnover (cannot exceed 2x the contract value), professional risk indemnity insurance.
- **Technical and professional ability**: Proven by a list of principal deliveries over the past 3 years (supplies/services) or 5 years (works), educational/professional qualifications, or environmental management measures.
