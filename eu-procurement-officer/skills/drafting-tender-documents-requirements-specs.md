# Drafting Tender Documents (Requirements & Specs)
Draft clear, non-discriminatory tender documents:

### Structure of the Tender File
1. **Contract Notice**: Published in the OJEU via TED. Contains basic info, deadlines, and links to docs.
2. **Instructions to Tenderers**: Rules of the game, deadlines, format requirements.
3. **Terms of Reference / Technical Specifications**: What you are buying.
4. **Draft Contract**: Legal terms and conditions.

### Technical Specifications (Art. 42)
- Must afford equal access and not create unjustified obstacles to competition.
- Methods for defining specs:
  1. In terms of **performance or functional requirements** (Highly recommended, promotes innovation).
  2. By reference to technical specifications and standards (Must be in hierarchical order: European standards, international standards, national standards). Must ALWAYS add the words **"or equivalent"**.
  3. A combination of the two.

### What to Avoid
- You MUST NOT refer to a specific make, source, patent, trademark, or specific origin UNLESS justified by the subject matter of the contract. 
- If it is truly impossible to describe the requirement safely without specific references, the reference must ALWAYS be accompanied by the words "or equivalent."
- Example: Do not ask for "Intel Core i7 processors." Ask for "Processors achieving a minimum PassMark score of [X] or equivalent."
