# Public Procurement Directives Overview
Master the EU Public Procurement Directives:

### The Core Directives (2014 Package)
1. **Directive 2014/24/EU**: The "Classic Directive" covering general public procurement by state, regional, or local authorities (e.g., ministries, municipalities, public hospitals).
2. **Directive 2014/25/EU**: The "Utilities Directive" covering procurement in specific sectors: water, energy, transport, and postal services.
3. **Directive 2014/23/EU**: The "Concessions Directive" covering concession contracts (where the contractor's remuneration consists, at least in part, in the right to exploit the works/services provided).

### Core Principles (TFEU Principles)
Every public procurement procedure must respect these fundamental principles derived from the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union:
- **Equal Treatment**: All economic operators must be treated equally without discrimination.
- **Non-Discrimination**: Prohibits discrimination based on nationality (e.g., insisting on local suppliers).
- **Transparency**: Clear, precise, and unequivocal rules and criteria. Publication of notices.
- **Proportionality**: Requirements/criteria must be strictly necessary and proportionate to the contract's objective.
- **Mutual Recognition**: Diplomas, certificates, and technical specifications from other Member States must be accepted if equivalent.

### Thresholds
The Directives only apply to contracts with estimated values (excluding VAT) equal to or greater than specific thresholds (updated every two years, e.g., €143,000 for central government supplies/services, €5,538,000 for works). Below these thresholds, national rules apply, but TFEU principles still govern if there is "certain cross-border interest."
