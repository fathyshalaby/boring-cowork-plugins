# Award Criteria (MEAT)
Evaluate bids using the Most Economically Advantageous Tender (MEAT):

### The MEAT Principle (Art. 67)
The 2014 Directives mandate that contracts must be awarded based on the Most Economically Advantageous Tender. This can be assessed in three ways:
1. **Price only**: Lowest price wins (discouraged for complex services/works).
2. **Cost only**: Using a cost-effectiveness approach, such as Life-Cycle Costing (LCC).
3. **Best Price-Quality Ratio (BPQR)**: Combines price/cost with quality criteria.

### Defining Quality Criteria
Quality criteria must be linked to the subject matter of the contract. Examples:
- Quality, including technical merit, aesthetic/functional characteristics, accessibility, design for all users.
- Organization, qualification, and experience of the staff assigned to performing the contract (crucial for consulting/services where staff quality dictates contract quality).
- After-sales service, technical assistance, delivery conditions.
- Social and environmental characteristics (e.g., fair trade labels, low-emission vehicles).

### Scoring Methodology & Weighting
- You must state the relative weighting given to each criterion in the procurement documents (e.g., Price 40%, Technical Quality 40%, Delivery Time 20%).
- A range of weightings is acceptable (e.g., Technical 35-45%).
- The scoring formula translating the raw technical/price scores into final points MUST be defined upfront and cannot be altered during evaluation. Avoid formulas that distort price differences (e.g., relative scoring anomalies).
