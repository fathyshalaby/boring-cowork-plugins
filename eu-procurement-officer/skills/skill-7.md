# Framework Agreements & Dynamic Purchasing Systems
Utilize flexible purchasing instruments:

### Framework Agreements (Art. 33)
- **Definition**: An agreement with one or more economic operators to establish the terms (price, quantities) governing contracts to be awarded during a given period.
- **Duration**: Maximum 4 years (unless exceptionally justified and documented).
- **Types**:
  1. *Single-supplier framework*: Call-offs (orders) are placed directly based on the agreed terms.
  2. *Multi-supplier framework (min. 3)*: Call-offs can be placed either directly (if all terms are defined and objective conditions for selecting the supplier were laid out) OR via a "mini-competition" among the framework members.
- **Volume**: The estimated MAXIMUM quantity/value must be stated upfront. Once reached, the framework is exhausted.

### Dynamic Purchasing System (DPS) (Art. 34)
- **Definition**: A completely electronic process for making commonly used purchases (standard off-the-shelf items).
- **Key Difference from Frameworks**: A DPS is OPEN throughout its validity. New suppliers who meet the selection criteria can join at any time.
- **Process**:
  1. Publish contract notice establishing the DPS and selection criteria.
  2. Any operator meeting criteria is admitted (no limit on numbers).
  3. When a specific need arises, the authority invites ALL admitted participants in that category to submit a tender for that specific contract.
- **Best Use Case**: IT hardware, standard stationary, temporary staff agencies.
