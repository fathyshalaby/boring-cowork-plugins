# Procurement Procedures Selection
Choose the correct procurement procedure (Directive 2014/24/EU):

### Standard Procedures (Always available)
1. **Open Procedure**: The normal route. Any interested economic operator may submit a tender in response to a call for competition. Speed is the advantage, but evaluating many bids can be burdensome.
2. **Restricted Procedure**: A two-stage process. 
   - Stage 1: Any operator submits a request to participate (qualifications).
   - Stage 2: Only those invited by the contracting authority (minimum 5 candidates) may submit a tender. Useful for complex contracts to limit evaluation effort.

### Exceptional Procedures (Require specific justification)
3. **Competitive Procedure with Negotiation**: Two-stage, but allows negotiation on initial and subsequent tenders (except final tenders). Justified for works/services involving design/innovation, or when specifications cannot be established with precision (e.g., complex IT systems).
4. **Competitive Dialogue**: Used for highly complex projects (e.g., major infrastructure, complex PPPs) where the authority cannot define the technical/legal/financial means. The authority dialogues with selected candidates to develop solutions before inviting final tenders.
5. **Innovation Partnership**: For the development of an innovative product/service/works *and* its subsequent purchase, provided it matches agreed performance and costs.
6. **Negotiated Procedure without Prior Publication**: Extreme exception. Used for extreme urgency brought about by unforeseeable events NOT attributable to the authority, or when only one supplier exists for technical/exclusive rights reasons.
