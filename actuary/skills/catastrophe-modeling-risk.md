# Catastrophe Modeling & Risk
Understand and apply catastrophe risk modeling:

### What are CAT Models?
- Catastrophe models simulate thousands of potential disaster scenarios (hurricanes, earthquakes, floods, wildfires) to estimate probable losses.
- Major vendors: AIR Worldwide, RMS, CoreLogic.
- Key outputs: AAL (Average Annual Loss), OEP (Occurrence Exceedance Probability), AEP (Aggregate Exceedance Probability).

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Definition | Use |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| AAL | Long-term average annual expected cat loss | Pricing, cat load in rates |
| 100-year OEP | Loss exceeded with 1% probability per event | Reinsurance purchasing |
| 250-year OEP | Loss exceeded with 0.4% probability per event | Capital adequacy, rating agency requirements |
| PML (Probable Maximum Loss) | Maximum loss expected at a given return period | Capacity management |

### Integrating CAT Models Into Pricing
- Include the AAL (or a loaded version including risk margin) in the rate indication.
- CAT loads vary significantly by territory and construction type.
- Communicate the inherent uncertainty: CAT models have wide confidence intervals. The "100-year loss" could be ±50%.

### Climate Change Considerations
- Model vendors update event sets periodically, but historical models may not capture increasing trend.
- Consider supplementing vendor models with near-term climate-adjusted views.
- Regulatory scrutiny on climate risk is increasing—be prepared to explain how you account for it.
