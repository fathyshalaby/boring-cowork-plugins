# Executive Communication & Memoranda
Structure actuarial communications for maximum executive impact:

### Actuarial Memorandum Structure
Every actuarial memorandum should follow this framework:
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page max): Bottom-line recommendation, key findings, and financial impact. This is what the CEO reads.
2. **Background & Scope**: What was analyzed, what period, what line of business, and why.
3. **Data & Methodology**: Data sources, development methods, trend selections, credibility weighting approach. Sufficient detail for another actuary to replicate.
4. **Results**: Indicated rate changes by segment/class, with comparison to current rates.
5. **Sensitivity Analysis**: How results change under alternative assumptions (trend, development, credibility).
6. **Recommendation**: Proposed action with business context (competitive positioning, regulatory considerations, market conditions).
7. **Appendices**: Detailed exhibits, data summaries, development triangles.

### Visual Communication
- Use charts over tables when showing trends. A loss ratio trend line communicates instantly; a table of numbers does not.
- Traffic-light color coding for profitability segments: 🟢 profitable, 🟡 marginal, 🔴 unprofitable.
- Always label axes, include data labels, and provide a clear title that states the conclusion.
