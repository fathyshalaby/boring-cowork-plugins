# Actuarial Terminology & Communication
Use precise actuarial terminology and translate complex concepts for stakeholders:

### Core Terminology
| Term | Definition | When to Use |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Pure Premium** | Expected loss per exposure unit before expenses/profit loading | Ratemaking, pricing analysis |
| **Loss Development Factor (LDF)** | Multiplier applied to immature loss data to project ultimate losses | Reserving, IBNR estimation |
| **IBNR** (Incurred But Not Reported) | Reserve for claims that have occurred but haven't been reported yet | Quarterly/annual reserve reviews |
| **Credibility** | Statistical weight given to a book of business based on volume | Rate filings, experience mods |
| **Trend Factor** | Annualized rate of change in frequency or severity | Projecting losses to future periods |
| **Loss Ratio** | Losses / Earned Premium. The fundamental measure of pricing adequacy | Profitability analysis, rate indications |
| **Combined Ratio** | Loss Ratio + Expense Ratio. <100% = underwriting profit | Overall financial performance |
| **Exposure Base** | The unit of measurement for risk (car-years, payroll, revenue, etc.) | Ratemaking, classification |

### Communicating With Non-Actuaries
- When presenting to underwriters: Focus on **what the numbers mean for pricing decisions**. "The loss ratio on this segment is 72%, which means we're keeping $0.28 of every premium dollar after paying claims."
- When presenting to executives: Lead with the **bottom-line impact**. "This rate change will improve our combined ratio by 2.3 points, adding approximately $4.5M to underwriting profit."
- When presenting to regulators: Be **transparent and methodical**. Show your work, cite your data sources, and demonstrate that the rate is not excessive, inadequate, or unfairly discriminatory.
