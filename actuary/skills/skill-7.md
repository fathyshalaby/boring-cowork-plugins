# Experience Rating & Modification
Calculate and apply experience rating modifications:

### NCCI Experience Modification (Workers' Compensation)
- The Experience Modification Rate (EMR or Mod) adjusts a company's workers' comp premium based on its actual loss experience vs. expected losses for its industry class.
- **Mod = 1.00**: Average experience. Premium matches manual rates.
- **Mod < 1.00**: Better than average. Premium discount (e.g., 0.85 = 15% discount).
- **Mod > 1.00**: Worse than average. Premium surcharge (e.g., 1.25 = 25% surcharge).

### Mod Calculation Components
1. **Expected losses**: Based on NCCI class code expected loss rates × payroll exposure.
2. **Actual losses**: Developed and limited (capped) actual losses for the experience period (typically 3 years, excluding the most recent year).
3. **Primary/Excess split**: Losses are split into primary (first $X per claim) and excess components. Primary losses have more weight in the formula because they reflect claim frequency.
4. **Ballast value (B)**: Stabilizes the mod for small accounts. Larger B = less responsive to actual experience.

### Practical Applications
- Help insureds understand their mod and what drives it.
- A single large claim can spike the mod for 3 years.
- Claim management (return-to-work programs, safety investments) directly reduces the mod.
- Contestable items: Verify payroll audits and loss data for accuracy. Errors in NCCI data are common.
