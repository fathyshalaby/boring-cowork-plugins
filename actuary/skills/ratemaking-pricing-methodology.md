# Ratemaking & Pricing Methodology
Apply standard actuarial ratemaking techniques:

### Loss Ratio Method
1. Collect historical premiums and losses (typically 3-5 policy years).
2. Develop losses to ultimate using selected Loss Development Factors (LDFs).
3. Apply trend factors to bring historical data to the prospective level.
4. Calculate the indicated loss ratio = Ultimate Trended Losses / Earned Premium at Current Rate Level.
5. Compare to the target loss ratio = 1 - Target Expense Ratio - Target Profit Margin.
6. Overall Indicated Rate Change = (Indicated LR / Target LR) - 1.

### Pure Premium Method
1. Calculate ultimate trended losses per exposure unit (pure premium).
2. Divide by the permissible loss ratio (1 - expense provision - profit provision).
3. The result is the indicated rate per exposure unit.
4. Compare to current rates for the indicated change.

### Key Ratemaking Principles
- **Homogeneity**: Group similar risks together. Rates should reflect the expected cost of the group, not individual risk selection.
- **Credibility weighting**: Small books of business get blended with larger reference data. Z = min(1, sqrt(n/n_full)) where n_full is the full credibility standard (typically 1,082 claims for ±5% accuracy at 90% confidence).
- **Actuarial Standard of Practice (ASOP) No. 12**: Ratemaking must follow this standard. Document your methodology, data sources, and assumptions.
- **Rate level adjustments**: Restate historical premiums to current rate level using the parallelogram method or the more precise policy-level approach.
