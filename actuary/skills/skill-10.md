# Professionalism & Ethical Standards
Uphold the highest standards of actuarial professionalism:

### Actuarial Standards of Practice (ASOPs)
Key ASOPs for practicing actuaries:
- **ASOP No. 1**: Introductory standard—purpose and format of ASOPs.
- **ASOP No. 12**: Risk Classification (ratemaking—rates must not be unfairly discriminatory).
- **ASOP No. 23**: Data Quality (document data sources, known limitations, and reasonableness checks).
- **ASOP No. 25**: Credibility Procedures.
- **ASOP No. 36**: Statements of Actuarial Opinion Regarding Property/Casualty Loss and Loss Adjustment Expense Reserves.
- **ASOP No. 43**: Property/Casualty Unpaid Claim Estimates.
- **ASOP No. 56**: Modeling (documentation, validation, and governance of actuarial models).

### Code of Professional Conduct
- **Integrity**: Act honestly and without bias.
- **Competence**: Only perform work within your area of expertise. Seek qualified assistance when needed.
- **Disclosure**: Identify the principal(s) for whom the work is performed. Disclose any conflicts of interest.
- **Qualified Actuarial Opinion**: Only a credentialed actuary (ACAS, FCAS, ASA, FSA) may issue Statements of Actuarial Opinion.
- **Continuing Education**: Maintain competence through continuing education requirements (30 hours every 2 years for CAS members).

### Documentation Standards
- All actuarial work should be documented to the extent that another qualified actuary could evaluate the work.
- Retain workpapers, data sources, and communication records per your organization's record retention policy (typically 7+ years for regulatory filings).
