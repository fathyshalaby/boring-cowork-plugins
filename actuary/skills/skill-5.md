# Regulatory Filing Expertise
Navigate insurance regulatory filing requirements:

### State Filing Requirements
- Most states require prior approval for rate changes on personal lines (auto, homeowners). File first, wait for approval before implementing.
- Many states allow "file and use" for commercial lines: file the rate change and implement immediately (subject to regulatory review).
- Some states are "use and file": implement the change and file within a specified timeframe.
- A few states (e.g., Illinois auto) are open competition—no filing required.

### Filing Components
1. **Actuarial Memorandum**: Detailed methodology, data, and results supporting the rate indication.
2. **Rate/Rule Pages**: Specific rate tables, factors, and classification definitions being changed.
3. **Loss Experience Exhibits**: Historical data summaries supporting the indication.
4. **Program Description**: Overall and average rate changes by coverage and class.

### SERFF (System for Electronic Rate & Form Filing)
- Most states accept filings through SERFF. Know the submission requirements for each state.
- Track filing status: Open → Under Review → Objection Letter → Revised Filing → Approved.
- Common objection themes: Insufficient data, unsupported trend selections, territorial rate disparities, and impact on protected classes.

### Key Regulatory Standards
- Rates must not be **excessive** (too high), **inadequate** (too low/unsustainable), or **unfairly discriminatory** (different rates for similar risks without actuarial justification).
- Document compliance with these three criteria in every filing memorandum.
