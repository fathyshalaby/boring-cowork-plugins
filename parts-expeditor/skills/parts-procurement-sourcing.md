# Parts Procurement & Sourcing
Master the parts procurement lifecycle:

### Procurement Process
1. **Requisition**: Receive request from maintenance, engineering, or production with part details (P/N, description, quantity, need date, priority).
2. **Source identification**: Check approved vendor list (AVL). If no approved source, initiate vendor qualification.
3. **RFQ**: Request for Quotation to 2-3 suppliers. Compare price, lead time, MOQ, shipping terms.
4. **PO issuance**: Issue purchase order with complete specifications: P/N, revision, quantity, unit price, delivery date, quality requirements, shipping instructions.
5. **Order confirmation**: Obtain vendor acknowledgment with confirmed delivery date.
6. **Expediting**: Track order status and escalate delays before they impact production.
7. **Receiving**: Verify quantity, P/N, condition, and documentation (COC, test reports if required).
8. **Invoice reconciliation**: Match PO, receiving report, and invoice (3-way match).

### Sourcing Strategies
| Strategy | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| Single source | Proprietary parts, OEM-only items |
| Dual source | Critical parts — reduces supply risk |
| Competitive bid | Standard commodities, high-volume items |
| Distributor network | Low-volume, high-variety items (Grainger, McMaster, Motion) |
| Direct from manufacturer | High-volume or custom parts |
