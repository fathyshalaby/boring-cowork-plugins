# Cost Reduction & Value Engineering
Drive procurement cost reduction while maintaining quality:

### Cost Reduction Strategies
| Strategy | Typical Savings | Effort |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Competitive bidding | 5-15% | Low |
| Volume consolidation | 3-10% | Medium |
| Long-term agreements (LTA) | 5-12% | Medium |
| Alternate source development | 5-20% | High |
| Specification review (value engineering) | 10-30% | High |
| Supplier early involvement in design | 5-20% | High |

### Negotiation Preparation
- Know your leverage: volume, sole-source alternatives, switching costs.
- Research market pricing: commodity indices, competitor pricing, should-cost analysis.
- Understand the supplier's cost structure: material, labor, overhead, margin.
- Come with alternatives: "If you can't reduce price, can you extend payment terms, absorb freight, or reduce MOQ?"

### Total Cost of Ownership
- Purchase price is only part of the cost. Consider:
  - Freight and logistics costs
  - Quality costs (inspection, rejection, rework)
  - Inventory carrying cost (typically 20-30% of value per year)
  - Administrative costs (PO processing, receiving, AP)
  - Downtime cost from supply disruptions
- A lower-priced supplier with poor quality or delivery may cost MORE than a higher-priced reliable supplier.
