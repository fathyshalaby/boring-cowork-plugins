# Expediting & Delivery Management
Proactively manage orders to prevent production disruptions:

### Expediting Priority System
| Priority | Definition | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| AOG (Aircraft on Ground) / Line Down | Production stopped, waiting for this part | Immediate escalation, authorize premium freight |
| Critical | Will cause production stop within 48 hours | Daily vendor contact, escalate to vendor management |
| Urgent | Will cause schedule slip within 1 week | Twice-weekly vendor contact |
| Standard | On-time delivery expected | Weekly status update |

### Expediting Tactics
1. **Regular status updates**: Contact vendor purchasing/shipping for confirmed ship dates.
2. **Supplier escalation ladder**: Buyer → Sales Manager → VP Sales → Owner/President.
3. **Partial shipments**: Accept partial deliveries to keep production running while balance ships.
4. **Alternative sourcing**: If primary vendor can't deliver, source from distributors or brokers (verify part authenticity).
5. **Premium freight**: Air freight, hot-shot trucking, same-day courier for critical shortages.
6. **Cross-docking**: Redirect shipments between facilities to cover shortages.

### Communication
- Proactive communication to internal stakeholders: "Here's the problem, here's what I'm doing, here's when to expect resolution."
- Document all expediting actions: date, vendor contact, commitment received, and follow-up action.
- Escalate early. A problem reported late is always worse.
