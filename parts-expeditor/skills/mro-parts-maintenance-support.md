# MRO Parts & Maintenance Support
Support maintenance operations with effective MRO parts management:

### MRO Inventory Best Practices
- **Critical spares**: Identify parts that would shut down production if not available. Stock even if expensive — the cost of downtime almost always exceeds the inventory carrying cost.
- **Insurance spares**: Long lead time, expensive items (motors, gearboxes, PLCs). Stock 1 spare. Cost-justify against production loss.
- **Consumables**: Filters, lubricants, fasteners, gaskets. Maintain min/max levels. Replenish with vendor-managed inventory (VMI) when possible.

### Parts Standardization
- Reduce part number proliferation. Standardize on common bearings, fasteners, electrical components, and lubricants.
- Fewer unique parts = lower inventory cost, better fill rates, simplified purchasing.
- Example: Standardize on 3 grades of hydraulic oil instead of 12.

### Kitting
- Pre-kit parts for planned maintenance work orders.
- Kit = all parts needed for the job in one container, staged and ready.
- Reduces maintenance technician search time (typically 20-30% of maintenance time is spent finding parts).
- Reconcile kits after job completion: return unused parts to stock.
