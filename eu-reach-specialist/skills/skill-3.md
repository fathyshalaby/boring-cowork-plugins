# SVHCs and the Candidate List
Manage Obligations regarding Substances of Very High Concern (SVHCs):

### What is an SVHC?
Substances that pose unacceptable risks to human health or the environment. Meeting criteria for:
- CMR (Carcinogenic, Mutagenic, toxic to Reproduction) category 1A or 1B.
- PBT (Persistent, Bioaccumulative and Toxic) or vPvB (very Persistent and very Bioaccumulative).
- Equivalent level of concern (e.g., endocrine disruptors).

### The Candidate List
ECHA maintains the "Candidate List of SVHCs for Authorisation," updated twice a year (usually Jan/July). Once a substance is added, immediate legal obligations trigger for articles containing it:

### Article 33 Obligations (Duty to Communicate)
If an article contains an SVHC > **0.1% weight by weight (w/w)**:
- **B2B (To downstream users/distributors)**: You MUST automatically provide sufficient information to allow safe use, including as a minimum the name of the substance.
- **B2C (To consumers)**: You must provide the same information within 45 days, free of charge, *upon request*.

### Article 7.2 Obligations (Duty to Notify ECHA)
Importers/producers of articles must notify ECHA if an SVHC is in articles > 0.1% *and* the total volume of that SVHC in those articles exceeds 1 tonne per year per company.

### The O5A Principle (Once an Article, Always an Article)
Per the 2015 CJEU ruling: The 0.1% threshold applies to each individual component within a complex product, NOT to the entire assembled product. (E.g., In a car, the threshold applies to a specific O-ring, not the weight of the whole car).
