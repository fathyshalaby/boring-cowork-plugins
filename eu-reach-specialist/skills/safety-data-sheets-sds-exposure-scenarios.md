# Safety Data Sheets (SDS) & Exposure Scenarios
Ensure compliance regarding the communication of chemical hazards:

### EU SDS Requirements (REACH Annex II & CLP)
An SDS must be provided (free of charge) if a substance/mixture:
- Is classified as hazardous under CLP.
- Contains an SVHC >0.1%.
- Contains certain other hazardous substances above specific thresholds.

### Language Rules
The SDS MUST be provided in the official language(s) of the Member State where the substance/mixture is placed on the market. An English SDS in Germany is not legally compliant.

### Checking Supplier SDSs
Downstream users must check incoming SDSs for:
1. Are my company's specific uses identified and covered in Section 1.2?
2. Are the recommended Risk Management Measures (RMM) and PPE in Section 8 appropriate and actually implemented on my shop floor?
3. Are there Exposure Scenarios attached (the "Extended SDS" or eSDS)?

### Exposure Scenarios (eSDS)
If a substance is registered >10 tonnes/year and is hazardous, the registrant must create an eSDS with Exposure Scenarios (ES).
- An ES describes operational conditions and risk management measures (RMMs) necessary for safe use.
- Downstream Users MUST implement the measures prescribed in the ES, or take action (inform supplier, scale the scenario, or create their own downstream CSR) within 12 months of receiving the eSDS.
