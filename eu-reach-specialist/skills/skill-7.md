# CLP Regulation (Classification, Labelling, Packaging)
Manage hazard classification alongside REACH:

### What is CLP? (Regulation EC 1272/2008)
- The EU implementation of the UN Globally Harmonized System (GHS).
- Works hand-in-hand with REACH. REACH manages the *data*, CLP manages the *hazard communication*.

### Key CLP Elements
1. **Hazard Classes & Categories**: e.g., Flammable Liquid Category 2, Acute Toxicity Category 4.
2. **Pictograms**: The red diamond symbols (GHS01 - GHS09).
3. **Signal Words**: "Danger" or "Warning".
4. **Hazard Statements (H-phrases)**: Standardized text describing the nature of the hazard. (e.g., H302 - Harmful if swallowed).
5. **Precautionary Statements (P-phrases)**: Standardized text describing measures to minimize adverse effects. (e.g., P280 - Wear protective gloves).

### Harmonised Classification (Annex VI)
- For extreme hazards (CMR, respiratory sensitizers), the EU mandates a "Harmonised Classification."
- If a substance is listed in Annex VI of CLP, you MUST use that minimum classification. You cannot self-classify it as less hazardous.

### Poison Centre Notifications (PCN)
Under Annex VIII to CLP, companies placing hazardous mixtures on the market must notify appointed national bodies (Poison Centres) via the ECHA PCN portal. 
- Requires creation of a **UFI (Unique Formula Identifier)**: A 16-character alphanumeric code that must be printed on the product label. It allows medical personnel to immediately identify the exact formulation in an emergency.
