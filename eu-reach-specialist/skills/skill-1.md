# REACH Fundamentals & Roles
Understand the core principles and roles within the REACH Regulation (EC 1907/2006):

### What is REACH?
- **R**egistration, **E**valuation, **A**uthorisation and Restriction of **Ch**emicals.
- Principle: "No data, no market." The burden of proof is on companies to identify and manage the risks linked to the substances they manufacture and market in the EU/EEA.
- Administered by ECHA (European Chemicals Agency) in Helsinki.

### Your Role Determines Your Obligations
| Role | Definition | Primary Obligations |
|------|------------|---------------------|
| **Manufacturer** | Established in EU/EEA, produces a chemical substance. | Registration (>1 tonne/yr), prepare SDS, classify hazards. |
| **Importer** | Established in EU/EEA, brings substances (on their own, in mixtures, or in articles) from outside EU/EEA. | Registration (>1 tonne/yr of substance), notify Candidate List substances in articles. |
| **Downstream User** | Established in EU/EEA, uses a substance (on its own or in a mixture) in industrial/professional activities. | Apply risk management measures from SDS, ensure use is covered in supplier's registration. |
| **Distributor** | Stores and places on the market substances/mixtures for third parties. | Pass information (SDS) up and down the supply chain. |
| **Article Producer** | Incorporates substances/mixtures into physical shapes/designs (e.g., cars, clothes, toys). | Communicate presence of SVHCs >0.1% to customers (Art 33), SCIP database notification. |

### The Only Representative (OR)
Non-EU companies cannot register under REACH. They can appoint an EU-based "Only Representative" to take over the importer obligations, relieving EU customers of the burden of becoming REACH Importers.
