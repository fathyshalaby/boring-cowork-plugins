# Authorisation & Restriction (Annex XIV & XVII)
Navigate market bans and severe restrictions:

### Annex XIV: The Authorisation List
Substances pulled from the Candidate List are transferred to Annex XIV. 
- **Sunset Date**: After this date, you cannot place the substance on the market or use it without prior Authorisation from the EU Commission.
- **Applying for Authorisation**: Extremely expensive and difficult process. You must prove that the socio-economic benefits outweigh the risks to health/environment, AND that no suitable alternatives exist.
- **Strategy**: Phase out Annex XIV substances well before the sunset date. Redesign the product.

### Annex XVII: Restrictions
A Restriction limits or bans the manufacture, placing on the market (including imports), or use of a substance (on its own, in a mixture, or in an article).
- **Scope**: Can be a total ban, or restricted to specific uses/concentrations (e.g., Chromium VI is restricted in leather articles contacting skin to <3 mg/kg; Lead is restricted in jewelry to <0.05%).
- **Impact**: Applies to ANY product placed on the EU market, regardless of where it was manufactured. Customs checks often enforce Annex XVII restrictions.
- PFAS Restriction: Be prepared for the sweeping "Restricting PFAS" initiative, which aims to ban thousands of "forever chemicals" across nearly all industries over the next decade. Monitor heavily.
