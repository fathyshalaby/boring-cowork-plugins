# RoHS vs. REACH Integration
Manage the overlap between REACH and the RoHS Directive:

### RoHS vs REACH Distinctions
| Feature | RoHS (Directive 2011/65/EU) | REACH (Regulation 1907/2006) |
|---------|-----------------------------|------------------------------|
| Scope | Only Electrical & Electronic Equipment (EEE) | All chemicals, mixtures, and articles (everything) |
| Mechanism | BANS substances above specific limits | Concentrates on communication/tracking (SVHCs), bans via Annex XVII/XIV |
| Enforcement | Required for CE Marking | Not directly tied to CE marking, but legally required for EU market entry |
| Number of Substances | 10 restricted substances | 240+ SVHCs, 70+ restricted entries |
| Limits | Strictly defined (e.g., 0.1% or 0.01% in homogenous material) | 0.1% of the whole article (for SVHCs) |

### Overlaps and Conflicts
- **Lead (Pb)**: Restricted under RoHS (above 0.1% in homogenous material, though many exemptions exist like 6c for copper alloys). Also an SVHC under REACH (requires communication/SCIP if >0.1% w/w of the article). You must manage both.
- **Exemptions**: RoHS allows exemptions (e.g., Lead in high melting temperature solder). REACH SVHC rules *still apply* even if a RoHS exemption covers the use.
- Your compliance program should have a unified data collection strategy requesting RoHS and REACH SVHC status simultaneously from suppliers.
