# The SCIP Database
Ensure compliance with the Waste Framework Directive SCIP database:

### What is SCIP?
**S**ubstances of **C**oncern **I**n articles as such or in complex objects (**P**roducts). It is established under the Waste Framework Directive (WFD), not REACH directly, but completely relies on REACH SVHC data.

### Legal Requirement
Since Jan 5, 2021, any supplier of an article placed on the EU market containing a Candidate List SVHC in a concentration >0.1% w/w must submit information to ECHA's SCIP database.

### Who Must Submit?
- EU Producers and assemblers.
- EU Importers.
- EU Distributors of articles (and other actors placing them on the market).
- *Exception*: Retailers exclusively supplying to consumers directly do not need to submit to SCIP.

### What Information is Required?
Submitted via IUCLID into the SCIP portal:
1. **Article Identification**: Name, primary article identifier (EAN/GTIN/Part number), article category (TARIC/CN code).
2. **Safe Use Information**: Instructions on safe use during lifecycle and waste stage.
3. **SVHC Identification**: Name, EC/CAS number, concentration range (e.g., >0.1 to <1.0%), material category where the SVHC is found (e.g., steel, PVC, rubber).

### Complex Objects (The Tree Structure)
For complex products (e.g., a laptop), you must build a hierarchy showing exactly which component (e.g., the motherboard capacitor) contains the SVHC, linking article datasets together.
