# REACH Registration Process
Navigate the requirements for registering chemical substances:

### The Registration Rule
Any manufacturer or importer of a substance, either on its own or in one or more mixtures, in quantities of **1 tonne or more per year** must submit a registration to ECHA.

### What Requires Registration?
- **Substances**: Yes (e.g., ethanol, titanium dioxide).
- **Mixtures**: You register the individual *substances* within the mixture, not the mixture itself (e.g., in a paint, you register the solvent, resin, and pigment separately).
- **Polymers**: The polymer itself is currently exempt from registration, but the *monomers* and other substances used to make the polymer must be registered if manufactured/imported >1 tonne/year.
- **Articles**: Substances intended to be released from articles must be registered (e.g., ink in a pen, scent in an eraser).

### The Registration Dossier (IUCLID)
Prepared using IUCLID software and submitted via REACH-IT.
1. **Joint Submission**: "One substance, one registration." Co-registrants of the same substance must form a SIEF/Consortium and submit data jointly to minimize animal testing and cut costs. The Lead Registrant submits the joint dossier.
2. **Letter of Access (LoA)**: What you pay the Consortium to buy the right to refer to the joint data.
3. **Tonnage Bands**: Data requirements increase massively with volume: 1-10t, 10-100t, 100-1000t, and >1000t per year.
4. **Chemical Safety Report (CSR)**: Required for substances ≥10 tonnes/year. Details hazard assessment and maps out Safe Use conditions (Exposure Scenarios).
