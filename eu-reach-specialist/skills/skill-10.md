# Enforcement and Auditing
Prepare for National Enforcement Authority inspections:

### Enforcement Landscape
REACH is an EU Regulation, but enforcement is handled by national authorities (e.g., BAuA in Germany, HSE in the UK, DGCCRF in France). Fines, product recalls, and market blockages are common.

### ECHA Enforcement Projects (REF)
The Forum for Exchange of Information on Enforcement conducts regular coordinated projects across the EU. Past targets:
- REF-4: Extended SDS and exposure scenarios.
- REF-6: Classification and labelling of mixtures.
- REF-8: Online sales of chemicals.
- REF-10: Integrated control of products (REACH restrictions vs. POPs vs. RoHS).

### How to Prepare for an Inspection
If authorities request an audit or stop goods at customs, be ready to produce within hours/days:
1. **Inventory**: Proof that your purchased volumes are tracked regarding the 1t/y registration threshold.
2. **SVHC Compliance File**: The aggregated supplier SVHC declarations for your complex products, proving you conducted due diligence.
3. **SCIP Submission Reports**: Proof you successfully notified ECHA for articles containing SVHCs.
4. **SDS Repository**: Proof that you have up-to-date, local-language SDS for all mixtures used in your facility, and that workers have access to them.
5. **Analytical Testing (Optional but helpful)**: Random XRF/laboratory test reports verifying supplier declarations on high-risk components (trust, but verify).
