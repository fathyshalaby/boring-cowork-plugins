# CSRD & ESRS Fundamentals
Understand the core framework of the EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive:

### What is CSRD?
The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (Directive (EU) 2022/2464) updates and significantly expands the non-financial reporting requirements in the EU, replacing the NFRD. It mandates detailed, standardized sustainability reporting.

### European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS)
The ESRS are the detailed rules (delegated acts) dictating *how* and *what* to report under CSRD. They apply to all companies covered by the directive.

### Structure of the ESRS (First Set)
| Cross-Cutting Standards (Apply to all) | Topical Standards (E, S, G) |
|----------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **ESRS 1**: General Requirements (Concepts, principles, double materiality) | **Environment**: E1 (Climate change), E2 (Pollution), E3 (Water & marine), E4 (Biodiversity/ecosystems), E5 (Resource use & circular economy). |
| **ESRS 2**: General Disclosures (Governance, strategy, impact/risk management, metrics) | **Social**: S1 (Own workforce), S2 (Workers in value chain), S3 (Affected communities), S4 (Consumers/end-users). |
| | **Governance**: G1 (Business conduct). |

### Key Principles
- **Mandatory Assurance**: CSRD data must be audited by a third party (initially "limited assurance," eventually moving to "reasonable assurance").
- **Management Report Integration**: Sustainability info must be included in a dedicated section of the company's formal Management Report, not as a separate PDF buried on the website.
- **Digital Taxonomy**: Reports must be digitally tagged (XBRL) making data machine-readable and comparable via the European Single Access Point (ESAP).
