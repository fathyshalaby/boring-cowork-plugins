# Governance & Business Conduct (ESRS G1)
Report on corporate culture and business conduct:

### G1 Focus Areas
While ESRS 2 handles general governance (role of the board, risk management systems), ESRS G1 delves into specific business conduct, anti-corruption, and political engagement.

### Key Disclosures
1. **Corporate Culture & Policies (G1-1)**:
   - Policies on bribery, corruption, and whistleblower protection.
   - Details on training provided on these policies.
2. **Management of Supplier Relationships (G1-2)**:
   - Policies on fair payment practices, supplier codes of conduct, and how ESG criteria are integrated into supplier selection.
3. **Anti-corruption/Anti-bribery (G1-3, G1-4)**:
   - Prevention and detection mechanisms.
   - Number of confirmed incidents of corruption or bribery during the reporting period, and action taken (or a statement that no incidents occurred).
4. **Political Influence & Lobbying (G1-5)**:
   - Describe activities related to lobbying and political influence.
   - Total monetary value of financial and in-kind political contributions.
   - Main topics covered by lobbying activities.
5. **Payment Practices (G1-6)**:
   - Crucial for SMEs relying on large corporate buyers.
   - Average time to pay suppliers (in days).
   - Proportion of payments to suppliers not aligned with standard terms.

### Auditee Readiness
For G1, auditors will heavily scrutinize the existence and enforcement mechanisms of Whistleblower policies and the empirical data behind Supplier Payment Practices.
