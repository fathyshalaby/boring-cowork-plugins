# Own Workforce Reporting (ESRS S1)
Manage the complex social metrics for ESRS S1:

### Scope of 'Own Workforce'
Covers employees bound by an employment contract, but also heavily extends to "non-employee workers" whose work/workplace is controlled by the undertaking (e.g., temporary agency workers, independent contractors integrated into the core operations).

### Key Disclosure Areas
1. **Policies / Processes**: Policies combating discrimination, promoting inclusion, ensuring health and safety, and managing grievances.
2. **Actions & Targets**: Time-bound targets for improving workforce conditions.
3. **Metrics (Highly granular)**:
   - **S1-6**: Total employees by headcount, gender, and geographic region. Breakdown of full-time, part-time, temporary, and non-guaranteed hours employees.
   - **S1-7**: Number of non-employee workers.
   - **S1-9**: Diversity metrics (top management gender percentage, distribution of employees by age group).
   - **S1-12**: Persons with disabilities percentage.
   - **S1-14**: Health and Safety metrics (number of fatalities, rate of recordable work-related accidents). Required for both employees and non-employee workers.
   - **S1-16**: Compensation metrics, including the **gender pay gap** and the ratio between highest paid individual and median pay.

### Data Collection Challenges
Metrics like S1-12 (disabilities) and age distribution often conflict with local privacy/GDPR laws or cultural taboos in certain EU countries. The ESRS acknowledges this; where local law prevents data collection, companies can report this constraint instead of the metric.
