# Double Materiality Assessment (DMA)
Execute the foundational Double Materiality Assessment (ESRS 1):

### The Core Concept
Double Materiality is the absolute foundation of CSRD. You do not report on all 10 topical standards automatically. You assess whether a topic is material from two dimensions. If a topic is material in *either* dimension, it must be reported.

### 1. Impact Materiality (Inside-Out)
- **Definition**: The actual or potential, positive or negative impacts the company has on people or the environment over the short, medium, and long term.
- **Assessment**: Based on severity (scale, scope, irremediable character) for actual impacts, multiplied by likelihood for potential impacts.
- *Example*: The company's operations cause significant river pollution.

### 2. Financial Materiality (Outside-In)
- **Definition**: A sustainability matter is financially material if it triggers (or may trigger) material financial effects on the undertaking's development, performance, position, cash flows, access to finance or cost of capital.
- **Focus**: Risks and Opportunities (R&O).
- *Example*: A carbon tax significantly increases operational costs, or climate change causes water scarcity that halts factory production.

### Outcomes of the DMA
The DMA process determines which topical ESRS (E1-E5, S1-S4, G1) are relevant to the company. 
- **Exception**: ESRS 2 (General Disclosures) is ALWAYS mandatory.
- **Climate Change (E1)**: Highly presumed material. If concluded non-material, a detailed justification is mandatory.
- **Own Workforce (S1)**: Specific data points (e.g., related to 1-9 employees) are mandatory if the company has >250 employees.
