# EU Taxonomy Alignment
Integrate EU Taxonomy reporting with CSRD:

### What is the EU Taxonomy? (Regulation (EU) 2020/852)
A classification system defining what economic activities can be considered "environmentally sustainable." It prevents greenwashing and directs investments toward sustainable projects.

### CSRD & Taxonomy Connection
Companies subject to CSRD must also comply with Article 8 of the Taxonomy Regulation, disclosing how much of their business is Taxonomy-eligible and Taxonomy-aligned.

### The Three KPIs
You must report the proportion of your:
1. **Turnover (Revenue)**
2. **CapEx (Capital Expenditure)**
3. **OpEx (Operating Expenditure)**
...that are associated with Taxonomy-aligned economic activities.

### The Alignment Assessment (3-Step Test)
For an activity to be "aligned" (green), it must pass three strict tests:
1. **Substantial Contribution (SC)**: Make a substantial contribution to at least one of the 6 environmental objectives (e.g., Climate mitigation, circular economy). Meets specific technical screening criteria.
2. **Do No Significant Harm (DNSH)**: It must not significantly harm any of the other 5 objectives.
3. **Minimum Social Safeguards (MSS)**: The company must respect human rights, labor rights (OECD guidelines, UN Guiding Principles), and anti-corruption laws.

*Note: Many companies find that a large portion of their business is "Eligible" (covered by the taxonomy), but less than 5% is actually "Aligned" due to the strict DNSH criteria.*
