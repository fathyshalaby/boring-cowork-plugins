# Data Architecture and Reporting Controls
Establish investor-grade data collection systems:

### From ESG Storytelling to ESG Accounting
CSRD shifts sustainability from a marketing/communications exercise to a rigorous financial compliance exercise. The data must withstand external audit equivalent to financial statements.

### Designing the Data Architecture
1. **Data Definition**: Clearly define every metric perfectly aligned with ESRS definitions (e.g., defining exactly what counts as a 'recordable work-related injury').
2. **Data Ownership**: Assign specific C-level, VP, or Director owners to each metric. (e.g., HR owns S1, Facilities owns Scope 1 & 2 gas/electric, Procurement owns Scope 3 purchased goods).
3. **Source Systems**: Identify where the "golden source" data resides (ERP, HRIS, Travel platforms, Utility portals). Moving away from decentralized, uncontrolled Excel spreadsheets is critical.
4. **Data Aggregation**: Implement specialized ESG reporting software (e.g., Workiva, Persefoni, Enablon) to aggregate data securely and maintain an audit trail.

### Internal Controls over Sustainability Reporting (ICSR)
Mirroring financial SOX controls:
- **Process Documentation**: Flowcharts detailing how data is generated, extracted, transformed, and reviewed.
- **Maker/Checker Controls**: The person calculating Scope 2 emissions cannot be the final approver.
- **Variance Analysis**: Controls that flag month-over-month or year-over-year deviations (e.g., if energy use drops 40% but production is flat, investigate before reporting).
