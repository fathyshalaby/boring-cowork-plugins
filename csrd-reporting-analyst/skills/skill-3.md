# The Value Chain Perspective
Extend assessment and reporting across the entire value chain:

### The CSRD Expansion
Unlike NFRD, which largely focused on a company's own operations, CSRD mandates reporting on impacts, risks, and opportunities across the entire upstream and downstream value chain.

### Upstream vs. Downstream
- **Upstream Value Chain**: Activities of suppliers (Tier 1, Tier 2, etc.) relating to the raw materials, services, and products purchased to fuel the company's operations.
- **Downstream Value Chain**: Activities occurring after the product leaves the company (distribution, use phase by consumers/end-users, and end-of-life disposal/recycling).

### Reporting Requirements
- **Impacts & Risks**: Your Double Materiality Assessment MUST consider the value chain. Are there severe human rights impacts deep in your supply chain (ESRS S2)? Does your product consume massive energy during the use phase (ESRS E1)?
- **Metrics & Targets**: Not all metrics require value chain data. ESRS 1 specifies that value chain information is required when necessary to allow users to understand the material impacts/risks/opportunities.

### Challenges & Solutions (The Transitional Provision)
Gathering primary data from all suppliers is notoriously difficult.
- **Phase-in**: For the first 3 years of reporting, if a company cannot obtain necessary value chain information after making reasonable efforts, it can use estimated data (sector averages, proxies, literature).
- **Strategy**: Start mapping Tier 1 strategic suppliers immediately. Integrate ESG data requests into standard procurement questionnaires.
