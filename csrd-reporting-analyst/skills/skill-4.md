# Climate Change Reporting (ESRS E1)
Master the most critical topical standard: ESRS E1:

### Why E1 is Unique
Climate Change is the most comprehensive standard. If deemed material (almost guaranteed), the reporting burden is significant.

### Core Disclosures (E1-1 to E1-9)
1. **Transition Plan**: Document how the business model aligns with a 1.5°C Paris Agreement pathway. (If you don't have one, you must disclose that fact and whether you plan to create one).
2. **Policies, Actions, and Resources**: What is the company actually doing to mitigate/adapt to climate change?
3. **Targets**: Decarbonization goals for Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions (must be absolute targets, not intensity targets, ending in 2030 and 2050).

### Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Emissions (E1-6)
You must calculate and report GHG emissions in metric tons of CO2 equivalent (tCO2e) mapped to the GHG Protocol:
- **Scope 1**: Direct emissions (owned boilers, vehicle fleets).
- **Scope 2**: Indirect emissions from purchased energy (electricity, heating). Must calculate both *Location-based* (regional grid average) and *Market-based* (what you actually buy via renewable energy certificates/PPAs).
- **Scope 3**: All other indirect emissions across the value chain (business travel, purchased goods, use of sold products). Identify and report the significant Scope 3 categories.

### Financial Effects (E1-9)
Must disclose anticipated financial effects from physical risks (extreme weather) and transition risks (carbon pricing, market shifts), and potential climate-related financial opportunities.
