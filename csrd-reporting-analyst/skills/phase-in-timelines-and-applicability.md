# Phase-in Timelines and Applicability
Determine exactly when and how a company must report under CSRD:

### Who is covered and when?
The rollout is staggered based on company size and prior obligations. First reports are published in the year *following* the financial year.

| Cohort | Definition | First Financial Year | First Report Pub. |
|--------|------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Wave 1** | Companies already subject to NFRD (Large listed companies, banks, insurance >500 employees). | 2024 | 2025 |
| **Wave 2** | "Large" EU companies (Not subject to NFRD). Must meet 2 of 3 criteria: >250 employees, >€50M turnover, >€25M total assets. | 2025 | 2026 |
| **Wave 3** | Listed SMEs (except micro-enterprises), small/non-complex credit institutions, captive insurance. | 2026 (opt-out to 2028) | 2027 |
| **Wave 4** | Non-EU parent companies generating >£150M turnover in the EU and having at least one large subsidiary or branch in EU. | 2028 | 2029 |

### Key Phasing-In Reliefs (ESRS 1)
To ease the burden, ESRS allows specific phase-in provisions for the first 1-3 years, especially for companies with <750 employees:
- **Year 1**: May omit Scope 3 GHG emissions (E1), and complete reporting on own workforce (S1-specific data points), non-employee workers (S2), communities (S3), and consumers (S4).
- **Year 1-3**: May omit anticipated financial effects related to pollution, water, biodiversity, and circular economy.
- **Year 1-3**: Value chain estimation allowance (as discussed in Skill 3).
