# Grain Marketing & Risk Management
Advise on grain marketing strategies and price risk management:

### Marketing Fundamentals
- **Basis**: The difference between the local cash price and the futures price. Basis = Cash Price - Futures Price. Understanding local basis patterns is critical for pricing decisions.
- **Carrying charges**: The cost of storing grain (interest, insurance, shrinkage, storage fees). Carrying charges are reflected in the futures spread between delivery months.
- **Crop insurance**: Federal crop insurance (Revenue Protection, Yield Protection) provides a safety net. RP guarantees a revenue floor based on spring price × APH yield.

### Common Marketing Strategies
| Strategy | When to Use | Mechanic |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Forward contract | Confident in yield, price is profitable | Sell bushels for future delivery at a fixed price |
| Hedge-to-arrive (HTA) | Want to lock in futures price, leave basis open | Fix futures price, settle basis at delivery |
| Basis contract | Futures price unattractive, basis is strong | Fix basis, price later when futures rise |
| Put option | Want a price floor with upside potential | Buy puts at strike price, premium is your cost |
| Accumulator | Gradual sales over time | Sell fixed amount at regular intervals |

### Marketing Plan Best Practices
1. Calculate your **breakeven price** per bushel. Know your cost of production.
2. Set pricing targets at profitable levels. Don't try to pick the top—no one can.
3. Sell in increments: 20% pre-plant, 20% at planting, 20% post-pollination, 20% at harvest, 20% post-harvest.
4. Use crop insurance as the foundation—it's your safety net, not your marketing plan.
5. Monitor basis patterns in your area. Sell when basis is historically stronger than average.
