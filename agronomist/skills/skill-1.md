# Soil Science & Fertility Management
Apply soil science fundamentals to optimize crop nutrition:

### Soil Test Interpretation
| Nutrient | Low (Action Needed) | Optimal | High (Reduce/Monitor) |
|----------|--------------------|---------|-----------------------|
| pH | <5.5 (lime needed) | 6.0-7.0 (most crops) | >7.5 (sulfur/acidification) |
| Phosphorus (P) | <15 ppm (Mehlich-3) | 25-50 ppm | >75 ppm (environmental risk) |
| Potassium (K) | <120 ppm | 150-250 ppm | >300 ppm |
| Organic Matter | <2% | 3-5% | >5% (excellent, maintain) |
| CEC | <5 meq/100g (sandy) | 10-25 meq/100g | >30 meq/100g (heavy clay) |

### Fertilizer Recommendations
- **N-P-K**: Always express fertilizer analysis in this order. A 10-20-10 fertilizer is 10% N, 20% P₂O₅, 10% K₂O.
- **Nitrogen**: Most dynamic nutrient. Mobile in soil. Apply based on yield goal, soil type, and previous crop credits (legumes provide 40-80 lbs N/acre credit).
- **4R Nutrient Stewardship**: Right Source, Right Rate, Right Time, Right Place. This is the industry standard framework.
- **Variable Rate Application (VRA)**: Use soil test results mapped by GPS to apply fertilizer at variable rates across the field. Zones with higher CEC and yield potential get more; sandy low-yield zones get less.

### Soil Health Assessment
Beyond NPK, evaluate:
- **Soil structure**: Aggregate stability, compaction (use a penetrometer—>300 PSI = compacted).
- **Biological activity**: Earthworm counts (>10 per shovelful = good), microbial biomass (Solvita CO2 burst test).
- **Water infiltration**: Timed infiltration test. Healthy soil absorbs 1 inch of water in <30 minutes.
- **Organic matter trend**: Track over years. Building 0.1% per year through cover crops and reduced tillage is a realistic goal.
