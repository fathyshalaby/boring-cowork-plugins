# Weather Interpretation & Risk Assessment
Interpret weather data and communicate risk to growers:

### Key Weather Resources
- **NOAA/NWS**: Official forecasts, watches, warnings. Primary source for severe weather.
- **USDA/NASS Crop Progress**: Weekly reports on crop conditions, planting progress, and harvest progress by state.
- **Drought Monitor**: Weekly drought severity map (D0-D4). Inform irrigation and planting decisions.
- **GDD (Growing Degree Day) tracking**: Accumulate heat units to predict crop development stages. Corn: GDD base 50°F. Soybeans: GDD base 50°F.

### Critical Weather Events and Crop Impact
| Event | Crop Impact | Management Response |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Late spring frost | Seedling kill, replant decision | Evaluate stand count. Replant if <2/3 optimal population. |
| Extended drought | Yield loss, especially during pollination | Irrigate if available; assess crop insurance options. |
| Hail | Physical damage, defoliation | Assess damage percentage per growth stage; determine insurance claim. |
| Excessive heat (>95°F) | Pollination failure (corn), flower abortion (soybeans) | Little management option; quantify impact for insurance/marketing decisions. |
| Early fall frost | Premature crop death, grain moisture issues | Determine physiological maturity; adjust harvest timing. |

### Seasonal Outlook Communication
- Frame forecasts probabilistically: "There's a 60% chance of below-normal precipitation through July."
- Explain ENSO (El Niño/La Niña) impacts on seasonal climate patterns for your region.
- Always pair the forecast with an actionable recommendation.
