# Water Management & Irrigation
Optimize water management for crop production and sustainability:

### Irrigation Scheduling Methods
1. **Soil moisture monitoring**: Use tensiometers or capacitance sensors. Irrigate when soil moisture reaches the Management Allowable Depletion (MAD), typically 50% of available water capacity.
2. **ET-based scheduling**: Calculate crop water use using reference ET (ETo) × crop coefficient (Kc) × stress factor. Data from local weather stations or CIMIS/CoAgMet networks.
3. **Look and feel**: Experienced method using soil texture and feel to estimate moisture. Less precise but useful as a field check.

### Irrigation Efficiency
| System Type | Application Efficiency | Best For |
|------------|----------------------|----------|
| Center Pivot | 80-90% (LEPA: 95%+) | Large fields, row crops |
| Drip/Micro | 90-95% | High-value crops, orchards, vineyards |
| Furrow/Flood | 50-70% | Level fields, rice, some row crops |
| Solid Set Sprinkler | 70-80% | Orchards, pasture |

### Water Quality Considerations
- Test irrigation water annually: pH, EC (salinity), SAR (sodium adsorption ratio), chloride, boron, and bicarbonate.
- **EC > 1.5 dS/m**: Moderate salinity risk—select salt-tolerant crops and monitor soil EC.
- **SAR > 9**: Sodium hazard—risk of soil structure degradation. May need gypsum amendment.

### Conservation Practices
- **Deficit irrigation**: Intentionally under-irrigating during stress-tolerant growth stages to conserve water with minimal yield impact.
- **Surge irrigation**: Alternating on/off cycles in furrow systems to improve advance rate and uniformity.
- **Cover crops and residue management**: Reduce evaporation from soil surface by 15-30%.
