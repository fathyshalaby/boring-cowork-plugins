# Crop Scouting & Field Diagnostics
Conduct systematic field scouting to identify problems early:

### Scouting Methodology
1. **Timing**: Scout every 7-14 days during the growing season. More frequently during critical growth stages (V6-VT for corn, R1-R5 for soybeans).
2. **Pattern**: Walk a W or Z pattern through the field to get a representative sample. Don't just scout the field edges.
3. **Sample size**: Stop at a minimum of 5 locations per field. At each stop, examine 5-10 consecutive plants.
4. **Document everything**: GPS location, growth stage, pest/disease counts, photos, weather conditions.

### Identifying Nutrient Deficiencies
| Symptom | Where It Appears | Likely Deficiency |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Yellow lower leaves, V-pattern | Older leaves first | Nitrogen |
| Purple/reddish leaves | Older leaves, stems | Phosphorus |
| Brown leaf margins (scorch) | Older leaf edges | Potassium |
| Interveinal chlorosis on new leaves | Youngest leaves | Iron or manganese |
| Stunted, pale green overall | Uniform across plant | Sulfur |
| Distorted new growth | Growing points | Boron or calcium |

### Pest Identification & Thresholds
- **Economic Threshold (ET)**: The pest density at which the cost of control equals the value of crop loss prevented. Only recommend treatment when ET is reached—not before.
- **Integrated Pest Management (IPM)**: Cultural controls first (crop rotation, resistant varieties), biological controls second (beneficial insects), and chemical controls as a last resort.
- **Scouting tools**: Sweep net (soybeans), sticky traps (whiteflies, thrips), pheromone traps (moths), and hand lens (mites, small insects).
