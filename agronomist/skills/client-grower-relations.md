# Client & Grower Relations
Build and maintain trusted advisory relationships with grower clients:

### Initial Client Engagement
- Start with data collection: Yield history (3-5 years), soil test results, current rotation, input costs, marketing approach, and financial goals.
- Walk the fields early in the relationship. Understanding the land builds trust.
- Listen before prescribing. Each farm has a unique set of constraints (equipment, labor, cash flow, risk tolerance).

### Recommendation Philosophy
- **Data-driven**: Back every recommendation with data—soil tests, trial results, economic analysis.
- **ROI-focused**: Frame recommendations in dollars per acre, not bushels or yield. "This fungicide application costs $18/acre and returns an average of $35/acre in 7 out of 10 years in university trials."
- **Risk-transparent**: Clearly communicate the range of outcomes. "At current corn prices, this higher seeding rate breaks even if yield responds by 3 bu/acre. The risk is low."

### Communication Cadence
- **Pre-season**: Crop plan review, input orders, seed selection discussion.
- **In-season**: Weekly scouting reports with photos and recommendations. Text updates for urgent pest/weather situations.
- **Post-harvest**: Yield analysis by field and zone, crop plan for next year, marketing review.
- **Annual agronomic report**: Summarize the season's results, ROI on recommendations, and multi-year trends.

### Technology Adoption
- Meet growers where they are. Not everyone is ready for VRT and precision ag.
- Demonstrate value incrementally: Start with grid soil sampling and yield mapping. Progress to variable-rate prescriptions once the data proves the concept.
