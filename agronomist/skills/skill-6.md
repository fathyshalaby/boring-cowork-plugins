# Pest Management & Crop Protection
Apply Integrated Pest Management principles for effective crop protection:

### IPM Decision Framework
1. **Identify**: Correctly identify the pest. Misidentification leads to wrong treatment. Use extension resources, diagnostic labs, or digital ID tools.
2. **Monitor**: Quantify the pest population using appropriate sampling methods. Compare to economic thresholds.
3. **Threshold reached?**: If population exceeds the Economic Threshold (ET), treatment is justified. If below ET, continue monitoring.
4. **Select control method**: Choose the most effective, least disruptive option:
   - Cultural: Crop rotation, resistant varieties, planting date adjustment, sanitation.
   - Biological: Beneficial insects (lady beetles, parasitoid wasps), microbial pesticides (Bt).
   - Chemical: Last resort. Select product based on pest, timing, and resistance management.

### Herbicide Resistance Management
- **Rotate herbicide modes of action (MOA)**. Do NOT rely solely on glyphosate (Group 9).
- Use multiple effective MOAs per application (tank mix or pre-mix products).
- Monitor for escapes—herbicide-resistant weeds survive at the same rate as untreated weeds.
- Document which MOAs are used in each field each year for a resistance management history.
- Waterhemp, Palmer amaranth, and marestail are the most agronomically significant resistant weeds in the US.

### Fungicide Application Timing
| Crop | Critical Application Window | Target Diseases |
|------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Corn | VT-R1 (tassel-silk) | Gray leaf spot, Northern corn leaf blight |
| Soybeans | R3 (beginning pod) | White mold, frogeye leaf spot, brown spot |
| Wheat | Flag leaf emergence + flowering | Stripe rust, Septoria, Fusarium head blight |
