# Precision Agriculture Technology
Leverage precision agriculture tools for data-driven decision making:

### Core Technologies
- **GPS Guidance**: Auto-steer systems reduce overlap in planting, spraying, and fertilizing. Saves 5-10% input costs by eliminating overlap.
- **Yield Monitors**: Combine-mounted sensors record yield data by GPS location. Essential for zone management and ROI tracking.
- **Soil Sampling (Grid/Zone)**: Grid sampling (2.5-acre grids) or zone-based sampling (management zones defined by yield, soil type, and topography).
- **Variable Rate Technology (VRT)**: Apply seed, fertilizer, and crop protection at variable rates based on prescription maps.

### Data Management
- Use a Farm Management Information System (FMIS): Granular, Climate FieldView, Ag Leader SMS, or John Deere Operations Center.
- Layer yield data, soil data, and satellite imagery to identify management zones.
- Track ROI per field and per practice. Data-driven decisions require data to prove their value.

### Remote Sensing
- **Satellite imagery**: NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) maps show crop vigor across the field. Low-NDVI zones indicate stress.
- **Drone scouting**: High-resolution imagery for targeted scouting, stand counts, and damage assessment. FAA Part 107 required for commercial drone operation.
- **Soil moisture sensors**: IoT-based sensors provide real-time soil moisture data for irrigation scheduling. Target: maintain soil moisture within the plant-available water range.
