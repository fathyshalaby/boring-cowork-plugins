# Crop Rotation & Cropping Systems
Design effective crop rotation and cropping system plans:

### Rotation Principles
- **Diversify**: Alternate between grass crops (corn, wheat, sorghum) and broadleaf crops (soybeans, sunflowers, canola). This disrupts pest and disease cycles.
- **Include legumes**: Soybeans, alfalfa, peas, and clover fix atmospheric nitrogen, reducing fertilizer needs for the following crop (credit: 40-80 lbs N/acre after soybeans).
- **Include cover crops**: Planted after cash crop harvest to protect soil, build organic matter, suppress weeds, and improve water infiltration.
- **Avoid repeating disease-susceptible crops**: Continuous corn amplifies corn rootworm, gray leaf spot, and northern corn leaf blight. Minimum 2-year rotation from host species for major diseases.

### Common Rotation Examples
| Rotation | Benefits | Considerations |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Corn-Soybean | Most common in Midwest. N credit, pest break | Narrow rotation; SCN risk over time |
| Corn-Corn-Soybean | Higher corn acres for grain markets | Increased rootworm risk in year 2 corn |
| Corn-Soybean-Wheat/Cover | Adds disease break, cover crop window | Requires wheat market, harvest logistics |
| Corn-Soybean-Wheat-Alfalfa (3yr) | Best soil health, excellent N credit | Long rotation, requires hay market |

### Cover Crop Selection
| Species | Type | Primary Benefit | When to Plant |
|---------|------|----------------|--------------|
| Cereal Rye | Grass | Erosion control, weed suppression | After corn/soybean harvest |
| Crimson Clover | Legume | N fixation (80-150 lbs/acre) | Fall, mild climates |
| Radish (Tillage) | Brassica | Compaction relief, nutrient cycling | Early fall, winterkills |
| Annual Ryegrass | Grass | Erosion control, root structure | After early harvest |
