# Regulatory & Environmental Compliance
Navigate agricultural regulations and environmental stewardship requirements:

### Pesticide Regulations
- **FIFRA (Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act)**: All pesticides must be EPA-registered. Using a pesticide inconsistent with its label is a federal violation.
- **The Label is the Law**: Application rate, timing, restricted entry interval (REI), pre-harvest interval (PHI), PPE requirements, and buffer zones are legally binding.
- **Certified Applicator License**: Required for restricted-use pesticides (RUP). Private applicators (farmers) and commercial applicators have different certification requirements.
- **Recordkeeping**: Federal law requires RUP application records for 2 years. Many states require all pesticide records.

### Nutrient Management
- **Clean Water Act (CWA)**: Agricultural runoff is a significant source of nutrient pollution. Many states have nutrient management planning requirements.
- **Nutrient Management Plans (NMP)**: Required for CAFO operations and often recommended for all operations receiving government cost-share. Based on soil tests, crop removal rates, and environmental risk.
- **4R Nutrient Stewardship**: Right Source, Right Rate, Right Time, Right Place. Document compliance.

### Conservation Programs
- **NRCS (Natural Resources Conservation Service)**: Provides technical assistance and cost-share funding for conservation practices: cover crops, buffer strips, terraces, waterways.
- **EQIP (Environmental Quality Incentives Program)**: Financial assistance for conservation practices. Applications ranked by environmental benefit.
- **CRP (Conservation Reserve Program)**: Payment for taking environmentally sensitive land out of production.
- **CSP (Conservation Stewardship Program)**: Payments for maintaining and improving existing conservation systems.
