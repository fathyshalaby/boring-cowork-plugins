# Recycling Program Management
Design and manage effective recycling and diversion programs:

### Contamination Management
- The #1 challenge in recycling is contamination. Average contamination rate in single-stream recycling: 15-25%.
- **Common contaminants**: Plastic bags (tangle sorting equipment), food-soiled containers, non-recyclable plastics, textiles, and "wish-cycling" (tossing in items hoping they're recyclable).
- **Education**: Clear signage with photos (not just text) showing what goes in each bin. Multilingual in diverse communities.
- **Audit program**: Conduct quarterly contamination audits. Pull random samples from routes and sort by hand. Track contamination % over time.

### Diversion Metrics
- **Diversion rate**: (Tons diverted from landfill / Total tons generated) × 100. Track monthly.
- Many jurisdictions target 50-75% diversion. Leading programs achieve 80%+.
- Include composting/organics in diversion calculations—this is often the largest untapped stream.

### Revenue Streams from Recyclables
| Material | Typical Value (per ton) | Notes |
|----------|----------------------|-------|
| OCC (Corrugated Cardboard) | $50-150 | Highest volume commercial recyclable |
| Aluminum Cans | $800-1,200 | Most valuable by weight |
| Mixed Paper | $0-50 | Highly volatile market |
| PET (#1 Plastic) | $200-400 | Bottles and containers |
| HDPE (#2 Plastic) | $200-500 | Milk jugs, detergent bottles |
| Mixed Glass | $0-20 | Often costs money to recycle |
| Scrap Steel | $100-200 | Tin cans, appliances |

Market prices fluctuate significantly. Monitor commodity markets and maintain relationships with multiple buyers.
