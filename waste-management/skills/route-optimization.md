# Collection Route Optimization
Design and optimize waste collection routes for maximum efficiency:

### Route Design Principles
1. **Right-turn routing**: Design routes to maximize right turns (reduces time at intersections and is safer for drivers). In residential areas, this means collecting one side of the street at a time.
2. **Deadhead minimization**: Reduce empty miles between the last pickup and the disposal/transfer facility. Schedule routes so the last pickup is closest to the facility.
3. **Density clustering**: Group stops by geographic density. High-density areas (downtown, industrial parks) get separate routes from rural/suburban areas.
4. **Time windows**: Schedule commercial pickups during low-traffic hours. Residential collection should start after noise ordinance hours end (typically 7:00 AM).

### Fleet Capacity Planning
- **Rear-loader**: 25 cubic yard body, typically serves residential curbside. ~800-1,200 residential stops per day.
- **Front-loader**: 40 cubic yard body, serves commercial dumpsters (2-8 yard). ~150-250 commercial lifts per day.
- **Roll-off**: 10-40 yard containers for construction, cleanouts, and industrial. 6-10 pulls per truck per day.
- **ASL (Automated Side Loader)**: 32 cubic yard, one-operator residential cart service. ~1,000-1,500 carts per day.

### Key Metrics
- **Lifts per hour**: Target 25-35 for residential ASL, 15-20 for commercial front-load.
- **Utilization rate**: Truck should be 75-85% full before heading to disposal. Returning half-empty wastes fuel and labor.
- **Route completion rate**: 98%+ target. Below 95% indicates route design problems or staffing issues.
