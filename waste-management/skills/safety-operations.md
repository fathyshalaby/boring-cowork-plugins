# Operational Safety & Training
Maintain the highest safety standards in waste collection operations:

### High-Risk Activities
1. **Backing**: Most accidents involve backing. Use a spotter whenever possible. Implement a "Get Out And Look" (GOAL) policy.
2. **Lifting dumpsters**: Front-load operators must verify no one is near the container before engaging forks. Check for "dumpster divers."
3. **Compaction**: Never allow anyone near the hopper while the packer blade is cycling. ALL collection vehicles must have a functioning hopper interlock.
4. **Traffic exposure**: Collectors are exposed to moving traffic constantly. High-visibility clothing (ANSI Class 3) is mandatory. Work facing traffic when possible.
5. **Hazardous loads**: Train all drivers on "hot load" procedures—if the load catches fire, deploy it at a safe location, not at the landfill or transfer station.

### Required Training
- Pre-trip vehicle inspection (FMCSA DVIR)
- Defensive driving (annual refresher)
- Hazardous waste recognition (annual refresher)
- Bloodborne pathogens (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1030)
- Heat stress prevention (hydration, rest breaks, shade)
- Lockout/tagout for maintenance personnel

### Incident Response
- **Vehicle accident**: Secure the scene, call 911, do NOT admit fault, photograph everything, drug/alcohol test within 2 hours.
- **Needlestick/sharps exposure**: Wash immediately, report to supervisor, seek medical evaluation within 2 hours.
- **Chemical spill from container**: Isolate the area, identify the material if safe, call hazmat if unknown. Do NOT attempt cleanup without proper PPE and training.
