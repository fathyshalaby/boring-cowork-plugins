# Waste Classification & Streams
Classify all waste types accurately according to federal and local regulations:

### Primary Waste Streams
| Stream | Examples | Regulation | Disposal Method |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Municipal Solid Waste (MSW) | Office trash, household garbage | Local ordinance | Landfill, WTE facility |
| Recyclables | Paper, cardboard, plastics #1-7, glass, metals | Local recycling mandate | MRF (Material Recovery Facility) |
| Construction & Demolition (C&D) | Concrete, wood, drywall, roofing | State regs | C&D landfill or recycling |
| Organic/Compostable | Food waste, yard waste, soiled paper | Varies by state | Composting, anaerobic digestion |
| Hazardous (HazWaste) | Solvents, paints, batteries, fluorescent tubes | RCRA (40 CFR 260-270) | Licensed HazWaste facility |
| Universal Waste | Batteries, lamps, electronics, mercury devices | 40 CFR 273 | Simplified HazWaste rules |
| Medical/Biohazardous | Sharps, pathological waste, contaminated PPE | OSHA + state health dept | Autoclave or incineration |
| E-Waste | Computers, monitors, TVs, phones | State e-waste laws | Certified e-waste recycler |

### Key Classification Rules
- **"When in doubt, don't throw it out."** If unsure whether waste is hazardous, treat it as hazardous until tested.
- A waste is hazardous if it exhibits any RCRA characteristic: Ignitability (D001), Corrosivity (D002), Reactivity (D003), or Toxicity (D004-D043 via TCLP test).
- **Listed wastes** (F, K, P, U lists) are hazardous by definition regardless of characteristics.
- **Mixture rule**: Mixing a hazardous waste with a non-hazardous waste makes the entire mixture hazardous.
- Document waste characterization decisions with written profiles. This is your audit defense.
