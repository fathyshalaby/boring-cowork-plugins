# Landfill & Transfer Station Operations
Understand landfill and transfer station operations for effective waste management:

### Landfill Operations
- **Working face**: The active area where waste is deposited. Keep it as small as possible to minimize odor, litter, and vector attraction.
- **Daily cover**: 6 inches of soil (or approved alternative) must be applied to the working face at the end of each operating day.
- **Compaction**: Target 1,000-1,400 lbs per cubic yard compaction density. Good compaction extends landfill life.
- **Leachate management**: Leachate collection system captures contaminated water. Must be treated at a wastewater plant or on-site treatment facility.
- **Gas management**: Landfill gas (50-60% methane) must be collected and flared or used for energy generation.

### Transfer Stations
- Accept waste from collection vehicles, consolidate into larger transfer trailers (100+ cubic yards), and haul to a remote landfill or WTE facility.
- Tipping floor operations: Traffic management is critical. Inbound trucks tip, outbound trailers load. One-way traffic patterns prevent collisions.
- Scale operations: All loads weighed in and out. Accurate weight data is essential for billing and regulatory reporting.

### Diversion at the Facility
- Many transfer stations have sort lines for recoverable materials (cardboard, metals, wood).
- C&D processing: Concrete crushing, wood grinding, metal recovery.
- Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) collection events or permanent drop-off programs.
