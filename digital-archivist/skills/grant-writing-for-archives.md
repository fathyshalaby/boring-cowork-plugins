# Grant Writing for Archives
Secure funding for archival projects through effective grant applications:

### Major Funding Sources
| Funder | Programs | Typical Award |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| NHPRC | Archival processing, digitization | $50K-$250K |
| NEH | Preservation, access, education | $50K-$350K |
| IMLS | Museums and libraries | $50K-$250K |
| Mellon Foundation | Digital humanities, preservation | $100K-$1M+ |
| State humanities councils | Public programs | $5K-$25K |
| Local foundations | Community-focused projects | $1K-$50K |

### Strong Applications Include
- Clear statement of need supported by data.
- Specific, measurable outcomes ("Digitize 5,000 photographs" not "Improve access").
- Realistic timeline with milestones.
- Detailed budget with justification.
- Letters of support from stakeholders and partner institutions.
- Sustainability plan: How will the project outcomes be maintained after the grant period?

### Common Weaknesses
- Vague objectives without measurable outcomes.
- Budget doesn't match the proposed activities.
- No plan for long-term sustainability.
- Insufficient description of the collection's significance.
