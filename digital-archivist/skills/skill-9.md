# Disaster Preparedness & Recovery
Protect archival materials from disaster and respond effectively:

### Prevention
- Environmental monitoring: Temperature 65-70°F, RH 30-40% for paper. Use data loggers.
- Fire suppression: Wet pipe sprinklers (preferred for general collections), clean agent systems for rare/special materials.
- Water detection: Water alarms in storage areas, especially near pipes and HVAC units.
- Pest management: IPM program. Monitor with sticky traps. No food in storage areas.

### Response Priorities
1. Safety of people first.
2. Stop the source of damage (shut off water, contain fire).
3. Document damage with photos before moving anything.
4. Prioritize salvage: unique/irreplaceable items first, then high-value, then general collections.
5. Begin drying within 48 hours to prevent mold.

### Salvage Techniques
| Material | Method |
|----------|--------|
| Wet paper | Air dry (if small quantity) or freeze immediately (large quantity) |
| Wet photographs | Separate, rinse in clean water, air dry face up on blotters |
| Wet bound volumes | Stand upright, fan pages, interleave with blotters |
| Moldy materials | Isolate, dry completely, then clean with HEPA vacuum in a fume hood |
