# Academic Policies & Catalog Management
Maintain academic policies and manage the institutional catalog:

### Catalog as Contract
- The academic catalog is a contract between the institution and the student.
- Students generally have the right to graduate under the catalog in effect when they matriculated (or a later one they elect).
- Changes to degree requirements apply prospectively. Grandfathering provisions for current students.

### Key Academic Policies
| Policy | Typical Standard |
|--------|------------------|
| Grading scale | A=4.0, B=3.0, C=2.0, D=1.0, F=0.0 |
| Good standing | ≥2.0 cumulative GPA |
| Academic probation | <2.0 cumulative GPA |
| Academic suspension | <2.0 for two consecutive semesters |
| Grade forgiveness/repeat | Replace grade on retake (varies by policy) |
| Credit hour definition | USDE: 1 credit = 1 hour instruction + 2 hours outside work, 15 weeks |

### Catalog Production
- Annual review and update cycle.
- Department/program review of degree requirements.
- Governance approval for policy changes (faculty senate, curriculum committee).
- Publish digitally (with search) and maintain archive of all versions.
- Ensure ADA accessibility of digital catalog.
