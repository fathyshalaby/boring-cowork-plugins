# Transfer Credit Evaluation
Evaluate and articulate transfer credit accurately:

### Evaluation Process
1. Receive official transcript from sending institution.
2. Verify accreditation of sending institution (regional accreditation standard for most institutions).
3. Course-by-course evaluation: Compare course description, credit hours, and level to catalog offerings.
4. Assign equivalency or elective credit.
5. Notify student of evaluation results.
6. Student may appeal with syllabus, course materials, and catalog description.

### Articulation Agreements
- **Institutional agreements**: Bilateral agreements with specific schools (2+2 programs with community colleges).
- **State transfer policies**: Many states have common course numbering or guaranteed transfer frameworks.
- **Standardized exams**: AP, IB, CLEP, DSST — publish credit policies with minimum score requirements.

### Transfer GPA
- Most institutions do NOT transfer grades into the institutional GPA. Credit transfers; grades do not.
- Exception: Consortium agreements or intra-system transfers may include GPA.
- Transfer credit typically counts toward degree requirements but not toward institutional GPA or honors calculations.
