# FERPA Compliance & Student Privacy
Protect student education records under FERPA:

### FERPA Basics (20 U.S.C. §1232g)
- Applies to all institutions receiving federal financial aid.
- **Education records**: Records directly related to a student maintained by the institution.
- Students gain FERPA rights at age 18 or when they attend a postsecondary institution.
- Written consent required before disclosing personally identifiable information from education records.

### Permitted Disclosures (Without Consent)
| Exception | Recipient | Condition |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| School official | Faculty, staff, contractors | Legitimate educational interest |
| Transfer | Another school | Student seeks to enroll or is enrolled |
| Financial aid | Aid administrators | Connected to financial aid |
| Accreditation | Accrediting organizations | Institutional accreditation |
| Judicial order | Court | Subpoena or court order |
| Health/safety | Appropriate parties | Emergency threatening health or safety |
| Directory information | Anyone | Student has not opted out |

### Directory Information
- Institutions define what constitutes directory information (name, email, major, enrollment dates, degrees, etc.).
- Students must be given the opportunity to opt out of directory information disclosure.
- NEVER include SSN, grades, or GPA as directory information.

### Violations
- Complaint to the Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO) at the US Department of Education.
- Potential loss of federal funding (extreme cases).
- Train ALL staff who access student records on FERPA requirements.
