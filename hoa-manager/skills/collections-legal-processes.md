# Collections & Legal Processes
Manage delinquent assessments and legal processes:

### Collection Timeline
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 1 | Assessment due |
| 15-30 | Grace period (per governing documents) |
| 31 | Late fee applied, late notice sent |
| 60 | Second notice, demand letter |
| 90 | Sent to collections attorney or collection agency |
| 90-180 | Lien filed against the property |
| 180+ | Foreclosure proceedings (if warranted and allowed by state law) |

### Lien Rights
- Most states give HOAs the right to lien delinquent properties.
- The lien attaches to the property, meaning the debt must be paid at sale or refinance.
- Some states give HOAs a "super lien" priority over the first mortgage (for a limited amount, typically 6 months of assessments).
- Always consult the association's attorney before filing liens or initiating foreclosure.

### Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)
- If collection is handled by a third party (management company or attorney), FDCPA applies.
- Required: Validation notice within 5 days of initial communication.
- Prohibited: Harassment, false statements, unfair practices.
- Best practice: Use a collections attorney experienced in HOA law.
