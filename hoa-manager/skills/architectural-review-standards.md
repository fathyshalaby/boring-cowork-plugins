# Architectural Review & Standards
Manage architectural review processes consistently and fairly:

### Architectural Review Committee (ARC)
- Reviews modification requests for compliance with architectural guidelines.
- Common submissions: paint colors, fencing, roofing, landscaping, additions, solar panels, satellite dishes.
- Decision must be consistent, based on written standards, and documented.

### Review Process
1. Owner submits written application with plans, materials, colors, timeline.
2. ARC reviews within the timeframe specified in governing documents (typically 30-45 days).
3. Decision: Approved, Approved with Conditions, or Denied (with specific reasons citing the applicable standard).
4. If no response within timeframe, some CC&Rs provide automatic approval. Know your documents.
5. Owner must complete work per approved plans. Final inspection confirms compliance.

### FHA & Fair Housing Considerations
- Cannot deny satellite dishes (FCC OTARD rule covers dishes ≤1 meter in exclusive use areas).
- Cannot deny reasonable disability accommodations (wheelchair ramps, grab bars).
- Solar panel restrictions increasingly limited by state law.
- Flagpoles for US flag often protected by federal Freedom to Display the American Flag Act.
- All enforcement must be uniform. Selective enforcement = legal exposure.
