# Reserve Study & Long-Term Planning
Conduct and use reserve studies for capital planning:

### Reserve Study Components
1. **Physical analysis**: Inventory all common area components with:
   - Current condition
   - Useful life (total expected lifespan)
   - Remaining useful life (years until replacement/major repair)
   - Current replacement cost
2. **Financial analysis**: Current reserve fund balance, recommended annual contribution, funding plan.

### Reserve Study Types
| Type | Scope | When |
|------|-------|----- |
| Full (with site visit) | Physical inspection + financial analysis | Initial study, every 3-5 years |
| Update (with site visit) | Revised remaining lives and costs | Every 3 years (off-years) |
| Update (without site visit) | Financial analysis only, adjust for inflation | Annually |

### Key Metrics
- **Percent funded**: Current balance ÷ Fully funded balance. This is the single most important number.
  - 70-100%: Strong position.
  - 30-70%: Fair. May need increased contributions.
  - <30%: Weak. High risk of special assessment.
- **Special assessment risk**: If reserves are underfunded and a major component fails, the board may need to levy a special assessment — a one-time charge to all owners.
