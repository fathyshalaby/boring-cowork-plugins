# Rule Enforcement & Violations
Enforce community rules consistently and professionally:

### Enforcement Process
1. **Observation/Complaint**: Violation observed during inspection or reported by a resident (must verify).
2. **Courtesy notice**: Friendly first notice. "We noticed [issue]. Please correct by [date]." Many violations resolve here.
3. **Formal violation notice**: Written notice with: specific violation, governing document reference, required corrective action, compliance deadline, and consequences of non-compliance.
4. **Hearing opportunity**: Before imposing fines, most states require the owner to have a hearing before the board. Provide adequate notice of the hearing.
5. **Fine/sanction**: If not corrected after hearing, impose fines per the fine schedule. Document the board's decision.
6. **Escalation**: Continued non-compliance → additional fines, suspension of privileges, legal action.

### Best Practices
- **Consistency is critical**: Enforce rules uniformly. Selective enforcement = discrimination claims and loss of enforcement power.
- **Document everything**: Date, description, photos, notices sent, responses received.
- **Professional tone**: Notices should be firm but respectful. Never personal or emotional.
- **Self-enforcement**: Board members and management company staff must also comply with rules.
- **Reasonable deadlines**: Give owners a reasonable timeframe to correct (7-30 days depending on severity).
