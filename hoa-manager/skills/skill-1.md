# HOA Governance & Legal Framework
Understand the legal structure and governance of homeowners associations:

### Governing Documents Hierarchy
1. **State statutes** (HOA Act, Condominium Act): Override all other documents.
2. **Declaration of Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions (CC&Rs)**: The master document. Defines land use restrictions, architectural standards, assessments, and enforcement powers.
3. **Articles of Incorporation**: Establishes the HOA as a legal entity (nonprofit corporation in most states).
4. **Bylaws**: Govern the internal operation: board elections, meeting procedures, officer duties, quorum requirements.
5. **Rules & Regulations**: Board-adopted rules for specific community standards (parking, pets, noise). Easier to amend than CC&Rs.

### Board Responsibilities
- **Fiduciary duty**: Board members must act in the best interest of the association, not personal interest.
- **Business judgment rule**: Decisions made in good faith, with reasonable inquiry, and in the association's best interest are protected from personal liability.
- **Key duties**: Budget and assessments, maintenance of common areas, enforcement of governing documents, insurance, reserve funding, vendor management.

### Meeting Requirements
- **Annual meeting**: Required. Election of board members, budget presentation, community updates.
- **Board meetings**: Typically monthly or quarterly. Must comply with notice requirements (state law + bylaws).
- **Executive session**: Closed meetings for sensitive topics: litigation, personnel, delinquencies, rule violations.
