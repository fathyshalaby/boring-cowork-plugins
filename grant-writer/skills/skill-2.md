# Proposal Writing Fundamentals
Write compelling grant proposals that win funding:

### Proposal Structure (Federal)
1. **Cover page/SF-424**: Administrative information, certifications.
2. **Abstract/Summary**: 1 page max. Problem, approach, significance, expected outcomes. Written last, reviewed first.
3. **Specific Aims / Project Narrative**: The heart of the proposal. What you'll do and why it matters.
4. **Background/Significance**: Literature review demonstrating the gap your project fills.
5. **Approach/Methods**: Detailed plan with timeline, milestones, and evaluation methodology.
6. **Evaluation Plan**: How you'll measure success. Required for program grants.
7. **Key Personnel**: Qualifications of the team. Biosketches (NIH format) or CVs.
8. **Budget & Budget Justification**: Line-item budget with detailed narrative justification.
9. **Facilities & Equipment**: Institutional resources available.
10. **Letters of Support**: From partners, collaborators, community stakeholders.

### Writing Principles
- **Lead with significance**: Why does this problem matter? Quantify the need.
- **Be specific, not vague**: "Train 200 community health workers" not "expand workforce capacity."
- **SMART objectives**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.
- **Reviewer-friendly formatting**: Use headers, numbered aims, bold key terms, and white space. Reviewers read 10-20 proposals — make yours easy to follow.
