# Compliance & Regulatory Requirements
Navigate grant compliance requirements:

### Federal Compliance Requirements
- **2 CFR 200 (Uniform Guidance)**: The bible of federal grant management. Covers allowable costs, audit requirements, and administrative standards.
- **IRB (Institutional Review Board)**: Required for human subjects research. Must be approved before research begins (can submit proposal before approval, but must have approval before funds are spent).
- **IACUC**: Required for animal research.
- **Conflict of Interest (COI)**: PIs must disclose significant financial interests. Institutional COI policy must be in place.
- **Data Management Plan**: Required by NSF and increasingly by other agencies. Describe how data will be collected, stored, shared, and preserved.

### Reporting Requirements
- **Progress reports**: Annual (typically via Research.gov for NSF, RPPR for NIH).
- **Financial reports**: Quarterly or annual Federal Financial Report (SF-425).
- **Final report**: Due 90-120 days after award end date.
- **Publications**: Acknowledge the funding agency and grant number in all publications. NIH requires deposit in PubMed Central.

### Common Compliance Pitfalls
- Spending before the award start date.
- Using funds for purposes not in the approved budget without prior approval.
- Not maintaining adequate time-and-effort documentation.
- Missing reporting deadlines (can result in funding suspension).
