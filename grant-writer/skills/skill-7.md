# NSF Proposal Writing
Master NSF-specific proposal requirements:

### NSF Proposal Components
1. **Project Summary** (1 page): Overview, Intellectual Merit, Broader Impacts. Must address both merit criteria explicitly.
2. **Project Description** (15 pages max): Background, proposed work, timeline, evaluation.
3. **References Cited**: No page limit.
4. **Biographical Sketches**: NSF format (SciENcv), 3 pages per PI.
5. **Budget & Budget Justification**: Standard NSF budget form.
6. **Facilities, Equipment & Other Resources**.
7. **Data Management Plan** (2 pages): Required for all NSF proposals.
8. **Supplementary Documents**: Letters of collaboration (brief), postdoc mentoring plan.

### NSF Merit Review Criteria
- **Intellectual Merit**: Potential to advance knowledge. Is the research question important? Is the approach sound?
- **Broader Impacts**: Benefit to society, broadening participation, education, infrastructure.
- Both criteria carry equal weight. Neglecting Broader Impacts is a common reason for decline.

### Common NSF Mistakes
- Letters of support that read as endorsements (NSF wants concise Letters of Collaboration).
- Not addressing both review criteria in every section.
- Exceeding page limits (desk rejection).
- Proposing work already completed.
