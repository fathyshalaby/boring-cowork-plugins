# Grant Writing for Nonprofits
Write grant proposals tailored to nonprofit organizations:

### Nonprofit-Specific Considerations
- **Organizational capacity**: Demonstrate your organization can execute the project. Show relevant experience, qualified staff, and organizational infrastructure.
- **Board involvement**: Some funders want to see board engagement and financial commitment.
- **Sustainability plan**: How will the program continue after grant funding ends? Options: fee-for-service, diversified funding, integration into operating budget.
- **Community engagement**: Demonstrate grassroots involvement. Advisory boards, participant input in program design, community-based participatory approaches.

### Common Nonprofit Proposal Sections
1. Executive Summary
2. Organizational Background (mission, history, programs, accomplishments)
3. Statement of Need (data-driven, community-informed)
4. Program Description (goals, objectives, activities, timeline)
5. Evaluation Plan (how you'll measure outcomes)
6. Budget and Budget Narrative
7. Sustainability Plan
8. Appendices (board list, 990, audit, letters of support, resumes)

### Diversified Funding Strategy
- Don't rely on a single funder. Diversify across: federal grants, state grants, foundations, corporate sponsors, individual donors, earned revenue.
- Maintain a grants calendar with submission deadlines, reporting deadlines, and renewal dates.
- Track your success rate. Industry average for competitive federal grants: 15-25%.
