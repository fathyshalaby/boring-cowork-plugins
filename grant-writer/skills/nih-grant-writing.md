# NIH Grant Writing
Master NIH-specific grant writing conventions:

### NIH Grant Mechanism Selection
| Mechanism | Purpose | Duration | Budget |
|-----------|---------|----------|--------|
| R01 | Major research project | 3-5 years | $500K/yr (typically) |
| R21 | Exploratory/developmental | 2 years | $275K total |
| R03 | Small research grant | 2 years | $50K/yr |
| K-awards | Career development | 3-5 years | Varies |
| T32 | Training grants | 5 years | Institutional |
| U-awards | Cooperative agreements | Varies | Varies |

### Specific Aims Page (Most Important Page)
- Paragraph 1: What is the big problem? Why does it matter?
- Paragraph 2: What is the specific gap in knowledge?
- Paragraph 3: What is your long-term goal and the objective of THIS application? State your central hypothesis.
- Specific Aims (bulleted): 2-3 aims that are related but not dependent on each other.
- Final paragraph: Expected outcomes and impact.

### Study Sections & Review
- NIH uses study sections (review panels) with subject-matter experts.
- Score criteria: Significance, Investigators, Innovation, Approach, Environment.
- Impact score: 1-9 scale (1=exceptional, 9=poor). Only top ~50% are discussed.
- Payline: Score threshold for funding. Varies by institute (typically 10th-25th percentile).
