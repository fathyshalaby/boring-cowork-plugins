# Budget Development & Justification
Create accurate, defensible grant budgets:

### Budget Categories
| Category | Components | Common Rules |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Personnel | Salaries, % effort, fringe benefits | NIH salary cap ($221,900 for FY2024), effort reporting |
| Equipment | Items >$5,000 per unit | Not subject to indirect costs (F&A) at most institutions |
| Travel | Domestic/international conference, fieldwork | Federal per diem rates (GSA), economy airfare |
| Supplies | Lab supplies, materials <$5,000 | Reasonable and necessary |
| Contractual | Subawards, consultants | Consultant daily rate cap ($750/day federal standard) |
| Other Direct | Participant costs, publication, tuition | Participant support costs excluded from F&A |
| F&A (Indirect) | Negotiated rate × MTDC | Institutional negotiated rate with cognizant agency |

### Budget Justification Writing
- Justify every line item: WHAT, WHY, HOW MUCH, and HOW CALCULATED.
- Example: "Senior Research Associate (Dr. Smith) — 25% effort × $85,000 salary = $21,250. Dr. Smith will lead data collection and analysis activities described in Aim 2."
- Fringe benefits: Use your institution's approved rate. Typically 25-35% of salary.
- Subawards: >$25K = first $25K subject to F&A, remainder excluded (federal rule).

### Common Budget Mistakes
- Math errors (always double-check totals).
- Requesting items not justified by the project narrative.
- Forgetting inflation escalation for multi-year budgets (3% per year standard).
- Not accounting for cost-share requirements.
