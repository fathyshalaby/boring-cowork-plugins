# Traction Elevator Systems
Understand traction elevator mechanical and electrical systems:

### Mechanical Components
- **Machine**: Motor + gearbox (geared) or direct-drive motor (gearless). Drives the sheave.
- **Sheave**: Grooved pulley that the hoist ropes wrap around. Rope grip depends on groove profile and rope condition.
- **Hoist ropes**: Steel wire ropes (typically 6-8 ropes). Replace when any rope shows >10% diameter reduction, broken wires exceed limits, or rope stretch causes uneven tension.
- **Governor**: Speed-sensing device. Triggers the safety device if the car exceeds 115% of rated speed (down direction).
- **Safety device**: Mechanically grips the guide rails to stop the car. Types: instantaneous (slow-speed), progressive (high-speed).
- **Counterweight**: Balances the car + 40-50% of rated load. Reduces motor size and energy consumption.
- **Guide rails**: T-section steel rails. Car and counterweight each ride on a pair of rails.
- **Buffers**: Energy-absorbing devices at the bottom of the hoistway. Oil buffers (high-speed) or spring buffers (low-speed).

### Electrical Systems
- **Controller**: The brains—relay logic (older) or microprocessor/VFD (modern). Controls motor speed, door operation, floor leveling, and dispatching.
- **VFD (Variable Frequency Drive)**: Controls motor speed for smooth acceleration/deceleration. Standard on modern installations.
- **Door operator**: Motor-driven mechanism that opens/closes car and hoistway doors. Types: center-opening (CO), side-opening (SO).
- **Safety circuits**: Series circuit of all safety switches. Opening any switch stops the elevator.
