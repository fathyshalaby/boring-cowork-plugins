# Safety Devices & Testing
Understand and test elevator safety devices:

### Critical Safety Devices
1. **Governor**: Overspeed detection device. Trips at 115% of rated speed. Setting is sealed and cannot be adjusted in the field.
2. **Car safety device**: Mechanically grips the guide rails when triggered by the governor. Types: instantaneous, progressive (for speeds >150 fpm).
3. **Buffers**: Bottom-of-hoistway energy absorbers. Spring (low-speed) or oil (high-speed).
4. **Door interlocks**: Prevent hoistway doors from opening unless the car is present and stopped. Each floor has an interlock.
5. **Final limits**: Backup switches that cut power if the car travels beyond the normal limits of travel.
6. **Slack rope switch (hydraulic)**: Detects if ropes go slack, indicating a mechanical failure.
7. **Low oil switch (hydraulic)**: Shuts down the unit if hydraulic oil level drops below safe minimum.
8. **Unintended car movement (UCM) protection**: Required on newer installations. Detects and stops unintended movement of the car with doors open.

### Category 5 Testing
- Full-load safety test every 5 years (1-year for some jurisdictions on select equipment).
- Car loaded to 125% of rated capacity. Governor is tripped mechanically or electrically.
- Car must stop within the code-specified distance.
- Measure stopping distance and inspect safety jaw/rail engagement marks.
- Document everything: date, tester, results, next test due date.
