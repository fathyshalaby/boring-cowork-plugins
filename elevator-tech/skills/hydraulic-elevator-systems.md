# Hydraulic Elevator Systems
Understand hydraulic elevator operation and maintenance:

### System Types
| Type | Description | Max Travel |
|------|------------|------------|
| Conventional (holed) | Jack extends into ground bore hole | ~60 feet |
| Holeless | Jack mounted in pit, no bore hole | ~30 feet |
| Roped hydraulic | Jack drives ropes through sheave | ~60 feet |
| MRL hydraulic | Compact power unit, no machine room | ~30 feet |

### Key Components
- **Power unit**: Electric motor + hydraulic pump + reservoir + control valves.
- **Jack (cylinder & piston)**: Single-acting cylinder pushes the car up. Gravity (car weight) brings it down, controlled by the down valve.
- **Hydraulic fluid**: Typically a petroleum-based oil. Check level monthly. Oil temperature affects ride quality — too cold = sluggish, too hot = rough.
- **Control valve**: Regulates flow for smooth starts/stops. Maladjusted valves cause jerky rides.

### Common Issues
- **Oil leaks**: At packing gland, fittings, or underground (if holed type). Underground leaks are environmental concerns.
- **Drift/creep**: Car slowly sinks when parked at a floor. Caused by leaking control valve or check valve. Allowable creep: ≤1 inch per 10 minutes (code threshold varies).
- **Overheating**: Oil temp >150°F degrades oil and seals. Check cooling system and duty cycle.
- **Re-leveling**: Car doesn't level accurately with floor. Adjust leveling valves or check leveling switches.
