# Fire Service & Emergency Operations
Understand elevator fire service and emergency operations:

### Phase I – Fire Recall (Automatic)
- Activated by building fire alarm system (smoke detector in lobby, machine room, or hoistway).
- All elevators recall to the designated floor (typically lobby). If smoke is detected at the designated floor, cars recall to an alternate floor.
- Doors open and stay open. Car will not respond to hall calls.
- Code requirement: ASME A17.1 Section 2.27.

### Phase II – Firefighter Control (Manual)
- Activated by firefighters using a key switch in the recalled car.
- Firefighter has direct control: doors open/close with constant pressure buttons only. Car responds only to car calls.
- Doors will not automatically close. If the firefighter releases the call button, the car stops at the next available floor.
- **Emergency in-car stop**: Additional key switch position allows the firefighter to remove the car from Phase II.

### Monthly Testing Protocol
1. Activate Phase I by initiating the fire alarm or using the lobby key switch.
2. Verify all cars recall to the designated floor.
3. Verify doors open and remain open.
4. Activate Phase II in each car. Verify firefighter controls work correctly.
5. Test alternate floor recall.
6. Document test results in the fire service test log.
