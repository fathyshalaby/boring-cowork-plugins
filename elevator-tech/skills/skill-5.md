# Troubleshooting & Diagnostics
Systematic approach to elevator troubleshooting:

### Diagnostic Methodology
1. **Gather information**: What's the symptom? When did it start? Is it intermittent or constant? Any recent work done?
2. **Check the obvious**: Is the unit on independent service? Are doors fully closed? Is the key switch in the correct position?
3. **Check the controller**: Modern controllers display fault codes. Read and research the code.
4. **Check safety circuits**: Use a multimeter to trace the safety circuit. A single open contact stops the elevator.
5. **Isolate the system**: Is it mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, or door-related?

### Common Faults
| Symptom | Likely Causes |
|---------|---------------|
| Doors won't close | Door track obstruction, worn rollers, close limit switch, detector malfunction |
| Car won't level properly | Leveling sensor/vane, leveling valve (hydraulic), encoder (traction) |
| Rough ride | Worn guide shoes/rollers, rail alignment, VFD tuning, rope tension uneven |
| Won't run in any direction | Safety circuit open, main contactor, motor overload tripped |
| Shuts down intermittently | Loose wiring, intermittent safety switch, overheating drive |
| Excessive noise | Worn bearings, misaligned sheave, door hardware, guide shoes |
