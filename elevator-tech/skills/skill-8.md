# Modernization Planning
Assess and plan elevator modernization projects:

### When to Modernize
- Equipment age >20-25 years (parts obsolescence becomes critical).
- Frequent breakdowns (>4 callbacks/month/unit).
- Long wait times and slow travel speed frustrating tenants.
- Non-compliance with current code requirements.
- Energy consumption is high (old motor/drive technology).
- ADA accessibility deficiencies.

### Modernization Scope Options
| Scope | Components | Typical Cost (per unit) |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| Controls only | Controller, VFD, wiring, signals | $100K-$200K |
| Controls + doors | Above + door operators, car interior | $150K-$300K |
| Full modernization | Above + machine, ropes, car frame, rails | $250K-$500K+ |
| Complete replacement | New everything including hoistway work | $500K-$1M+ |

### Planning Considerations
- **Phasing**: In a multi-car bank, modernize one car at a time to maintain service.
- **Downtime**: Typical 8-16 weeks per car for controls mod, 16-24 weeks for full mod.
- **Temporary provisions**: Portable signals, key access controls during construction.
- **Code compliance**: Modernization triggers code compliance requirements for the scope of work (ASME A17.3).
- **Energy savings**: Modern VVVF drives can reduce energy consumption by 30-50% vs. old MG sets.
