# Door Systems & Adjustments
Maintain and adjust elevator door systems — the #1 source of service calls:

### Door Components
- **Door operator**: Motor that drives the car doors. Types: single-speed, two-speed, VVVF (modern, smooth and quiet).
- **Car doors**: Panels that travel with the elevator car.
- **Hoistway doors**: Panels at each floor. Mechanically coupled to car doors by a clutch/retiring cam mechanism.
- **Door tracks**: Upper and lower tracks guide the door panels. Must be clean and properly aligned.
- **Interlock (hoistway door)**: Electrical and mechanical lock that prevents the hoistway door from opening unless the car is at that floor. **Critical safety device.**
- **Door restrictor**: Prevents hoistway door from being opened more than 4 inches when the car is not at that floor (ASME A17.1 requirement on newer installations).

### Common Door Adjustments
- **Close force**: Must not exceed 30 lbf (A17.1 requirement). Use a force gauge to measure.
- **Kinetic energy**: Must not exceed 7 ft-lbs (A17.1). Calculated from door weight and speed.
- **Reopening device**: Infrared curtain or mechanical edge must detect obstructions and reopen doors.
- **Nudging**: After a timeout (typically 20-30 seconds), doors close at reduced speed with an audible warning. Adjustable timing.
- **Door dwell time**: Minimum time doors stay open. ADA requires sufficient time for passengers with disabilities.
