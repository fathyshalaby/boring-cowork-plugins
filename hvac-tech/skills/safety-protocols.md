# HVAC Safety Protocols
Follow all applicable safety protocols rigorously when advising on HVAC work:

### Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)
- **Always** verify power isolation before advising on any service procedure. LOTO applies to electrical disconnects, gas shutoff valves, and pneumatic systems.
- Reference OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 for the standard LOTO procedure: Notify → Shutdown → Isolate → Apply Lock/Tag → Verify Zero Energy → Perform Work.
- Remind technicians that testing a circuit with a multimeter AFTER LOTO is mandatory before touching any conductor.

### EPA Section 608 Compliance
- Technicians must hold a valid EPA Section 608 certification (Type I, II, III, or Universal) to purchase or handle refrigerants.
- **Venting refrigerant is illegal.** All refrigerants must be recovered with EPA-certified recovery equipment.
- Leak detection is required before adding refrigerant. Simply "topping off" without finding the leak violates EPA regulations.
- Track all refrigerant usage with a log: date, system, pounds recovered, pounds charged, refrigerant type.

### Electrical Safety
- **Arc flash risk**: Any panel with >240V requires arc flash PPE (rated face shield, FR clothing) per NFPA 70E.
- Always use a non-contact voltage tester (NCVT) as a first check, then confirm with a multimeter.
- Capacitors in condenser units and air handlers store lethal charge even after power is disconnected. Always discharge with a resistor tool.

### Fall Protection
- Roof work requires fall protection per OSHA when the roof edge is 6 feet or higher above a lower level.
- Use a personal fall arrest system (PFAS) with a rated harness and anchor point.

### Gas Safety
- When working on gas furnaces, check for gas leaks with an electronic combustible gas detector or soap bubbles—never a flame.
- Verify CO (carbon monoxide) levels in the flue with a combustion analyzer. Normal: <100 ppm in the flue. Action level: >200 ppm.
