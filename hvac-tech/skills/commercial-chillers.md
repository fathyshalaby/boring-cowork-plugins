# Commercial Chiller Systems
Provide advanced guidance on commercial chiller diagnostics and maintenance:

### Chiller Types
| Type | Capacity Range | Coolant | Common Application |
|------|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| Air-Cooled Scroll | 7–200 tons | R-410A | Small commercial, process cooling |
| Air-Cooled Screw | 60–500 tons | R-134a, R-513A | Mid-size commercial |
| Water-Cooled Centrifugal | 200–2,000+ tons | R-134a, R-1233zd | Large commercial, hospitals, campuses |
| Absorption | 100–1,500 tons | Lithium bromide/water | CHP applications, waste-heat driven |

### Key Operating Parameters
- **Leaving chilled water temperature (LCHWT)**: Typically 42–45°F. Measure with a calibrated thermometer at the chiller's outlet.
- **Condenser water temperature**: Entering (ECWT) typically 85°F at design. Monitor approach temperature (ECWT − condensing temp).
- **Approach temperature**: Evaporator approach = LCHWT − evaporating temp. Should be 1–3°F. Rising approach indicates fouled tubes.
- **kW/ton**: The efficiency metric. Modern centrifugal chillers achieve 0.50–0.60 kW/ton at full load. If trending above 0.70, investigate.

### Preventive Maintenance Essentials
- **Tube cleaning**: Annual condenser tube brushing (or chemical cleaning). Fouled tubes increase head pressure and energy consumption 10–30%.
- **Oil analysis**: Annual spectrometric oil analysis detects moisture, acid, and metal wear particles before failure.
- **Vibration analysis**: Baseline and annual vibration readings on bearings, shafts, and motor. Rising vibration = developing mechanical failure.
- **Refrigerant analysis**: Annual refrigerant sample checks for moisture, acid, and non-condensables.
- **Eddy current testing**: Every 3–5 years, test heat exchanger tubes for wall thinning and pitting.
