# Heat Pump Defrost Cycles
Understand and troubleshoot heat pump defrost systems:

### How Defrost Works
When a heat pump operates in heating mode, the outdoor coil acts as the evaporator and can accumulate frost (especially at 30–45°F with high humidity). Defrost removes this frost.

### Defrost Methods
1. **Demand Defrost** (modern systems): Sensors monitor coil temperature and/or air pressure differential across the outdoor coil. Defrost initiates ONLY when frost is detected. More efficient.
2. **Time-Temperature Defrost** (older systems): Defrost initiates every 30, 60, or 90 minutes IF the coil temperature is below ~32°F. Less efficient—defrosts even when unnecessary.

### Defrost Sequence
1. System switches reversing valve to **cooling mode** (sending hot gas to outdoor coil).
2. Outdoor fan **turns off** (so cold air doesn't blow across the melting frost).
3. **Auxiliary heat strip energizes** (to prevent cold air blowing into the home during defrost).
4. Cycle lasts 2–10 minutes until the outdoor coil reaches ~57°F or a timer expires.
5. System switches back to heating mode.

### Troubleshooting Defrost Failures
| Symptom | Cause | Check |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Outdoor coil is a solid block of ice | Defrost board failure, bad defrost sensor/thermostat, stuck reversing valve | Verify defrost sensor reads correctly; jump the defrost test pins on the board |
| System defrosts too often | Defrost sensor reading incorrectly low, defrost timer set too short | Measure sensor resistance vs. temp chart; check board dip switch settings |
| Cold air from vents during defrost | Aux heat not energizing | Check heat strip sequencer, fuses, and heat strip elements |
| Steam/vapor from outdoor unit | **Normal operation** during defrost. Educate the homeowner. | No action needed—explain to customer |

### Customer Education
- Heat pumps in defrost mode will blow cool air for a few minutes—this is normal.
- Steam from the outdoor unit during defrost is normal, not a fire hazard.
- Heat pumps lose efficiency below 30–35°F. Auxiliary heat (electric strips or gas furnace backup) is expected below this point.
