# Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) Solutions
Advise on IAQ products and strategies to improve comfort and health:

### Filtration
| MERV Rating | Captures | Use Case |
|------------|---------|----------|
| MERV 1–4 | Dust, pollen (>10 μm) | Basic fiberglass filters (minimum code) |
| MERV 8 | Mold spores, dust mites (3–10 μm) | Good residential standard |
| MERV 11–13 | Fine dust, pet dander, bacteria (1–3 μm) | Recommended for allergy sufferers |
| MERV 16 | Virus carriers, smoke (<1 μm) | Hospital-grade, requires commercial air handler |
| HEPA | 99.97% of 0.3 μm particles | Standalone units or bypass filtration |

**Caution**: Higher MERV = more static pressure. Never install MERV 13+ without verifying the system can handle the added restriction. Check blower performance and static pressure.

### Humidity Control
- **Target**: 40–60% relative humidity year-round.
- **Too humid** (Southeast US, coastal): Install a whole-home dehumidifier (70–100 pint/day capacity for most homes). These plumb into the return duct and have their own humidistat.
- **Too dry** (Northern winters): Install a bypass or power humidifier on the supply plenum. Set to 35–40% RH. Watch for condensation on windows—reduce humidifier setting if condensation forms.

### Ventilation
- **ERV (Energy Recovery Ventilator)**: Brings in fresh outdoor air while recovering 70–80% of the energy from exhaust air. Best for humid climates.
- **HRV (Heat Recovery Ventilator)**: Similar to ERV but recovers only sensible heat (not moisture). Best for cold, dry climates.
- ASHRAE Standard 62.2 requires mechanical ventilation in tight homes (<5 ACH50).

### UV & Air Purification
- **UV-C germicidal lamps**: Installed in the return plenum or air handler to kill mold and bacteria on the coil. Effective but limited to line-of-sight.
- **Bipolar ionization / PCO**: Active air purification. Effectiveness varies by manufacturer. Recommend ONLY products with third-party testing (e.g., UL 2998 for ozone safety).
- **Carbon filters**: Effective against VOCs and odors but must be replaced regularly (every 6–12 months).
