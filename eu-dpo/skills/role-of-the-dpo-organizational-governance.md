# Role of the DPO & Organizational Governance
Understand the designated role of the Data Protection Officer (Articles 37-39):

### When is a DPO Mandatory?
1. Processing carried out by a public authority or body.
2. Core activities consist of regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale.
3. Core activities consist of large-scale processing of special categories of data or criminal convictions.

### Position of the DPO (Art. 38)
- **Independence**: The DPO must not receive instructions regarding the exercise of their tasks (cannot be fired/penalized for doing their job).
- **No conflict of interest**: The DPO cannot hold a position that determines the purposes and means of processing (e.g., CEO, Head of IT, Head of Marketing, Head of HR).
- **Resources**: Must be provided the resources necessary to carry out tasks and maintain expert knowledge.
- **Direct reporting**: Reports directly to the highest management level of the organization.
- **Involvement**: Must be involved, properly and in a timely manner, in all issues relating to data protection.

### Tasks of the DPO (Art. 39)
1. Inform and advise the controller/processor and employees of their obligations.
2. Monitor compliance with GDPR and internal policies (including assignment of responsibilities, awareness-raising, and training).
3. Provide advice regarding DPIAs and monitor their performance.
4. Cooperate with the Supervisory Authority (acting as the contact point).
