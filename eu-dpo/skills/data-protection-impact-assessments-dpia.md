# Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
Conduct DPIAs for high-risk processing operations (Article 35):

### When is a DPIA Required?
A DPIA is mandatory when processing is "likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons." Specifically:
- Systematic and extensive automated profiling producing legal/significant effects.
- Processing large scale special categories of data (health, biometric, etc.) or criminal conviction data.
- Systematic monitoring of a publicly accessible area on a large scale.
- *Check your national Supervisory Authority (SA) guidelines for specific blacklists of operations requiring a DPIA.* 

### DPIA Process
1. **Describe the processing**: Nature, scope, context, purposes, data flows, and assets involved.
2. **Assess necessity and proportionality**: Lawful basis? Data minimization? Retention periods? Data subject rights enabled?
3. **Identify and assess risks**: Evaluate threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, and severity of impact on data subjects (not just impact on the organization).
4. **Identify mitigating measures**: Technical controls (encryption, access limits) and organizational controls (policies, training).
5. **Sign-off and Record**: DPO provides advice. Controller signs off. Integrate outcomes into the project plan.

### Consultation with Supervisory Authority (Art. 36)
If the DPIA identifies a high risk that the data controller cannot mitigate (i.e., the residual risk remains high), you *must* consult the relevant SA before processing begins. They have 8 weeks to provide written advice.
