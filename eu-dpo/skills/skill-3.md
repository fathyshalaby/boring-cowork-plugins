# Data Subject Rights (DSR/DSAR)
Manage Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) and other rights:

### The 8 Data Subject Rights
1. **Right to be informed** (Arts. 13 & 14): Privacy notices explaining who, what, why, how long, and rights.
2. **Right of access** (Art. 15): The DSAR. Individuals can request a copy of their data and processing details.
3. **Right to rectification** (Art. 16): Correcting inaccurate or incomplete data.
4. **Right to erasure / Right to be forgotten** (Art. 17): Deletion when data is no longer necessary, consent withdrawn, or processed unlawfully.
5. **Right to restrict processing** (Art. 18): Halting processing temporarily while a dispute is resolved.
6. **Right to data portability** (Art. 20): Receiving data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format to transfer to another controller (applies only to data provided by the subject based on consent or contract, processed by automated means).
7. **Right to object** (Art. 21): Stopping processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing (absolute right for direct marketing).
8. **Rights regarding automated decision-making** (Art. 22): Human intervention in decisions with legal/significant effects.

### DSAR Handling Protocol
1. **Verification**: Verify the identity of the requester.
2. **Deadline**: Respond without undue delay, and at the latest within **1 month** of receipt. Can be extended by 2 further months for complex or numerous requests (must notify within the first month).
3. **Search & Collation**: Search all systems, emails, filing cabinets, and processors.
4. **Redaction**: Redact information identifying third parties or covered by legal privilege.
5. **Format**: Provide securely, in electronic format if requested electronically.
6. **Fees**: Generally free, unless manifestly unfounded or excessive (can charge a reasonable administrative fee or refuse).
