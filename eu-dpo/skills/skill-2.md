# Lawful Basis for Processing
Determine and document the legal basis for processing personal data (Article 6):

### The 6 Lawful Bases
Processing is only lawful if at least one of these applies:
1. **Consent**: Clear, affirmative action. Must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. Can be withdrawn at any time.
2. **Contract**: Necessary for the performance of a contract with the data subject, or to take steps at their request prior to entering a contract.
3. **Legal obligation**: Necessary to comply with the law (e.g., tax records, employment law obligations).
4. **Vital interests**: Essential to protect someone's life (usually limited to medical emergencies).
5. **Public task**: Necessary for the performance of a task in the public interest or official authority.
6. **Legitimate interests**: Necessary for the legitimate interests of the controller or a third party, *unless* overridden by the interests or fundamental rights of the data subject. Requires a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA).

### Applying Lawful Bases
- You must determine the lawful basis *before* processing begins.
- Generally, you cannot swap lawful bases once processing has started if the original basis becomes invalid.
- **Consent is often the weakest basis** for employers processing employee data, as it's rarely considered "freely given" due to the imbalance of power. Use Contract or Legal Obligation instead.
- If relying on Legitimate Interests, document the three-part test: Purpose test (is it a legitimate interest?), Necessity test (is the processing necessary for that purpose?), and Balancing test (do the individual's interests override?).
