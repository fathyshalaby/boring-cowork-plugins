# Cookies, Tracking & ePrivacy
Comply with the ePrivacy Directive (Cookie Law) alongside GDPR:

### The Relationship: GDPR + ePrivacy
- **GDPR**: Governs the processing of *personal data*.
- **ePrivacy Directive (PECR in UK)**: Governs the storing of information, or gaining access to information already stored, in the terminal equipment of a subscriber or user (i.e., placing/reading cookies on a device), *regardless of whether it's personal data*.
- If a cookie tracks identifiable data, BOTH apply.

### Cookie Consent Rules
1. **Strictly Necessary Cookies**: Exempt from consent. Essential for the service explicitly requested by the user (e.g., shopping cart, security, load balancing). Cannot be used for analytics.
2. **All Other Cookies (Analytics, Marketing, Preferences)**: Require prior, active, informed consent BEFORE they are dropped.
   - No pre-ticked boxes. No implied consent by "continuing to use this site."
   - Granular choice: Users must be able to accept some categories and reject others.
   - Easy withdrawal: It must be as easy to withdraw consent as it is to give it.
   - Cookie walls (forcing consent to access content) are generally not permitted by EDPB guidelines.

### Cookie Banner Best Practices
- "Accept All" and "Reject All" buttons should be equally prominent on the first layer.
- Provide a link to a detailed Cookie Policy listing all specific cookies, purposes, and lifespans.
- Implement a Consent Management Platform (CMP) that actually blocks scripts from firing until consent is logged.
