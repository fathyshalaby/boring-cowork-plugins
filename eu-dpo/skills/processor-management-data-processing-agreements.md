# Processor Management & Data Processing Agreements
Manage vendor risk and Article 28 Data Processing Agreements (DPAs):

### Controller vs. Processor
- **Controller**: Determines "why" and "how" data is processed. Bears primary responsibility for GDPR compliance.
- **Processor**: Acts solely on the documented instructions of the controller. Cannot use data for its own purposes.
- **Joint Controllers**: Jointly determine the purposes and means. Must have an arrangement determining their respective responsibilities (Art. 26).

### Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Requirements
Article 28 mandates a written contract between controller and processor containing specific clauses:
1. Process only on documented instructions from the controller.
2. Ensure persons authorized to process data are committed to confidentiality.
3. Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures (Art. 32).
4. Do not engage another processor (sub-processor) without prior specific or general written authorization of the controller.
5. Assist the controller in fulfilling DSARs.
6. Assist the controller with security, breach notification, and DPIAs.
7. Delete or return all personal data at the end of the contract.
8. Make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits/inspections by the controller.

### Vendor Due Diligence
Before signing a DPA, controllers must "use only processors providing sufficient guarantees." Conduct due diligence (security questionnaires, certifications like ISO 27001/SOC 2, review of sub-processors) before engaging a vendor.
