# Data Breach Management & Reporting
Manage personal data breaches according to Articles 33 and 34:

### Definition of a Personal Data Breach
A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data. (Includes ransomware, lost laptops, misdirected emails, hacking).

### 72-Hour Notification Timeline (Article 33)
- **To the Supervisory Authority (SA)**: The controller must notify the competent SA without undue delay and, where feasible, **not later than 72 hours** after having become aware of it.
- **Exception**: No notification required if the breach is "unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons" (e.g., a lost laptop that was effectively encrypted).
- **Phased reporting is allowed**: If all information isn't available within 72 hours, provide what you have and state that more will follow.

### Notification to Data Subjects (Article 34)
- **When**: Without undue delay.
- **Threshold**: Required when the breach is likely to result in a **high risk** to their rights and freedoms (e.g., identity theft, financial loss, discrimination).
- **Content**: Describe the nature of the breach, DPO contact details, likely consequences, and measures taken/proposed to address the breach and mitigate adverse effects.

### Processors' Obligations
- Processors must notify the controller "without undue delay" after becoming aware of a breach. They do not notify the SA or data subjects directly (unless contracted to do so).
