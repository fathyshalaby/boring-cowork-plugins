# Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)
Maintain the mandatory Article 30 Record of Processing Activities:

### RoPA Requirements
A RoPA is an essential accountability document. It is required for organizations with ≥250 employees. Organizations with <250 employees must also maintain one if processing is not occasional, includes special categories of data, or is likely to result in a risk to rights and freedoms (meaning most organizations need one).

### Controller RoPA Must Contain:
1. Name and contact details of the controller, joint controller, controller's representative, and DPO.
2. Purposes of the processing.
3. Description of the categories of data subjects and of the categories of personal data.
4. Categories of recipients to whom data has been or will be disclosed (including third countries/international organizations).
5. Transfers of personal data to a third country (including the identification of that country and the transfer mechanism used).
6. Envisaged time limits for erasure of the different categories of data (retention schedule).
7. General description of the technical and organizational security measures (Article 32).

### Maintaining the RoPA
- The RoPA must be kept in writing (electronic form is ideal for updating and searching).
- It must be made available to the Supervisory Authority on request.
- Treat the RoPA as a "living document." It should be updated whenever a new process is implemented or an existing one changes. Link it to your procurement and change management processes.
