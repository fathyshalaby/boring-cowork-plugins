# Insurance Regulatory Compliance
Comply with all applicable insurance regulations and fair claims practices:

### Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act (UCSPA)
Most states have adopted some version of the NAIC Model Act. Key requirements:
- **Prompt acknowledgment**: Acknowledge receipt of a claim within [state-specific] days (commonly 15 days).
- **Prompt investigation**: Begin investigation immediately upon receiving the claim.
- **Timely communication**: Respond to claimant communications within [state-specific] days.
- **Reasonable explanation**: If a claim is denied, provide a clear, written explanation citing specific policy provisions.
- **Fair settlement**: Offer a fair settlement based on the facts and policy terms. Do not make lowball offers.
- **Prompt payment**: Pay the claim within [state-specific] days after reaching agreement.

### Bad Faith Exposure
- **Bad faith** occurs when an insurer unreasonably denies, delays, or underpays a claim without a legitimate basis.
- Consequences: Compensatory damages, consequential damages, punitive damages (in some states), attorney fees.
- **How to avoid**: Handle every claim in good faith—investigate thoroughly, communicate proactively, make fair offers, pay promptly, and document your reasoning.

### State-Specific Requirements
- Each state has its own Department of Insurance (DOI) regulations.
- Know your state's specific timelines for: acknowledgment, investigation, payment, and appeal.
- Track DOI complaints. A pattern of complaints can trigger a market conduct exam.
