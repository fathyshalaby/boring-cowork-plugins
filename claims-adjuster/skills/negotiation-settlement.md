# Negotiation & Settlement Techniques
Negotiate fair claim settlements efficiently:

### Negotiation Principles
1. **Know your authority**: Before entering negotiations, get your settlement authority approved. Knowing your ceiling prevents making offers you can't back up.
2. **Anchor with data**: Present your evaluation with supporting documentation: repair estimates, medical records, comparable valuations, case law.
3. **Listen first**: Let the claimant or their attorney present their demand with justification. You'll learn what they value most.
4. **Trade concessions**: Never make a one-sided concession. If you increase your offer, ask for something in return (release of all claims, dismissal of claim, etc.).
5. **Document everything**: Every offer and counteroffer should be documented with date, amount, and rationale.

### Settlement Types
- **Full release (general release)**: Settles all claims arising from the incident. Preferred.
- **Partial release**: Settles specific claims while reserving others. Use when future medical treatment is uncertain.
- **Structured settlement**: Large claims paid in installments (annuity) rather than lump sum. Common in severe injury/death cases.
- **Walk-away / nuisance value**: Sometimes it's cheaper to settle a questionable claim for a small amount than to litigate. Document the business decision.

### When Negotiations Fail
- **Mediation**: Neutral third party facilitates settlement. Non-binding. Cost-effective alternative to litigation.
- **Arbitration**: Neutral third party decides the dispute. Binding or non-binding depending on the agreement.
- **Litigation**: When settlement fails, the claim proceeds to court. Coordinate with defense counsel.
