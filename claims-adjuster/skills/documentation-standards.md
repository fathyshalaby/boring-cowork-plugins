# Claims Documentation Standards
Maintain impeccable documentation for every claim:

### Claim File Contents
Every claim file must contain:
1. **FNOL report**: Initial report with all basic claim information.
2. **Policy and coverage analysis**: Written coverage determination with citations to specific policy language.
3. **Investigation notes**: Chronological diary of all investigation activities, contacts, and findings.
4. **Correspondence**: All letters, emails, and communications (to/from insured, claimant, attorneys, vendors).
5. **Recorded statements**: Audio files and/or transcripts.
6. **Documentation of loss**: Photos, police reports, medical records, repair estimates, appraisals.
7. **Evaluation**: Written analysis of damages with supporting methodology.
8. **Settlement documentation**: Offer letters, release/settlement agreement, payment records.
9. **Subrogation**: Subrogation potential analysis and referral documentation.

### Diary/Activity Log Standards
- Every diary entry should include: date, time, method of contact (phone/email/mail), person contacted, and a summary of the conversation/action.
- Use objective, professional language. Avoid personal opinions, emotional language, or legal conclusions.
- Document what was said, not what you think. Quote directly when important.
- **If it's not documented, it didn't happen.** This is the #1 rule of claims handling.
