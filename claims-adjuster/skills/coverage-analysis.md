# Policy Coverage Analysis
Analyze insurance policies to determine coverage, exclusions, and limits:

### Coverage Analysis Framework
1. **Is this an insured event?** Match the reported loss to a covered peril in the policy. If the peril isn't listed (named perils policy) or is specifically excluded, coverage may not apply.
2. **Was the policy active?** Verify the policy was in force on the date of loss. Check for lapse, cancellation, or non-payment.
3. **Is the claimant covered?** Named insured, additional insured, permissive user (auto), resident relative.
4. **Do exclusions apply?** Read every applicable exclusion carefully. Common exclusions: intentional acts, wear and tear, flood (homeowners), business use (auto/home), nuclear/war.
5. **What are the limits?** Per occurrence, aggregate, per person, per property. Sublimits for specific perils (e.g., $5,000 sublimit for jewelry).
6. **What is the deductible?** Dollar amount or percentage-based (common for hurricane/wind).

### Common Policy Types
| Policy | Covers | Key Exclusions |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| HO-3 (Homeowners) | Dwelling (open perils), personal property (named perils), liability | Flood, earthquake, intentional loss, war, neglect, ordinance/law |
| Auto (PAP) | Liability, collision, comprehensive, UM/UIM, medical payments | Business use, racing, intentional damage |
| Commercial General Liability (CGL) | Bodily injury, property damage to third parties | Professional services (E&O), auto, pollution, employment practices |
| Commercial Property (CP) | Building, business personal property, business income | Flood, earthquake (unless endorsed), mechanical breakdown |
| Workers' Compensation | Employee work-related injuries/illness | Intentional self-injury, intoxication |

### Reservation of Rights
- When coverage is uncertain, issue a **Reservation of Rights (ROR) letter** to the insured. This protects the insurer's right to later deny coverage based on the investigation findings while continuing to investigate the claim.
- Always consult with your coverage counsel before issuing a ROR on complex matters.
