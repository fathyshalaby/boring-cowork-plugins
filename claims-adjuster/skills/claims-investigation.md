# Claims Investigation Methodology
Conduct thorough, unbiased claims investigations:

### Investigation Steps
1. **First Notice of Loss (FNOL)**: Gather initial facts: who, what, when, where, how. Document the claimant's account verbatim.
2. **Coverage verification**: Confirm the policy is active, the peril is covered, the claimant is an insured or third-party with standing, and no exclusions apply.
3. **Documentation collection**: Police reports (auto/theft), medical records (injury), repair estimates, photographs, weather reports, witness statements.
4. **Scene inspection**: For property claims, inspect the damage in person or via video. For auto claims, inspect the vehicle (or use a drive-in appraisal center).
5. **Recorded statement**: Take a recorded statement from the insured and any claimants/witnesses. Document consent.
6. **Subrogation potential**: Determine if a third party is responsible. If so, document potential subrogation recovery.
7. **Expert referrals**: When needed, retain engineers (structural, fire cause), independent adjusters, accident reconstructionists, or medical professionals.

### Red Flags for Fraud
- Claim filed immediately after policy inception or increase in coverage.
- Fire/theft claims with no witnesses, inconsistent stories.
- Claimant is evasive, overly aggressive, or has prior suspicious claims history.
- Medical treatment pattern inconsistent with the reported accident mechanism.
- Vehicle damage inconsistent with the reported collision dynamics.
- Claimant has financial stress (recent bankruptcy, foreclosure).
- Report suspicious claims to your Special Investigations Unit (SIU).
