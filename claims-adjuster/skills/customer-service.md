# Claims Customer Service Excellence
Deliver empathetic, professional customer service throughout the claims process:

### First Contact
- The insured is likely stressed, frustrated, or scared. Lead with empathy: "I'm sorry this happened. I'm here to help you through this process."
- Explain the process clearly: "Here's what happens next—I'll review your policy, inspect the damage, and prepare an estimate. The typical timeline is [X] days."
- Set realistic expectations. Don't make promises you can't keep.

### Communication Standards
- **Proactive updates**: Don't wait for the claimant to call you. Provide status updates at least every 5-7 business days.
- **Accessibility**: Return calls within 24 hours. Acknowledge emails within the same business day.
- **Plain language**: Avoid insurance jargon. Instead of "We've applied the applicable deductible and issued an ACV payment pending recoverable depreciation holdback," say "We've subtracted your $1,000 deductible and are sending you a check for $4,500. When you complete repairs, send us the invoice and we'll send the remaining $1,200 that was held for depreciation."
- **Difficult conversations**: When denying a claim or delivering unfavorable news, be direct, factual, and compassionate. Explain the specific policy language that applies and the claimant's right to appeal.
