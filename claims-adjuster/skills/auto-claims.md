# Auto Claims Handling
Handle auto insurance claims from accident to settlement:

### Auto Claim Process
1. **FNOL**: Date/time/location of accident, vehicles involved, driver info, passenger info, injuries, police report number.
2. **Liability determination**: Review police report, witness statements, photos, and applicable traffic law. Assign fault percentage.
3. **Vehicle inspection**: Inspect for damage consistent with the reported loss. Photo-document all damage.
4. **Medical treatment tracking**: For injury claims, request medical records and billing periodically. Don't settle until Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI).
5. **Subrogation**: If the other party is at fault, pursue recovery through their insurer.

### Liability Assessment
- **Contributory negligence states**: If the claimant is even 1% at fault, they are barred from recovery (only a few states: AL, DC, MD, NC, VA).
- **Comparative negligence (modified)**: Claimant's recovery reduced by their fault percentage. Barred at 50% or 51% (depending on state).
- **Pure comparative negligence**: Claimant can recover even if 99% at fault, reduced by their percentage (CA, FL, NY, etc.).

### Common Auto Coverage
| Coverage | What It Pays | Trigger |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Liability BI | Other party's injuries | You at fault |
| Liability PD | Other party's vehicle/property | You at fault |
| Collision | Your vehicle damage in an accident | Any collision, regardless of fault |
| Comprehensive | Your vehicle damage from non-collision | Theft, vandalism, weather, animal strike |
| UM/UIM | Your injuries/damages from uninsured driver | Other driver at fault, uninsured/underinsured |
| Medical Payments | Your medical bills regardless of fault | Accident-related medical treatment |
| PIP | Medical + lost wages (no-fault) | Accident-related (in no-fault states) |
