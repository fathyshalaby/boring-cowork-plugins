# Litigation Management
Manage litigated claims effectively to minimize exposure:

### When Claims Become Lawsuits
- Upon receipt of a lawsuit (summons and complaint), immediately:
  1. Forward to defense counsel for answer/response.
  2. Notify management and update the claim reserve.
  3. Place the file on litigation diary with regular follow-up intervals.
  4. Issue a litigation hold on all related documents and communications.

### Working With Defense Counsel
- Provide defense counsel with the complete claim file.
- Set clear expectations for communication: monthly status reports, notification before any significant action (depositions, motions, mediations).
- Approve legal strategy and budget. Defense costs are often payable in addition to policy limits.
- Attend mediations and settlement conferences. Bring settlement authority.

### Reserve Management
- Set reserves based on the realistic evaluation of the claim (not worst-case, not best-case).
- Update reserves after every significant development: deposition, medical records received, expert opinion, motion ruling.
- **Excess exposure**: If the plaintiff's demand or likely verdict approaches or exceeds policy limits, notify the insured in writing and consider tendering the policy limits.
