# EV Fleet Transition Planning
Plan and execute the transition to electric vehicles:

### Readiness Assessment
1. **Route analysis**: Which vehicles have daily routes within EV range? Target these first.
2. **Charging infrastructure**: What's the electrical capacity at your facilities? Can you install Level 2 or DC fast chargers?
3. **TCO comparison**: Compare EV vs. ICE total cost of ownership over the holding period. Include fuel/electricity, maintenance (EVs are significantly lower), purchase price, incentives.
4. **Driver acceptance**: Pilot program with volunteer drivers before full deployment.

### Charging Infrastructure
| Charger Type | Power | Charge Time (empty to full) | Cost (installed) |
|-------------|-------|---------------------------|------------------|
| Level 1 (120V) | 1.4 kW | 40-60 hours | $500-$1,500 |
| Level 2 (240V) | 7-19 kW | 4-10 hours | $2,000-$8,000 |
| DC Fast (480V) | 50-350 kW | 20-60 minutes | $30,000-$150,000 |

### Federal & State Incentives
- Federal tax credit: Up to $7,500 per vehicle (IRS 30D for purchased, 45W for leased).
- State incentives vary: rebates, tax credits, HOV access, reduced registration.
- Utility incentives: Many utilities offer EV charging rate programs and infrastructure rebates.
- Track all incentives — they can significantly change the TCO equation.
