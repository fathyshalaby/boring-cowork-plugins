# Vehicle Specification & Procurement
Specify and procure fleet vehicles effectively:

### Specification Development
- Define the job requirements first: payload capacity, towing needs, passenger count, cargo volume, special equipment (PTO, liftgate, crane).
- Consider: fuel type (gas, diesel, hybrid, EV, CNG), 2WD vs. 4WD, cab configuration, bed/body length.
- Standardize across the fleet when possible. Fewer configurations = simpler parts inventory, training, and maintenance.

### Procurement Methods
| Method | Best For | Typical Savings |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| State/cooperative contract | Government/nonprofit fleets | 10-20% off MSRP |
| Competitive bid | Large orders (10+ units) | 15-25% off MSRP |
| Fleet incentive programs | Any fleet purchases | Varies by OEM |
| Dealer negotiation | Small orders | 5-15% off MSRP |

### Upfitting & Commissioning
- Plan upfitting early: lead times for bodies, racks, and special equipment can be 8-16 weeks.
- Specify upfitting in the bid when possible to get a turnkey vehicle.
- Commissioning checklist: graphics/decals, telematics install, fuel card, registration/title, insurance, driver assignment, PM schedule setup.
