# Denial Management & Appeals
Aggressively manage claim denials to maximize revenue recovery:

### Common Denial Reason Codes (CARC/RARC)
| Code | Reason | Action |
|------|--------|--------|
| CO-4 | Procedure code inconsistent with modifier | Review modifier usage, correct and resubmit |
| CO-16 | Missing information | Check claim for required fields, resubmit with needed info |
| CO-18 | Duplicate claim | Verify prior submission, appeal if legitimately separate |
| CO-29 | Timely filing limit exceeded | Check date tracking, appeal with proof of timely submission |
| CO-50 | Non-covered service | Verify coverage, check medical necessity, consider ABN for patient |
| CO-97 | Bundled into another service | Review CCI edits, apply appropriate modifier or adjust billing |
| CO-197 | Prior authorization required | Obtain retro-auth if possible, inform provider for future |
| PR-1 | Patient responsibility (deductible) | Bill patient |
| PR-2 | Patient responsibility (coinsurance) | Bill patient |
| PR-3 | Patient responsibility (copay) | Bill patient |

### Appeal Process
1. **Level 1 (Internal appeal)**: Submit a written appeal with supporting documentation (medical records, operative notes, letter of medical necessity) within the payer's appeal deadline (typically 60-180 days from denial).
2. **Level 2 (External review)**: If the internal appeal is denied, request an external/independent review. The payer must provide instructions.
3. **For Medicare**: Follow the 5-level appeal process: Redetermination → Reconsideration (QIC) → ALJ Hearing → Medicare Appeals Council → Federal District Court.

### Best Practices
- Work denials within 48 hours of receipt. The clock is ticking on appeal deadlines.
- Categorize denials by type and payer. Identify patterns (e.g., a specific payer consistently denying a certain service).
- Track denial rates, appeal success rates, and revenue recovered through appeals.
