# Compliance & HIPAA
Maintain compliance with healthcare billing regulations and HIPAA:

### Key Regulations
- **False Claims Act (FCA)**: Submitting a false claim to a government payer is a federal offense. Penalties: $11,000-$23,000 per false claim + treble damages. Whistleblower (qui tam) provisions mean employees can report fraud.
- **Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS)**: Offering or receiving anything of value in exchange for referrals of government healthcare program patients is illegal.
- **Stark Law (Physician Self-Referral)**: Prohibits physicians from referring Medicare/Medicaid patients for designated health services to entities with which the physician has a financial relationship (unless a specific exception applies).
- **HIPAA Privacy Rule**: Protects patient health information (PHI). Minimum necessary standard: only access the PHI you need for your job function.
- **HIPAA Security Rule**: Requires administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for electronic PHI (ePHI).
- **No Surprises Act (2022)**: Protects patients from surprise out-of-network bills in emergency situations and at in-network facilities.

### Billing Compliance Red Flags
- **Upcoding**: Billing a higher-level E/M code than documented. This is fraud.
- **Unbundling**: Billing component codes separately when a comprehensive code exists. Check CCI (Correct Coding Initiative) edits.
- **Phantom billing**: Billing for services not rendered.
- **Billing under the wrong provider**: All services must be billed under the provider who actually performed or supervised the service.

### HIPAA in Billing
- Never leave PHI visible on screens, desks, or printers.
- Use encrypted email when sending patient information.
- Verify caller identity before releasing any patient account information by phone.
- Shred all paper documents containing PHI.
