# Insurance Plans & Payer Knowledge
Understand different insurance types and their billing requirements:

### Insurance Types
| Type | Key Characteristics | Billing Notes |
|------|-------------------|--------------|
| Medicare Part A | Hospital/facility services | UB-04 form, MAC (Medicare Administrative Contractor) |
| Medicare Part B | Professional services, outpatient | CMS-1500, submitted to MAC |
| Medicare Advantage (Part C) | Commercial managed care plan | Bill the plan directly, not CMS |
| Medicaid | State-run, low-income | Varies significantly by state, strict timely filing |
| Commercial PPO | Out-of-network benefits available | Patient responsible for balance after allowed amount |
| Commercial HMO | In-network only, requires referrals | PCP referral required for specialists |
| Workers' Comp | On-the-job injuries | Separate fee schedules, employer's carrier info required |
| Auto/PIP | Motor vehicle accident injuries | Varies by state, PIP limits apply |
| TRICARE | Military/dependent coverage | Follows Medicare rules with some differences |
| VA | Veterans Administration | Typically billed directly by VA facilities |

### Coordination of Benefits (COB)
- When a patient has multiple insurance plans, determine primary vs. secondary vs. tertiary.
- **Birthday rule** (for dependent children): The plan of the parent whose birthday comes first in the calendar year is primary.
- **Active vs. retiree**: Active employee coverage is primary over retiree/COBRA coverage.
- Bill primary first. After primary pays, bill secondary with the primary's EOB attached.

### Contracted vs. Non-Contracted
- **In-network**: Provider has a contract with the payer. Accept the allowed amount as payment in full (cannot balance-bill the patient except for copay/deductible/coinsurance).
- **Out-of-network**: No contract. Can balance-bill the patient for the difference between the billed amount and the payer's payment (subject to state surprise billing laws like the No Surprises Act).
