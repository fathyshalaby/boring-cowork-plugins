# Payment Posting & Reconciliation
Post payments accurately and reconcile accounts:

### Posting ERA/EOB Payments
1. **Match the payment**: Match the check or EFT to the corresponding ERA (Electronic Remittance Advice) or EOB (Explanation of Benefits).
2. **Post by line item**: Post the allowed amount, payment, adjustment, and patient responsibility for each line item on the claim.
3. **Adjustment types**:
   - Contractual adjustment (CO-45): Difference between billed amount and allowed amount. Write off per contract.
   - Patient responsibility: Deductible (PR-1), coinsurance (PR-2), copay (PR-3). Transfer to patient balance.
   - Denial (CO with various reason codes): DO NOT write off. Work the denial.

### Payment Discrepancies
- If the payment doesn't match the expected allowed amount per the fee schedule, investigate:
  - Is the CPT code correct?
  - Was a modifier missing that reduced payment?
  - Was the service down-coded by the payer?
  - Is this the correct payer fee schedule?
- Track underpayments systematically. Even small underpayments add up across thousands of claims.

### Patient Balance Management
- Send patient statements within 5 days of receiving insurance payment.
- Statement cycle: Statement 1 → 30 days → Statement 2 → 30 days → Final Notice → 30 days → Collections.
- Offer payment plans for large balances ($200+). Document the agreement.
- Train front desk to collect copays at time of service. This is the most cost-effective collection point.
