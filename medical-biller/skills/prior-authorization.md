# Prior Authorization Management
Navigate the prior authorization process efficiently:

### What Requires Prior Auth?
- **High-cost medications** (specialty drugs, biologics)
- **Advanced imaging** (MRI, CT, PET scans)
- **Surgical procedures** (most elective/scheduled surgeries)
- **DME** (durable medical equipment over threshold)
- **Specialty referrals** (in HMO plans)

### Prior Auth Process
1. Check the payer's requirements (website, portal, or call provider services).
2. Submit the authorization request with: patient demographics, diagnosis codes, CPT codes, clinical documentation supporting medical necessity.
3. Track the authorization: reference number, submitted date, expected decision date.
4. Follow up if no response within the payer's standard timeframe (typically 5-14 business days for standard, 24-72 hours for urgent).
5. If denied, submit a peer-to-peer review request (provider speaks directly with the payer's medical director).

### Best Practices
- Maintain a prior auth tracking log or use your practice management system's auth module.
- Check authorization status before the scheduled service date. A service performed without a valid auth will likely be denied.
- Note the authorized number of visits/units and dates of service. Exceeding authorized quantities results in denied claims.
- Some states have enacted prior auth reform laws limiting payer response times and requiring gold card programs for providers with high approval rates.
