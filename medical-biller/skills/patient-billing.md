# Patient Billing & Collections
Handle patient billing with compassion, clarity, and compliance:

### Patient Financial Communication
- **Upfront cost estimates**: Provide patients with an estimated out-of-pocket cost before the service whenever possible. Use their deductible/coinsurance information from eligibility verification.
- **Good Faith Estimate (No Surprises Act)**: Uninsured or self-pay patients are entitled to a Good Faith Estimate of charges for scheduled services.
- **Explanation**: When patients question a bill, explain it clearly: "Your insurance paid $X. Your deductible was $X. Your coinsurance is $X. That leaves a patient balance of $X."

### Collection Best Practices
- **In-office collection**: Collect copays, outstanding balances, and self-pay payments at the time of service. This is the single most effective collection strategy.
- **Prompt statements**: Send the first statement within 5 business days of posting the insurance payment.
- **Multiple touchpoints**: Statement → Phone call → Final notice → Collection agency.
- **Payment plans**: Offer interest-free payment plans. Standard: divide the balance into 3-6 monthly payments.
- **Payment options**: Accept multiple payment methods: cash, check, credit/debit card, HSA/FSA card, online portal payment.

### Regulatory Considerations
- **Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)**: Governs collection practices. Cannot call before 8 AM or after 9 PM, cannot use abusive language, must cease contact if requested in writing.
- **Charity care/sliding fee scale**: Non-profit and FQHC organizations must have financial assistance policies. Screen patients for eligibility.
- **Medical debt and credit reporting**: Recent changes limit reporting of paid medical debt. Check current federal and state regulations.
