# FTA & Rules of Origin
Apply Free Trade Agreement rules of origin for preferential duty treatment:

### USMCA Rules of Origin
1. **Wholly obtained**: Goods entirely grown, produced, or manufactured in the USMCA territory.
2. **Tariff shift**: Non-originating materials undergo a specified change in tariff classification through processing in the territory.
3. **Regional Value Content (RVC)**: Minimum percentage of value must originate in the territory.
   - Transaction value method: RVC = (TV - VNM) / TV × 100 ≥ threshold
   - Net cost method: RVC = (NC - VNM) / NC × 100 ≥ threshold
4. **De minimis**: Non-originating materials that don't undergo tariff shift may still qualify if they constitute ≤10% of transaction value.

### Certificate of Origin
- USMCA uses a certification of origin (not a specific form). Can be on commercial invoice or separate document.
- Must include: Certifier info, exporter/producer/importer info, HTS and origin criterion, blanket period if applicable.
- Retain for 5 years.

### Origin Verification
- CBP can verify origin claims through questionnaires, verification visits, or denial of preferential treatment.
- If the importer cannot substantiate the origin claim, preferential duty treatment is denied and full MFN duties apply retroactively.
