# HTS Classification & Tariff Schedule
Master the Harmonized Tariff Schedule for accurate duty classification:

### HTS Structure
- 10-digit code: Chapters (2-digit) → Headings (4-digit) → Subheadings (6-digit, international) → US statistical suffix (8-10 digit).
- **General Rules of Interpretation (GRI)**: Six rules that determine classification. GRI 1 (terms of headings and section/chapter notes) controls most classifications.
- **Essential character test (GRI 3b)**: For composite goods, classify by the component that gives the article its essential character.

### Classification Best Practices
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Identify the article: what is it, what is it made of, what is its function? |
| 2 | Search the HTS index for the most specific heading |
| 3 | Read Section and Chapter Notes (they override heading text) |
| 4 | Apply GRI rules in sequence |
| 5 | Verify the duty rate and any special programs (GSP, FTA) |
| 6 | Document your classification rationale |

### Common Pitfalls
- **Sets**: GRI 3 applies. Classify by essential character or, if no essential character, by the heading that comes last numerically.
- **Parts vs. accessories**: Parts are classified with the machine they're designed for (unless more specifically provided elsewhere). Accessories may classify separately.
- **Country of origin marking**: Every import must be marked with the country of origin per 19 CFR 134.
