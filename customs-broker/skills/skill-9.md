# Logistics & Supply Chain Coordination
Coordinate international logistics for efficient customs clearance:

### Pre-Arrival Planning
- Obtain commercial documents from the shipper/supplier well before cargo arrival.
- File ISF (ocean) or pre-clearance (air) to avoid delays and penalties.
- Verify cargo is not subject to holds (FDA, USDA, CPSC examinations).
- Pre-clear when possible: file entry before arrival so goods release immediately.

### Cargo Examinations
| Exam Type | Agency | Impact |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| VACIS/NII (X-ray) | CBP | 1-2 days delay, low cost |
| Intensive Exam | CBP | 3-5 days, devanning/restuffing fees ($1,000-5,000+) |
| Tailgate Exam | CBP | 1-2 days, visual inspection |
| FDA Hold | FDA | Variable, may require lab testing |
| USDA Hold | USDA | Fumigation may be required |

### Demurrage & Detention Management
- **Demurrage**: Charges for cargo sitting at the port/terminal beyond free time (typically 3-5 free days). $150-400/day per container.
- **Detention**: Charges for keeping the container beyond free time after it leaves the terminal. $100-300/day.
- Proactive communication with the importer about expected clearance timeline prevents costly delays.
