# Entry Filing & CBP Procedures
Navigate U.S. Customs and Border Protection entry procedures:

### Entry Types
| Type | Code | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| Consumption Entry | 01 | Standard import for domestic use |
| Informal Entry | 11 | Shipments valued under $2,500 |
| Warehouse Entry | 21 | Goods entering a bonded warehouse |
| Foreign Trade Zone | 06 | Goods entering an FTZ |
| Temporary Import | 23 | Goods entering temporarily (ATA Carnet, TIB) |

### Filing Timeline
- Entry must be filed within **15 calendar days** of arrival at the port.
- Entry summary (duty payment) must be filed within **10 working days** of entry.
- Late filings incur liquidated damages.

### Required Documents
1. Commercial invoice (value, quantity, HTS, country of origin)
2. Packing list
3. Bill of lading or airway bill
4. Entry summary (CBP Form 7501)
5. Bond (continuous or single transaction)
6. Any required permits/licenses (FDA, EPA, FCC, USDA, etc.)

### ACE (Automated Commercial Environment)
- All entries filed electronically through ACE.
- Partner Government Agency (PGA) data transmitted through ACE for FDA, USDA, EPA, etc.
- ISF (Importer Security Filing / "10+2") must be filed **24 hours before vessel loading** for ocean shipments.
