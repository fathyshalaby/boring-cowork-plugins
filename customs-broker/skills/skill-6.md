# Bonded Warehousing & FTZ Operations
Understand bonded warehouse and Foreign Trade Zone operations:

### Bonded Warehouses
- Goods can be stored for up to **5 years** without paying duty.
- Duty is paid only when goods are withdrawn for consumption.
- Types: General order (unclaimed goods), public bonded warehouse, private bonded warehouse.
- Goods can be manipulated (repacked, relabeled, tested) but not manufactured in a bonded warehouse.

### Foreign Trade Zones (FTZ)
- Designated areas within the US that are considered outside the customs territory for duty purposes.
- **Inverted tariff benefit**: If the finished product has a lower duty rate than the components, manufacture in an FTZ and pay the lower rate.
- **Duty deferral**: Defer duty payment until goods enter US commerce.
- **Duty elimination**: If goods are re-exported from the FTZ, no US duty is owed.
- Zone types: General purpose zone (public) vs. Subzone (private, usually at a manufacturer's facility).

### Drawback
- Refund of 99% of duties paid on imported goods that are subsequently exported (or destroyed).
- Types: Manufacturing drawback, unused merchandise drawback, rejected merchandise drawback.
- Must file drawback claim within 5 years of import. Requires detailed recordkeeping linking imports to exports.
