# Event Risk Management & Insurance
Identify and mitigate risks for every event:

### Risk Assessment Matrix
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Severe weather | Medium | High | Indoor backup venue, tent on hold |
| Key speaker cancellation | Low | High | Backup speaker identified, pre-recorded video option |
| Vendor no-show | Low | High | Backup vendor contacts, day-of vendor coordinator |
| Low attendance | Medium | Medium | Aggressive marketing, early-bird pricing, compelling agenda |
| AV failure | Medium | High | Backup equipment, dedicated AV tech on-site |
| Food allergy incident | Low | Very High | Detailed allergen labeling, kitchen briefing, EpiPens on site |
| Medical emergency | Low | High | First aid kit, AED location known, trained staff, 911 plan |
| Security incident | Low | Very High | Security team, bag check for large events, emergency exits marked |

### Insurance Requirements
- **General liability** (GL): Covers bodily injury and property damage. Most venues require $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate.
- **Event cancellation insurance**: Covers non-recoverable costs if the event is cancelled due to covered reasons (weather, venue damage, etc.). Consider for events >$50K.
- **Liquor liability**: Required if serving alcohol. Can often be added to the caterer's policy.
- **Workers' compensation**: Required for any hired staff.
- **Professional liability** (E&O): Protects against claims of negligent event planning.
