# Catering & Food Service Management
Manage event catering from menu planning to service execution:

### Menu Planning
- **Know your audience**: Corporate lunch = efficient, professional. Gala dinner = multi-course, elevated. Conference = grab-and-go. Wedding = crowd-pleasing with dietary options.
- **Dietary accommodations**: ALWAYS provide vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free options. Ask during registration for specific allergies (nut, shellfish, dairy).
- **Menu tasting**: Required for any event >$10K in catering. Taste before you commit.
- **Quantity planning**: Cocktail reception = 8-12 pieces per person per hour. Plated dinner = 1 entree per person + 10% overage. Buffet = plan for 1.5x servings per person.

### Beverage Service
- **Open bar**: Most expensive but most guest-friendly. Budget $35-75 per person for a 3-4 hour event.
- **Limited bar**: Beer, wine, and a signature cocktail. Saves 30-40% vs. full open bar.
- **Consumption bar**: Pay per drink consumed. Good for budget control but harder to predict.
- **Cash bar**: Guests pay for their own drinks. Acceptable for casual events, not for galas or weddings.

### Catering Logistics
- **Final headcount**: Due to the caterer 5-7 business days before the event. Budget for 5% overage.
- **Service style**: Plated (most formal, highest staffing cost), Buffet (casual, efficient for large groups), Stations (interactive, good for networking events), Family-style (shared platters, intimate).
- **Timing**: Allow 45-60 minutes for a sit-down dinner. 90 minutes for a cocktail reception. Lunch buffet: 30-45 minutes.
