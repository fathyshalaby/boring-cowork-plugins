# Audio/Visual & Production Management
Manage AV and production elements for a polished, professional event:

### AV Requirements by Event Type
| Event Type | AV Needs |
|-----------|---------|
| Meeting/Boardroom | Projector/screen, confidence monitor, conference phone |
| Conference/Seminar | PA system, wireless mics (podium + lavalier), projector + screens, podium |
| Gala/Dinner | Uplighting, intelligent lighting, PA for DJ/band, wireless mic for toasts |
| Trade Show/Expo | Booth power, general PA for announcements, breakout room AV |
| Hybrid/Virtual | All above + HD cameras, streaming platform, encoder, internet bandwidth |

### Common AV Mistakes to Avoid
- **Not testing**: Sound check is mandatory. Test EVERY mic, every input, every slide transition BEFORE attendees arrive.
- **Insufficient mics**: If there's Q&A, have handheld mics for the audience. "Can you hear me from the back?" is amateur.
- **Bad lighting on speakers**: Dedicated stage wash lights. No one wants to watch a silhouette.
- **No backup**: Have spare batteries, backup laptop with presentation loaded, spare cables. Murphy's Law applies double to AV.
- **Wi-Fi overload**: If you're livestreaming, get a dedicated hardwired internet line. Don't rely on venue Wi-Fi.

### Livestream/Hybrid Setup
- Platform: Zoom Webinar, YouTube Live, Vimeo, or dedicated platform like Hopin.
- Camera: Minimum 1 HD camera on the speaker. Ideally 2 cameras (wide + close-up) with a switcher.
- Audio: Board feed from the PA mixer to the stream—do NOT use the camera's built-in mic.
- Moderator: Dedicated person managing virtual attendees, Q&A, chat, and technical issues.
