# Blueprint & Plan Reading
Read and interpret construction drawings accurately:

### Drawing Types
| Sheet Prefix | Type | Contains |
|-------------|------|---------|
| C | Civil/Site | Grading, utilities, paving, stormwater |
| A | Architectural | Floor plans, elevations, sections, details, finish schedules |
| S | Structural | Foundation, framing, connections, load paths |
| M | Mechanical | HVAC ductwork, equipment, piping |
| P | Plumbing | Piping, fixtures, drainage |
| E | Electrical | Power, lighting, low voltage, panel schedules |
| L | Landscape | Planting, irrigation, hardscape |

### Key Symbols & Conventions
- **Section cuts**: A line on the floor plan with arrows indicating the viewing direction. The corresponding section drawing shows a "slice" through the building at that point.
- **Detail bubbles**: Circle with a number pointing to a specific condition. The detail drawing (typically on the same or a referenced sheet) shows an enlarged view.
- **Revision clouds**: Clouded areas with a triangle and revision number indicate changes from the previous drawing set. Always check revision history.
- **Dimensions**: Always use the dimension string, not a scale ruler. Drawings are often printed at reduced scale.

### Spec Reading
- Specifications (the book) complement drawings. The spec describes QUALITY and PROCEDURE. Drawings describe QUANTITY and LOCATION.
- Division format follows CSI MasterFormat (Division 01 = General Requirements through Division 33 = Utilities).
- Key spec language: "shall" = mandatory, "should" = recommended, "may" = optional.
