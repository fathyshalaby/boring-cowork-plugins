# Quality Control & Inspections
Implement a comprehensive quality control program to eliminate rework:

### QC Inspection Points
| Phase | Inspection Items | Before Covering |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Foundation | Rebar placement, form dimensions, anchor bolt locations | Before concrete pour |
| Framing | Stud spacing, header sizes, sheathing nailing pattern, hold-downs | Before insulation/drywall |
| Rough MEP | Pipe slopes, duct sizing, wire gauge, box locations per plans | Before insulation/drywall |
| Insulation | R-value, coverage gaps, vapor barrier placement | Before drywall |
| Drywall | Finish level (Level 4/5), seam quality, corner bead | Before paint |
| Exterior | Flashing sequence (WRB → window flashing → siding), caulk joints | Before final landscaping |
| Final | Punch list, fixture operation, finishes, cleanup | Before owner walkthrough |

### Preventing Rework
- **Pre-installation meetings**: Meet with each trade BEFORE they start. Walk the plans, clarify details, and identify potential conflicts.
- **First-piece inspection**: Inspect the first unit of repetitive work (first window installed, first room of tile, etc.) before the trade continues. Catching errors early prevents repeating them 100 times.
- **RFIs (Requests for Information)**: When the plans are unclear or conflicting, submit an RFI to the architect. NEVER assume. Document the response.
- **Mock-ups**: For finish work (stone veneer, specialty ceilings, custom millwork), require a mock-up panel for architect approval before proceeding.

### Punch List Management
- Walk each area with the subcontractor before the owner's walkthrough.
- Use a digital punch list tool (Procore, PlanGrid, or even a shared spreadsheet with photos).
- Assign each item to a responsible sub with a completion deadline.
- Re-inspect completed items before calling them done.
