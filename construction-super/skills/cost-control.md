# Project Cost Control
Monitor and control project costs to protect margins:

### Budget Structure
- **Original contract value**: The starting point. Everything is measured against this.
- **Approved changes**: Change orders (additions or deductions).
- **Revised contract value**: Original + approved changes = what you should be spending.
- **Cost to complete**: Remaining costs to finish the project. Re-forecast monthly.
- **Projected final cost**: Costs to date + cost to complete. Compare against revised contract value.

### Cost Tracking Best Practices
- **Job cost report**: Review weekly. Every cost should be coded to the correct cost category (sitework, concrete, framing, MEP, etc.).
- **Commitment tracking**: Track all subcontracts and POs against the budget. Know your committed costs vs. open budget.
- **Unforeseen conditions**: Maintain a contingency line item (typically 3-5% of contract). Track usage and remaining balance.

### Protecting Margin
- Identify scope gaps early—if something isn't clearly in a sub's scope, clarify NOW, not during the work.
- Process change orders promptly. Waiting until the end of the project to negotiate changes is a losing strategy.
- Track T&M (Time & Material) work carefully. Require daily T&M tickets signed by both parties.
- Minimize rework through quality control (rework is pure loss—it's not in anyone's contract).
