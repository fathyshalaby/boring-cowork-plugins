# Project Closeout & Turnover
Execute a thorough project closeout to protect your reputation and avoid callbacks:

### Closeout Checklist
**Inspections & Approvals**:
- [ ] All rough and final inspections passed (building, fire, health department, AHJ)
- [ ] Certificate of Occupancy (CO) or Temporary CO (TCO) issued
- [ ] Fire alarm system acceptance test completed
- [ ] Elevator inspection certificate posted
- [ ] Commissioning of HVAC/BAS systems complete

**Documentation**:
- [ ] As-built drawings (contractor redlines incorporated by architect)
- [ ] O&M manuals for all equipment (HVAC, elevator, fire alarm, BAS, etc.)
- [ ] Warranty letters from all subcontractors and manufacturers
- [ ] Final lien waivers from all subs and suppliers
- [ ] Attic stock delivered (extra flooring, paint, ceiling tiles per spec)
- [ ] Keys, access cards, and system passwords turned over

**Financial**:
- [ ] Final change order log reconciled
- [ ] Final pay application submitted (retain retainage release for separate billing)
- [ ] All subcontractor final payments processed
- [ ] Project cost report finalized

**Owner Turnover**:
- [ ] Owner training: Walk the owner's maintenance staff through all building systems
- [ ] Emergency procedures manual provided
- [ ] Punch list 100% complete and signed off by owner/architect
- [ ] Warranty start dates documented (typically CO date or substantial completion date)

### Warranty Period Management
- Standard construction warranty: 1 year from substantial completion.
- Respond to warranty callbacks within 48 hours.
- Track warranty items by trade. If a sub isn't responding, enforce the subcontract warranty clause.
