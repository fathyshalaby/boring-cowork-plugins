# Subcontractor Coordination & Management
Manage subcontractors effectively to keep the project on schedule and on budget:

### Subcontractor Coordination
- **Pre-construction meeting**: Before each trade mobilizes, hold a meeting covering: scope, schedule, safety requirements, laydown areas, access, and coordination with other trades.
- **Daily coordination**: Walk the site with foremen each morning to review the day's plan, identify access issues, and resolve conflicts.
- **Trade coordination sequence**: Foundation → Underground utilities → Slab → Framing → Roof → MEP rough-in → Insulation → Drywall → MEP trim → Finishes → Punch → Closeout.
- **MEP coordination**: Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing trades must coordinate their routing to avoid conflicts. In tight spaces, require a coordination drawing or BIM clash detection.

### Managing Performance Issues
- **Undermanning**: If a sub isn't providing enough workers to maintain the schedule, send a written "cure notice" referencing the contract manpower requirements.
- **Quality deficiencies**: Document with photos, issue a non-conformance report (NCR), and require a corrective action plan.
- **Backcharges**: If a sub damages another trade's work or fails to clean up, document the damage and costs, then issue a formal backcharge notice per the subcontract terms.
- **Payment**: Pay subs promptly upon receiving owner payment. Slow payment destroys relationships and leads to liens.

### Subcontract Documentation
- Ensure every sub has: signed subcontract, current COI (insurance certificate), W-9, safety program, and lien waivers with each pay application.
