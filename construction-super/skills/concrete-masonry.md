# Concrete & Masonry Fundamentals
Understand concrete and masonry work for effective supervision:

### Concrete Basics
- **Mix design**: Specified by PSI (compressive strength). Typical residential: 3,000 PSI. Commercial: 4,000-6,000 PSI. High-rise columns: 8,000-12,000+ PSI.
- **Slump**: Measures workability. Typical structural: 4-6 inches. Self-consolidating (SCC): 18-24 inches. Too much slump = too much water = weaker concrete.
- **Admixtures**: Water reducers, air entrainment (for freeze-thaw resistance), accelerators (cold weather), retarders (hot weather).
- **Curing**: Critical for strength development. Keep concrete moist and above 50°F for at least 7 days. Methods: wet burlap, curing compound, plastic sheeting.

### Pre-Pour Checklist
- [ ] Forms set to correct dimensions and elevation (survey verified)
- [ ] Rebar placed per structural drawings (size, spacing, cover, lap splices)
- [ ] Rebar tied and supported on chairs/bolsters
- [ ] Anchor bolts and embeds placed per plans
- [ ] Sleeves and blockouts for MEP penetrations installed
- [ ] Forms oiled/released
- [ ] Concrete truck access route clear
- [ ] Inspector notified (if required inspection)
- [ ] Adequate vibration equipment on site
- [ ] Finishing crew and tools ready

### Cold/Hot Weather Concrete
- **Cold weather (<40°F)**: Heat mixing water, use accelerator, protect placed concrete with insulated blankets/heated enclosures. Never let fresh concrete freeze.
- **Hot weather (>90°F)**: Use ice in mix water, retarder admixture, pour early morning, apply curing compound immediately after finishing.
