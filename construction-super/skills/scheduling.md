# Construction Scheduling & CPM
Master project scheduling using Critical Path Method and modern scheduling tools:

### Critical Path Method (CPM)
- The **critical path** is the longest sequence of dependent activities that determines the minimum project duration. Any delay on the critical path delays the entire project.
- **Float/Slack**: Non-critical activities have float—the amount of time they can be delayed without affecting the project end date. Zero-float activities ARE the critical path.
- **Pull planning**: Work backward from the completion date. Identify what must happen last, then what must happen before that, etc. This reveals the critical path naturally.

### Scheduling Best Practices
1. **3-Week Look-Ahead**: Maintain a rolling 3-week detailed schedule showing daily activities by trade. Update weekly.
2. **Milestone tracking**: Key milestones include: Permit issuance, foundation complete, framing complete, dry-in (roof + windows), rough inspections (MEP), drywall complete, final inspections, CO/TCO, owner turnover.
3. **Weather days**: Build weather contingency into the schedule (typically 1 day/week during rainy seasons). Track actual weather delays for delay claims.
4. **Trade stacking**: Avoid scheduling too many trades in the same area simultaneously. Congestion kills productivity.
5. **Submittal and procurement lead times**: Long-lead items (structural steel, custom windows, electrical switchgear, elevators) can be 8-20+ weeks. Order immediately after award.

### Schedule Compression Techniques
- **Crashing**: Adding resources (overtime, additional crews) to critical path activities. Increases cost.
- **Fast-tracking**: Overlapping activities that would normally be sequential (e.g., starting rough framing before foundation is 100% complete). Increases risk.
- **Re-sequencing**: Look for activities that can be reordered without violating logic.
