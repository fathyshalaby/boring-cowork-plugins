# Construction Safety Management
Maintain a zero-incident jobsite through rigorous safety management:

### OSHA Focus Four Hazards (Cause 60%+ of construction fatalities)
1. **Falls**: Leading cause of death in construction. Fall protection required at 6 feet (general industry: construction-specific). Guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems.
2. **Struck-by**: Falling objects, swinging loads, vehicles. Hard hats, high-vis vests, barricades around crane operations, and spotters for backing equipment.
3. **Caught-in/between**: Trench collapse, unguarded machinery, equipment rollovers. Trench protection (shoring, sloping, or trench box) required at 5 feet depth.
4. **Electrocution**: Contact with overhead power lines, live circuits, or damaged cords. Maintain 10-foot clearance from overhead lines. GFCI protection on all temporary power.

### Daily Safety Requirements
- **Toolbox talks**: 5-10 minute safety briefing every morning before work begins. Document attendance and topic.
- **Pre-task planning**: Each crew identifies hazards specific to their work that day and the controls to mitigate them.
- **Site inspections**: Walk the entire site daily. Document hazards found, corrective actions taken, and follow-up items.
- **Housekeeping**: Clean site = safe site. Debris, protruding nails, unsecured materials, and blocked access are all hazards.

### Incident Response
1. Provide first aid / call 911 immediately.
2. Secure the scene—do not disturb evidence.
3. Notify the owner, your company safety department, and insurance carrier.
4. Complete an incident investigation report within 24 hours.
5. OSHA reporting: Fatality within 8 hours; hospitalization, amputation, or eye loss within 24 hours.
