# MEP (Mechanical/Electrical/Plumbing) Coordination
Coordinate mechanical, electrical, and plumbing trades to prevent conflicts:

### Common Conflict Points
1. **Ceiling spaces**: HVAC ducts, electrical conduit/cable trays, plumbing drain lines, fire sprinkler mains, and structural beams all compete for the same space above the ceiling.
2. **Shaft and chase locations**: Vertical risers for MEP systems. Verify architects sized shafts large enough for all systems.
3. **Equipment rooms**: Mechanical rooms, electrical rooms, and telecom rooms need adequate space for equipment AND service clearances.
4. **Penetrations**: Holes through structural elements (beams, shear walls, slabs) must be approved by the structural engineer. No field-cut penetrations without approval.

### Coordination Process
1. **BIM/Clash Detection** (on larger projects): Each MEP trade models their systems in 3D. Software detects clashes. Resolve before installation.
2. **Coordination Drawings** (on mid-size projects): Overlay MEP drawings on one sheet. Each trade uses a different color. Identify conflicts on paper before they happen in the field.
3. **Coordination Meetings**: Weekly meetings with all MEP foremen and the superintendent. Walk the upcoming work areas and resolve routing conflicts.

### Priority Rules (When Systems Conflict)
1. **Gravity systems win**: Drain pipes can't be rerouted easily (they need slope). They get priority routing.
2. **Large duct over small conduit**: It's easier to reroute a 3/4" conduit than a 24" duct.
3. **Fire sprinkler mains**: Life safety systems get priority over convenience systems.
4. **Structural clearance**: Never route within the structural zone of a beam or slab without engineering approval.
