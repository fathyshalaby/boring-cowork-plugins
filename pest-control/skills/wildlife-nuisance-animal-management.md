# Wildlife & Nuisance Animal Management
Handle wildlife conflicts legally and humanely:

### Common Nuisance Wildlife
| Animal | Common Issues | Legal Status |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Raccoons | Attic intrusion, trash raiding, chimney nesting | Varies by state — trapping may require permit |
| Squirrels | Attic intrusion, gnawing on wires/wood | Generally unprotected but check state regs |
| Bats | Roosting in attics/walls | **Federally protected** — cannot harm. Exclusion only. |
| Birds (pigeons, starlings, sparrows) | Nesting, droppings, damage | Most birds protected (Migratory Bird Treaty Act). Pigeons, starlings, English sparrows generally unprotected. |
| Opossums | Denning under structures | Generally unprotected |
| Skunks | Denning, odor, digging | Varies — potential rabies vector species (special handling) |

### Bat Exclusion (Critical)
- **NEVER** trap, poison, or kill bats. Protected under federal and state law.
- Exclusion-only approach: Install one-way excluder devices at entry points. Bats leave at dusk and cannot re-enter.
- Timing: Exclude in early spring (before pups are born) or late fall (after pups are flying). **NEVER** during June-August in most regions (flightless pups would be trapped inside).
- Seal all secondary entry points before installing excluders. Bats can enter ⅜" gaps.

### Legal Considerations
- Know your state's wildlife control regulations. Many require a Nuisance Wildlife Control Operator (NWCO) license.
- Rabies vector species (raccoons, skunks, bats, foxes) may have special handling and reporting requirements.
- Document all wildlife work with photos, species ID, and methods used.
