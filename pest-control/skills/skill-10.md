# Client Communication & Service Reports
Communicate effectively with clients and document all services:

### Service Report Essentials
Every service visit must be documented with:
- Date and time of service
- Technician name and certification number
- Target pests and evidence found
- Treatment performed: specific products, EPA reg. numbers, application rates, locations treated
- Non-chemical actions: exclusion work, sanitation recommendations, monitor placement
- Recommendations for the client (sanitation, repairs, moisture correction)
- Follow-up schedule

### Client Communication Best Practices
- **Set expectations**: "You may see increased activity for 1-2 weeks after treatment as pests contact the product. This is normal."
- **Explain what you did**: Clients want to know what they're paying for. Walk them through the service.
- **Educate on prevention**: 80% of pest control is prevention. Empower clients to reduce pest-conducive conditions.
- **Document recommendations**: Put sanitation and exclusion recommendations in writing. If the client doesn't comply, it's documented.

### Selling Additional Services
- Identify upsell opportunities during routine service: "I noticed termite mud tubes on the south foundation wall. I'd recommend a termite inspection."
- Seasonal programs: Mosquito/tick reduction (spring-fall), rodent exclusion (fall), stored product pest prevention (year-round).
- Recurring service agreements: Monthly, bi-monthly, or quarterly service plans provide steady revenue and better pest management outcomes.
- Annual termite renewal/warranty programs.
