# Bed Bug Management
Manage bed bug infestations with proven protocols:

### Inspection Protocol
1. Start with the bed: mattress seams, tufts, and folds. Box spring (most common harborage—inspect all seams, stapled fabric, corners).
2. Headboard: remove and inspect back side, screw holes, frame joints.
3. Bed frame: joints, casters, screw holes, any crevice.
4. Nightstands: drawers, screw holes, back panel, inside and out.
5. Expand outward: baseboards, electrical outlets/switches, picture frames, curtain rods, upholstered furniture.
6. Use a flashlight and magnification. Look for: live bugs, cast skins, eggs (1mm, white, sticky), fecal spots (dark stains).

### Treatment Approaches
| Method | Effectiveness | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| Chemical (liquid/dust/aerosol) | High (if thorough) | Cost-effective, long residual | Resistance concerns, prep required |
| Heat treatment (140°F+) | Very high | Kills all stages including eggs in one treatment | Expensive, equipment-intensive |
| Cryonite (freezing) | Moderate | Instant knockdown, no residue | No residual, eggs may survive |
| Mattress encasements | Preventive | Traps bugs in mattress/box spring | Doesn't kill bugs elsewhere |
| Combination | Highest | Addresses resistance and harborage | Most expensive |

### Client Preparation
- Remove clutter from around the bed and furniture.
- Launder all bedding and clothing on HIGH heat (>120°F dryer for 30 min).
- Do NOT move items to other rooms (spreads the infestation).
- Vacuum thoroughly (dispose of bag immediately in sealed container).
- Set expectations: 2-3 treatments typically needed, spaced 10-14 days apart.
