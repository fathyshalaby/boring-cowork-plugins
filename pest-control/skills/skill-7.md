# Ant Management
Identify and control ant species with targeted approaches:

### Common Structural Ants
| Species | Size/Color | Nesting | Preferred Bait |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Odorous house ant | ⅛", dark brown, coconut odor when crushed | Wall voids, under objects indoors | Sweet liquid bait |
| Carpenter ant | ¼-½", black, large | Moisture-damaged wood, trees | Protein bait, liquid bait |
| Argentine ant | ⅛", light brown | Soil, under objects, massive colonies | Sweet liquid bait |
| Pavement ant | ⅛", dark brown, striations on head | Under pavement, slabs, walls | Sweet and protein baits |
| Fire ant (imported) | ⅛-¼", reddish | Soil mounds, aggressive stinging | Broadcast bait, mound drench |
| Pharaoh ant | 1/16", yellow-brown | Warm interior spaces, hospitals | Sweet protein bait ONLY (sprays cause budding) |

### Critical Rule: Pharaoh Ants
- **NEVER** spray Pharaoh ants. Repellent treatments cause colonies to split ("budding"), spreading the infestation.
- Use non-repellent baits exclusively. Place near trails and suspected nest sites.

### Treatment Approach
1. **Identify the species** — different species require different strategies.
2. **Find the nest(s)** — follow trails back to their source.
3. **Bait** — use species-appropriate bait. Place in the trail line. Don't contaminate with sprays.
4. **Exclusion** — seal entry points after the colony is eliminated.
5. **Exterior perimeter** — non-repellent liquid treatment along the foundation if appropriate.
