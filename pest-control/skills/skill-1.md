# Pest Identification & Biology
Accurately identify pest species and understand their biology for effective control:

### Common Structural Pests
| Pest | Key ID Features | Behavior | Control Challenge |
|------|----------------|----------|------------------|
| German cockroach | ½-⅝", tan, 2 dark stripes on pronotum | Nocturnal, clusters in warm/moist areas near food | Rapid reproduction (30-40 eggs/capsule), bait aversion |
| American cockroach | 1.5-2", reddish-brown, figure-8 on pronotum | Sewer/basement dweller, can fly | Large, mobile, often from external sources |
| Bed bug | ¼", flat oval, reddish-brown | Nocturnal blood feeder, hides in crevices near beds | Pesticide resistance, cryptic hiding, no bait available |
| House mouse | 2-3" body, gray, large ears | Curious, small home range (10-30 ft), gnaws | Small entry points (¼"), rapid breeding |
| Norway rat | 7-10" body, brown, blunt nose | Cautious (neophobic), burrows, larger range | Neophobia makes baiting slow, strong gnawing ability |
| Subterranean termite | Workers: soft, white, ⅛" | Colonies in soil, mud tubes to wood | Hidden damage, colonies of 100K-1M+ |

### Identification Resources
- University extension pest ID guides.
- Digital: PestID.com, iNaturalist, BugGuide.net.
- Magnification: 10-20x hand lens for field ID, stereomicroscope for lab ID.
- When in doubt, **collect a specimen** and submit to your state extension entomologist for confirmation.
