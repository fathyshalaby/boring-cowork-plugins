# Rodent Management
Control rodent populations effectively:

### Inspection Fundamentals
- **Droppings**: Mouse droppings ¼" (rice-shaped). Rat droppings ¾" (capsule-shaped). Fresh = dark and moist. Old = gray and crumbly.
- **Gnaw marks**: Mice gnaw small, clean holes. Rats gnaw larger, rougher openings.
- **Rub marks/Grease trails**: Dark, oily marks along walls and pipes from repeated travel paths.
- **Entry points**: Mice: ¼" gap (dime-sized). Rats: ½" gap (quarter-sized). Check utility penetrations, door gaps, foundation cracks, roof joints.
- **Nesting**: Shredded paper, fabric, insulation in hidden spaces.

### Exclusion (Most Important Step)
- Seal all entry points with appropriate material: copper mesh + caulk for small gaps, sheet metal for larger openings, hardware cloth (¼" mesh) for vents.
- Door sweeps on all exterior doors (max ¼" gap).
- Trim vegetation 2-3 feet from the building.
- Eliminate outdoor harborage: debris piles, ground cover against foundation.

### Control Methods
| Method | Indoor/Outdoor | Best For |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| Snap traps | Both | Mice and rats. Quick kill, can recover for counting. |
| Glue boards | Indoor only | Mice. Not effective for rats. |
| Bait stations (tamper-resistant) | Exterior perimeter | Ongoing population reduction. |
| Tracking powder | Structural voids | Areas where traps/bait can't reach. |

### Bait Station Placement
- Exterior: Every 50-100 feet around the building perimeter, at entry points, and along known travel paths.
- Interior: Avoid if possible (dead rodent odor risk). Use traps instead.
- Check and service every 30 days (minimum). Document bait consumption.
