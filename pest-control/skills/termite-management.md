# Termite Management
Detect and control termite infestations:

### Termite Types
| Type | Distribution | Colony | Treatment |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| Subterranean (Eastern, Formosan) | Nationwide (Formosan: Southeast) | Soil-based, 100K-millions | Liquid barrier, bait systems |
| Drywood | Southern coastal states | In-wood, <5,000 typically | Localized treatment or fumigation |
| Dampwood | Pacific states | In decayed/wet wood | Moisture correction, localized treatment |

### Inspection Indicators
- **Mud tubes**: Pencil-width tubes on foundations, walls, or piers. Break and check for live termites.
- **Swarmers**: Winged reproductives, often mistaken for flying ants. Equal-length wings (ants: unequal). Straight antennae (ants: elbowed).
- **Frass**: Drywood termite pellets (6-sided, sand-grain sized) kicked out of exit holes.
- **Wood damage**: Subterranean termites eat along the grain, leaving a layered/honeycomb pattern. Probe with a screwdriver.
- **Moisture**: Termites need moisture. Check for leaks, poor drainage, wood-soil contact.

### Treatment Options
- **Liquid soil treatment**: Trench and treat around foundation with non-repellent termiticide (Termidor, Altriset). Creates a continuous treated zone.
- **Bait systems**: Monitor stations around perimeter. When termites are detected, install bait matrix. Colony elimination approach.
- **Localized treatment (drywood)**: Inject termiticide foam or dust into galleries. For isolated infestations.
- **Fumigation**: Tent the entire structure and introduce Vikane gas. For widespread drywood infestations. Requires evacuation.
