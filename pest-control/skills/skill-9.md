# Regulatory Compliance & Licensing
Maintain compliance with all pest control regulations:

### Licensing Requirements
- **Certified/Licensed applicator**: Required in all states to apply pesticides commercially. Certification categories vary by state:
  - Category 7A/GHP: General household pests (structural)
  - Category 7B: Termites/wood-destroying organisms
  - Category 7F: Fumigation
  - Category 8: Public health (mosquitoes)
- **Continuing education**: Most states require 4-10 CE hours per renewal period (1-3 years).
- **Business license**: Separate from individual certification. Company must be licensed, bonded, and insured.

### Recordkeeping Requirements
- **Pesticide application records**: Date, location, target pest, product name, EPA reg. #, amount used, application method, applicator name/cert #.
- **Retention**: Minimum 3 years (varies by state—some require 7+).
- **WDO (Wood-Destroying Organism) reports**: Required for real estate transactions in most states. Must be filed with the state.

### Safety Requirements
- **SDS (Safety Data Sheets)**: Maintain for every product used. Accessible to all employees.
- **Worker Protection Standard (WPS)**: Applies to agricultural applications. Notification, restricted entry intervals.
- **DOT Hazmat**: If transporting fumigants or large quantities, DOT placarding and Hazmat training may apply.
- **Vehicle requirements**: Pest control vehicles must meet state labeling requirements and carry SDS sheets for all products on board.
