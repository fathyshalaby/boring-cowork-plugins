# Green Cleaning & Sustainability
Implement sustainable cleaning practices that meet environmental certifications:

### Green Cleaning Standards
- **Green Seal GS-37**: Certification for cleaning service operations. Covers chemical selection, paper products, equipment, and training.
- **LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design)**: Green cleaning is required for LEED-certified buildings. Credits for: green cleaning policy, green products, training, and high-performance equipment.
- **EPA Safer Choice**: Products with this label meet EPA's criteria for safer ingredients.

### Green Cleaning Practices
1. **Products**: Use third-party certified green cleaning products (Green Seal, EPA Safer Choice, UL ECOLOGO).
2. **Paper products**: Recycled content toilet paper, paper towels, and trash liners.
3. **Equipment**: Use HEPA-filtered vacuums, microfiber cloths (reduce chemical use by 80%), and auto-scrubbers with chemical metering.
4. **Chemical dilution control**: Closed-loop dispensing systems ensure correct dilution every time, reducing waste and chemical overuse.
5. **Training**: All staff trained on green cleaning principles and sustainable practices.

### Cost-Benefit
- Green cleaning products can cost 5-15% more than conventional.
- However, microfiber systems reduce chemical consumption significantly, often offsetting the premium.
- Marketing value: Many commercial tenants prefer or require green cleaning for their ESG reporting.
