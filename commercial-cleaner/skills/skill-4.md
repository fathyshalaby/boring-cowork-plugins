# Quality Assurance & Inspection
Implement a QA program that ensures consistent cleaning quality:

### Inspection Process
- Conduct formal inspections at least monthly for each facility.
- Use a standardized checklist covering every area: lobbies, offices, restrooms, break rooms, stairwells, exterior areas.
- Score each area on a 1-5 scale or pass/fail for each item.
- Calculate an overall facility score. Target: 90%+ consistently.

### Inspection Focus Areas
| Area | Critical Items | Common Failures |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| Restrooms | Disinfection, supplies stocked, odor-free | Missed toilet bases, empty dispensers |
| Lobbies/Common | Floors gleaming, furniture dust-free, glass clean | Fingerprints on glass, dusty ledges |
| Offices | Trash emptied, desks/surfaces wiped (if clear), vacuum tracks visible | Missed trash cans, cobwebs in corners |
| Break rooms | Counters clean, microwave wiped, trash empty | Sticky floors, full trash at day end |
| Stairwells | Dust-free handrails, clean treads, no debris | Cobwebs in corners, dirty handrails |

### Quality Metrics
- **Customer satisfaction surveys**: Quarterly email survey to building contacts. Track trends.
- **Complaint response time**: Target: acknowledge within 2 hours, resolve within 24 hours.
- **Re-clean rate**: Track how often areas need to be re-cleaned. Target: <5%.
- **Inspection scores**: Trend over time by facility. Identify underperforming buildings for intervention.
