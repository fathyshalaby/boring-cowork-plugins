# Staffing & Shift Management
Manage cleaning staff effectively for consistent facility coverage:

### Staffing Calculations
- **Production rate**: Measured in cleanable square feet per hour. Industry benchmarks:
  - Standard office cleaning: 3,500-5,000 SF/hour
  - Healthcare/medical: 2,000-3,000 SF/hour
  - Industrial/warehouse: 6,000-10,000 SF/hour
- **Required hours**: Total cleanable SF ÷ Production rate = Cleaning hours needed per shift.
- **Staffing factor**: Add 15-20% for breaks, absenteeism, and non-productive time.

### Shift Organization
- Assign specific areas to each cleaner with a clear task list.
- **Zone cleaning**: Each cleaner is responsible for a defined zone and performs all tasks within it.
- **Team cleaning**: Specialists (restroom cleaner, vacuumer, trash collector, detail cleaner) rotate through the building. More efficient for large buildings.

### Employee Training Essentials
- **Day 1**: Safety orientation, chemical handling, SDS location, PPE requirements, emergency procedures.
- **Week 1**: Hands-on training in each area type (restrooms, offices, floors) with a trainer.
- **Ongoing**: Monthly safety refreshers, quarterly skill assessments, annual OSHA compliance training (bloodborne pathogens, chemical safety, fall protection if applicable).
- **Documentation**: Maintain training records for every employee.
