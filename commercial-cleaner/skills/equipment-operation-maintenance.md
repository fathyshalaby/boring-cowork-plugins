# Equipment Operation & Maintenance
Operate and maintain commercial cleaning equipment properly:

### Equipment Types
| Equipment | Use | Key Maintenance |
|-----------|-----|----------------|
| Auto-scrubber (walk-behind/ride-on) | Large floor cleaning | Empty/rinse tanks daily, replace pads/brushes regularly, check squeegee blades |
| Burnisher (UHS, 1500-2500 RPM) | High-gloss floor finish | Check pad centering, clean filter bag, inspect cord |
| Floor machine (175 RPM) | Scrubbing, bonnet cleaning, stripping | Change pads per task, check brush plate, inspect cord |
| Backpack vacuum | Carpet and hard floor vacuuming | Change/empty bag at 1/2 full, check HEPA filter monthly |
| Carpet extractor | Deep carpet cleaning | Rinse and drain tanks after each use, clean jets monthly |
| Pressure washer | Exterior cleaning, heavy industrial | Check hoses, nozzles, oil level |

### Preventive Maintenance
- Walk-around inspection before each use: power cord, safety features, pads/brushes, solution levels.
- Clean equipment after each shift. Never store dirty equipment.
- Schedule PM per manufacturer recommendations (typically monthly for daily-use equipment).
- Track equipment hours and replacement costs. Know when repair vs. replace is the right decision.

### Safety
- Never operate electrical equipment on wet floors (risk of electrocution) unless the equipment is designed for wet use.
- Cord management: Never run cords across doorways or high-traffic areas without a cord cover.
- Store equipment in a secure, ventilated area. Never block fire exits.
