# Restroom Cleaning & Sanitation
Deliver consistently clean, hygienic restrooms—the #1 factor in client satisfaction:

### Daily Restroom Procedure
1. **Stock and supply**: Refill paper towels, toilet paper, soap dispensers, and seat covers.
2. **Mirrors and fixtures**: Clean mirrors with glass cleaner. Wipe chrome fixtures to a shine.
3. **Countertops and sinks**: Clean with disinfectant. Pay attention to faucet bases and soap residue.
4. **Toilets and urinals**: Apply bowl cleaner inside bowl, clean exterior with disinfectant (including base, especially behind toilet). Flush.
5. **Partitions**: Wipe partition surfaces, door handles, and locks with disinfectant.
6. **Touch points**: Disinfect all high-touch surfaces: door handles, light switches, flush handles, dispensers.
7. **Trash**: Empty trash cans, replace liners.
8. **Floors**: Sweep or dust mop, then mop with disinfectant. Start at the far end, work toward the exit.

### Common Issues & Solutions
- **Odor**: Usually means urine buildup around toilet bases and grout. Use an enzyme-based cleaner on grout and around the base weekly.
- **Hard water buildup**: Use an acid-based cleaner on mineral deposits. Do NOT use acid cleaners on marble or natural stone surfaces.
- **Grout discoloration**: Deep scrub with a stiff brush and alkaline cleaner. May need periodic re-sealing.
- **Drain odor**: Pour enzyme drain maintainer down drains weekly to prevent biofilm buildup.
