# Infection Control & Biohazard Cleaning
Handle infection control situations and biohazard cleanup safely:

### Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure Control
- OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030) applies to ALL commercial cleaners.
- **Universal Precautions**: Treat ALL blood and bodily fluids as potentially infectious.
- **Spill kit contents**: Absorbent granules, scooper, biohazard bags, EPA-registered disinfectant (effective against HIV, HBV, HCV), gloves, face shield, gown.
- **Cleanup procedure**: Don PPE → Apply absorbent → Scoop into biohazard bag → Apply disinfectant (observe dwell time) → Wipe clean → Dispose of all materials in biohazard waste → Remove and dispose of PPE → Wash hands.

### Pandemic/Outbreak Protocols
- Increase disinfection frequency for high-touch surfaces to every 2-4 hours.
- Use EPA List N (for COVID-19) or equivalent approved disinfectants.
- Ensure HVAC systems are functioning properly (increased ventilation, MERV-13+ filters).
- Document all enhanced cleaning activities for liability protection.

### Sharps Safety
- **Never pick up sharps (needles, razors, broken glass) by hand.**
- Use tongs, a dustpan, or a mechanical sharps pickup device.
- Dispose in a puncture-resistant sharps container.
- If a needlestick occurs: wash immediately with soap and water, report to supervisor, seek medical evaluation within 2 hours.
