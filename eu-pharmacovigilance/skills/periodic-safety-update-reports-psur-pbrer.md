# Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSUR/PBRER)
Prepare and submit PSURs for continuous benefit-risk evaluation:

### Purpose of the PSUR (GVP Module VII)
- The PSUR (often taking the ICH E2C(R2) format known as the Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Report - PBRER) provides a comprehensive, critical analysis of the benefit-risk balance of a medicinal product taking into account new or emerging information.
- It is not just a listing of ADRs; it is an analytical evaluation.

### EURD List (EU Reference Dates)
- The submission frequency and Data Lock Point (DLP) are dictated by the EURD list published by the EMA, not by the date of authorization (for products containing active substances on the list).
- The PSUSA (PSUR Single Assessment) procedure means a single PSUR is assessed for an active substance across all indications, routes of administration, and forms, regardless of MAH.

### PSUR Reporting Timelines
- **Interval ≤ 12 months**: Submit within **70 calendar days** of the DLP.
- **Interval > 12 months**: Submit within **90 calendar days** of the DLP.
- Submitted electronically via the PSUR repository.

### Key PSUR Content
- **Worldwide marketing authorization status**.
- **Estimated exposure and use patterns** (patient-years of exposure).
- **Summary tabulations** of safety data from clinical trials and post-marketing sources.
- **Signal and risk evaluation**: Detailed analysis of closed, ongoing, and new signals during the reporting interval.
- **Benefit evaluation**: New efficacy/effectiveness information.
- **Overall benefit-risk analysis** for approved indications.
- **Conclusions and actions** (e.g., proposed changes to the SmPC/PIL or RMP).
