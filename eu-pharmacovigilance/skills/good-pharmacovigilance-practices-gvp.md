# Good Pharmacovigilance Practices (GVP)
Apply the EU Good Pharmacovigilance Practices guidelines:

### What are the GVP Modules?
The GVP modules are a set of measures drawn up by the EMA to facilitate the performance of pharmacovigilance in the EU. They are the definitive rulebook for MAHs (Marketing Authorisation Holders) and regulatory authorities.

### Core GVP Modules overview
- **Module I (Pharmacovigilance Systems and their Quality Systems)**: Requirements for the PSMF (Pharmacovigilance System Master File) and the QPPV (Qualified Person Responsible for Pharmacovigilance).
- **Module II (PSMF)**: Detailed requirements for the content and maintenance of the PSMF.
- **Module V (Risk Management Systems)**: Designing and implementing RMPs throughout the product lifecycle.
- **Module VI (Management and Reporting of ADRs)**: Rules for collecting, validating, evaluating, and expedited reporting of Individual Case Safety Reports (ICSRs) to EudraVigilance.
- **Module VII (PSURs)**: Format, content, and submission of Periodic Safety Update Reports.
- **Module VIII (PASS)**: Guidelines for non-interventional Post-Authorisation Safety Studies.
- **Module IX (Signal Management)**: Processes for detecting, validating, and evaluating safety signals.
- **Module XV (Safety Communication)**: Principles for communicating safety information to healthcare professionals (e.g., Direct Healthcare Professional Communications - DHPCs) and the public.
- **Module XVI (Risk Minimisation Measures)**: Selection of tools and evaluation of their effectiveness.
