# Pharmacovigilance Risk Assessment Committee (PRAC)
Understand the role and functions of the PRAC within the EMA:

### What is the PRAC?
The Pharmacovigilance Risk Assessment Committee is the European Medicines Agency's (EMA) committee responsible for assessing and monitoring the safety of human medicines in the EU. Established under the 2012 pharmacovigilance legislation (Directive 2010/84/EU and Regulation (EU) No 1235/2010).

### Key PRAC Responsibilities
1. **Risk Management Systems (RMP)**: Evaluates RMPs for new and existing medicines to ensure risks are minimized.
2. **Signal Detection & Management**: Assesses signals of new or changing safety issues arising from spontaneous reporting (EudraVigilance), clinical trials, or literature.
3. **Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSURs)**: Reviews PSURs (specifically the Single Assessment process, PSUSA) to continuously evaluate the benefit-risk balance of authorized medicines.
4. **Post-Authorisation Safety Studies (PASS)**: Reviews protocols and results of non-interventional PASS imposed as a condition of marketing authorization.
5. **Safety Referrals (Article 20, 31, 107i procedures)**: Conducts urgent or standard EU-level reviews of safety concerns, recommending regulatory action (e.g., modifying, suspending, or revoking marketing authorizations).

### Interaction with Other Committees
- The PRAC provides recommendations to the CHMP (Committee for Medicinal Products for Human Use) for centrally authorized products, or to the CMDh (Coordination Group for Mutual Recognition and Decentralised Procedures) for nationally authorized products.
- The CHMP/CMDh adopts the final scientific opinion or position, which the European Commission then translates into a legally binding decision applicable across all Member States.
