# Individual Case Safety Reports (ICSRs) & EudraVigilance
Manage ADR reporting and the EudraVigilance database:

### ICR Reporting Timelines (GVP Module VI)
- **Serious Adverse Reactions (ADRs)**: Must be reported to EudraVigilance within **15 days** of day 0 (Day 0 = the day the MAH or its partners first receive the minimum reporting criteria).
- **Non-Serious ADRs**: Must be reported to EudraVigilance within **90 days** of day 0. (Valid for all ADRs occurring in the EU. Non-serious cases from outside the EU do not require expedited reporting to EV, but must be included in the PSUR).

### Minimum Criteria for a Valid ICSR
To be considered a valid case requiring expedited reporting, four elements must be present:
1. An identifiable reporter (HC professional or consumer).
2. An identifiable patient (age, sex, initials, etc.).
3. One or more suspected medicinal products (usually the MAH's product).
4. One or more suspected adverse reactions.
*If any element is missing, the case is invalid, but the MAH must attempt follow-up to obtain missing information.*

### EudraVigilance (EV)
- The EU web-based information system designed to manage safety data by the EMA.
- **Direct Reporting**: MAHs report ALL valid ICSRs directly to EV (not to national competent authorities, NCAs). NCAs then download the cases relevant to their territory from EV.
- **EVDAS (EudraVigilance Data Analysis System)**: Used for signal detection. MAHs must monitor EVDAS for signals related to their active substances, combining this with their own database findings.
