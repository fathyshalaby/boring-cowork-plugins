# Signal Detection and Management
Perform systematic signal management (GVP Module IX):

### What is a Safety Signal?
Information arising from one or multiple sources (including observations and experiments), which suggests a new potentially causal association, or a new aspect of a known association, between an intervention and an event or set of related events, either adverse or beneficial, that is judged to be of sufficient likelihood to justify verificatory action.

### The Signal Management Process
1. **Signal Detection**: Continuously scanning data sources (MAH safety database, EVDAS, literature, clinical trials) to identify potential signals. Often uses quantitative/statistical methods (e.g., Disproportionality Analysis like PRR or ROR) and qualitative review of individual case series.
2. **Signal Validation**: Evaluating the data to determine if there is sufficient evidence suggesting a new causal association or a new aspect of a known association. Evaluates the strength of evidence, clinical relevance, and previous awareness (is it already in the SmPC?). If sufficient, it becomes a "Validated Signal."
3. **Signal Confirmation**: The PRAC confirms if a validated signal requires action.
4. **Signal Analysis and Prioritisation**: Continuously assessing the impact (e.g., severity of the reaction, public health impact) and deciding the timeframe for evaluation.
5. **Signal Assessment**: The formal review process. Often linked to the PSUR cycle or via a standalone PRAC procedure (EPITT - European Pharmacovigilance Issues Tracking Tool).
6. **Recommendation for Action**: The PRAC recommends action (e.g., update the SmPC, conduct a PASS, issue a DHPC, or no action needed).
