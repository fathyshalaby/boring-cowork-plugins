# Risk Management Plans (RMP)
Develop and maintain Risk Management Plans (GVP Module V):

### What is an RMP?
A detailed document required for all new marketing authorization applications in the EU. It describes the risk management system necessary to identify, characterize, and minimize a product's important risks.

### RMP Structure (Key Parts)
- **Part II: Safety Specification**: The foundation. Identifies:
  - Important identified risks (known hazards with a confirmed causal relationship).
  - Important potential risks (suspected hazards where causality is not yet confirmed).
  - Missing information (gaps in knowledge, e.g., use in pregnancy, pediatric populations, long-term use).
- **Part III: Pharmacovigilance Plan**: How the MAH will characterize the safety profile further. Includes routine PV (expedited reporting, signal detection) and additional PV activities (e.g., PASS, clinical trials, registries) targeted at addressing the risks/missing info defined in Part II.
- **Part V: Risk Minimisation Measures**: How the MAH will reduce the probability or severity of risks.
  - **Routine RM measures**: The SmPC (Summary of Product Characteristics), PIL (Patient Information Leaflet), packaging size/design, legal status (prescription only).
  - **Additional RM measures**: Only used when routine measures are insufficient. Examples: Educational materials for doctors/patients (DHPCs, brochures, training), Controlled Distribution Systems, Pregnancy Prevention Programmes (PPP).
- **Part VI: Summary of the RMP**: A lay-language summary published on the EMA/NCA website.

### The Proportionality Principle
RMPs must be proportional to the identified risks and the potential for missing information. Not all products require additional PV activities or additional risk minimization measures.
