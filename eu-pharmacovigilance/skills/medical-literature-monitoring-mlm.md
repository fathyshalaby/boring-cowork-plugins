# Medical Literature Monitoring (MLM)
Conduct required medical literature monitoring for PV:

### The MAH Obligation
MAHs must systematically monitor medical literature worldwide to identify ICSRs and safety signals concerning their active substances. This is a crucial source of post-marketing safety data.

### EMA Medical Literature Monitoring (MLM) Service
- The EMA performs literature monitoring for a specific list of active substances and selected medical journals.
- For these specific substances/journals, the EMA takes on the responsibility of entering valid ICSRs directly into EudraVigilance.
- **Crucial limitation**: MAHs cannot rely solely on the EMA service. The MAH remains responsible for monitoring:
  - Active substances NOT on the EMA MLM list.
  - Literature sources (journals, conference abstracts) NOT covered by the EMA.
  - Literature for identifying safety *signals* (not just ICSRs), recognizing emerging trends, and gathering data for PSURs, regardless of whether the substance is on the EMA list.

### Best Practices for Literature Monitoring
- **Frequency**: At least once a week for ICSR identification.
- **Search Strategy**: Documented search strings covering the active substance (and all known synonyms/trade names) combined with adverse event terms. Search recognized databases (e.g., Medline/PubMed, Embase).
- **Review Process**: Experienced PV personnel/medical reviewers must perform "Level 1" (title/abstract) and "Level 2" (full-text) review to identify valid ICSRs or signals.
- **Timelines**: The clock for expedited reporting starts on the day a member of the MAH's organization (or an outsourced vendor acting on its behalf) becomes aware of a publication containing a valid ICSR.
