# The QPPV and the PSMF
Manage the critical roles of the QPPV and the PSMF documentation:

### The EU QPPV (Qualified Person Responsible for Pharmacovigilance)
- **Legal Requirement**: Every MAH must have permanently and continuously at its disposal an appropriately qualified person responsible for pharmacovigilance.
- **Qualifications**: Must reside and operate in the EU/EEA. Must have documented experience in all aspects of PV. If not medically qualified, access to a medically qualified person must be available.
- **Responsibilities**: 
  - Establish and maintain the MAH's pharmacovigilance system.
  - Maintain an overview of safety profiles and emerging safety concerns for all products.
  - Act as a single point of contact for competent authorities (e.g., responding to queries, providing any information relevant to benefit-risk balance).
  - Ensure prompt, high-quality ICSR reporting and PSUR submission.
  - Must have *oversight* over the PSMF.

### The Pharmacovigilance System Master File (PSMF)
- **Purpose**: A detailed description of the pharmacovigilance system used by the MAH. Replaced the old DDPS summary.
- **Content (GVP Module II)**: 
  - Details of the QPPV (CV, contact info, back-up arrangements).
  - Organizational structure of the MAH relative to PV activities.
  - Sources of safety data (clinical trials, patient support programs, literature, spontaneous).
  - Computerized systems and databases (e.g., safety database validation status).
  - PV processes (SOPs for ICSRs, PSURs, RMPs, signal management).
  - PV system performance (compliance metrics, KPIs).
  - Quality system elements (audits, CAPAs, training records).
- **Location and Registration**: The PSMF must be located (or accessible) at the site where main PV activities are performed or where the QPPV operates within the EU. Its summary and location are registered in the XEVMPD/Art 57 database.
