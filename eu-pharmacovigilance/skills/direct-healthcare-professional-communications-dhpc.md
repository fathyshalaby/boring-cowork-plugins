# Direct Healthcare Professional Communications (DHPC)
Draft and manage DHPCs (GVP Module XV):

### What is a DHPC?
A letter sent directly to individual healthcare professionals by an MAH, or a competent authority, to inform them of important new safety information about a medicine and the actions they need to take to minimize risks.

### When is a DHPC necessary?
- Suspension, withdrawal, or revocation of a marketing authorization due to safety.
- Important changes to the SmPC (e.g., new contraindications, new severe warnings, restriction of indications).
- A change in the balance of benefits and risks for a product.
- New recommendations for treating adverse reactions or changing dosing to mitigate risk.
- Ongoing evaluation of a major safety concern (an interim DHPC).
- Falsified medicines or serious quality defects affecting safety.

### DHPC Process and Format
1. **Agreement**: The need for a DHPC, the target audience, the dissemination plan, and the exact text must be agreed upon with the relevant National Competent Authority (NCA) or the EMA (usually following PRAC assessment) *before* distribution.
2. **Format**: Must follow the standard EU template.
  - Prominent heading: "Direct Healthcare Professional Communication."
  - Clear statement of the reason for the communication.
  - Summary of the safety concern (the "what").
  - Advice for the healthcare professional (the "what to do").
  - Background information.
  - Call for reporting suspected adverse reactions.
3. **Clarity**: Must be objective, not misleading, and strictly separated from any promotional material (no commercial bias).
