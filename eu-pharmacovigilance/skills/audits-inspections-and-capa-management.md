# Audits, Inspections, and CAPA Management
Manage PV audits and regulatory inspections:

### Pharmacovigilance Inspections
- Conducted by National Competent Authorities (NCAs), often acting on behalf of the EMA for centrally authorized products, to verify the MAH complies with PV obligations.
- Types: Routine (scheduled based on risk factors), For Cause (triggered by specific compliance issues or safety triggers), Pre-authorisation (rare for PV, more common for GCP/GMP).
- **Common Critical Findings**: Failure to report ICSRs on time; inadequate PSMF; insufficient oversight by the QPPV; flawed/missing signal detection processes; failure to implement RMP commitments or labeling changes (SmPC updates).

### The Quality System & Internal Audits (GVP Module I)
- MAHs must perform regular, risk-based internal audits of their own PV system (and of vendors/partners performing PV tasks).
- Audits must be independent (auditors cannot audit their own work).
- Audit reports must be documented. Critical/Major findings must be noted in an annex to the PSMF until resolved.

### Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA)
- The backbone of the PV quality system. Any deviation, audit finding, or inspection deficiency requires a CAPA plan.
- **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)**: The most important step. Ask "Why" until the fundamental process failure is identified (not just "human error").
- **Corrective Action**: Fixes the immediate problem (e.g., submitting the specific late ICSRs).
- **Preventive Action**: Fixes the root cause to ensure the problem never happens again (e.g., automating data transfer between databases, rewriting the SOP, retraining staff).
- CAPA tracking: Must be formally tracked to closure, and effectiveness verified (did the PA actually stop the error from recurring?).
