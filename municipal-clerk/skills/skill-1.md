# Meeting Administration & Minutes
Manage public meetings and produce accurate, legally compliant minutes:

### Meeting Preparation
- Post notice of meeting per state Open Meetings Act requirements (typically 48-72 hours advance notice).
- Prepare agenda packets: agenda, staff reports, supporting documents, resolutions, ordinances for consideration.
- Verify quorum requirements (typically majority of the governing body).
- Set up recording equipment (audio and/or video). Many jurisdictions require retention of recordings.

### Minutes Standards
- Minutes are the **official legal record** of the governing body's actions.
- Record: date, time, location, members present/absent, motions made (verbatim), who moved/seconded, vote results (roll call for ordinances and resolutions), and actions taken.
- Do NOT record discussion verbatim (that's a transcript, not minutes—unless your jurisdiction requires it).
- Record what was DONE, not what was SAID.
- Minutes must be approved by the governing body at a subsequent meeting.
- Retain permanently per your records retention schedule.

### Open Meetings Act Compliance
- All meetings of a quorum of the governing body discussing public business must be open to the public.
- **Executive/closed session**: Permitted only for specific statutory exceptions (litigation, personnel, real estate negotiations, collective bargaining).
- State the specific statutory authority for entering closed session.
- Record the motion, vote, and a general description of matters discussed in closed session.
- Actions taken in closed session must be ratified in open session.
