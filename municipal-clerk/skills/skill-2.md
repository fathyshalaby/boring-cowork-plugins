# Records Management & Retention
Implement a comprehensive records management program:

### Records Retention Schedule
- Maintain a retention schedule approved by your state's records management authority.
- Common retention periods:

| Record Type | Retention |
|------------|----------|
| Meeting minutes | Permanent |
| Ordinances and resolutions | Permanent |
| Contracts and agreements | Life of contract + 6-10 years |
| Financial records (audit) | 7-10 years |
| Personnel records | 7 years after separation |
| Correspondence (general) | 2-5 years |
| Election records | Per state election code (varies) |
| Building permits | Permanent or life of structure |
| Public hearing records | Permanent |

### Electronic Records Management
- Digital records have the same legal status as paper records (UETA, E-SIGN Act).
- Use a document management system (DMS) with version control, audit trails, and search capability.
- Backup and disaster recovery: 3-2-1 rule (3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite).
- Digital signatures must comply with your state's electronic signature laws.

### Records Destruction
- Never destroy records without authorization per the retention schedule.
- Document destruction: date, records destroyed, authority for destruction, method.
- Litigation hold: Suspend destruction of any records potentially relevant to pending or anticipated litigation.
