# Public Records & FOIA Requests
Process public records requests accurately and within legal deadlines:

### FOIA/Public Records Request Process
1. **Receive**: Log the request with date, requester name, and description of records sought.
2. **Acknowledge**: Respond within the statutory timeframe (varies by state: 3-10 business days typically).
3. **Search**: Conduct a reasonable search of all locations where responsive records could exist.
4. **Review**: Examine responsive records for exemptions (personally identifying information, law enforcement records, trade secrets, attorney-client privilege, personnel records).
5. **Redact**: Redact exempt information. Cite the specific statutory exemption for each redaction.
6. **Respond**: Provide records with a cover letter. If denying or partially denying, state the specific legal basis.
7. **Fee calculation**: Per your fee schedule (copying costs, extensive search time charges, if allowed by state law).

### Common Exemptions (Vary by State)
- Personal information (SSN, medical records, financial account numbers)
- Law enforcement investigation records (active investigations)
- Attorney-client privileged communications
- Trade secrets and proprietary business information
- Personnel records (performance evaluations, disciplinary records)
- Draft documents and internal deliberative process

### Best Practices
- Respond within the statutory deadline, even if it's to acknowledge receipt and request an extension.
- When in doubt, disclose. The presumption in most states favors public access.
- Document your search methodology and decision-making process.
- Train all department heads on records preservation obligations.
