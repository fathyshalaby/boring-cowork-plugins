# UKCA vs. CE Marking Integration
Manage compliance in a post-Brexit landscape:

### UKCA Marking Basics
- The **UKCA (UK Conformity Assessed)** marking is the new UK product marking used for goods placed on the market in Great Britain (England, Wales, Scotland).
- It covers most goods that previously required the CE marking.
- **Northern Ireland** operates differently under the NI Protocol: CE marking remains valid, or CE + UKNI if a UK Approved Body is used.

### Current Transition Timelines (Subject to change)
- *In August 2023, the UK Government announced an indefinite extension for the recognition of CE marking for most goods.* This means for many sectors (like LVD, EMC, Machinery), you can continue using the CE mark in Great Britain indefinitely.
- However, specific rules remain for Medical Devices, Construction Products, Marine Equipment, and Transportable Pressure Equipment.

### Designated Standards
- Instead of "Harmonized Standards" in the OJEU, the UK uses "Designated Standards" published on GOV.UK.
- Currently, designated standards (BS EN) are largely identical to EU harmonized standards (EN).

### Combined Compliance Strategy
If a product needs both, the technical requirements are currently essentially identical. Your strategy:
1. Ensure design meets EN standards.
2. Compile one Technical File.
3. Draw up an EU DoC listing EU Directives.
4. Draw up a separate UK DoC listing UK Statutory Instruments (e.g., Supply of Machinery (Safety) Regulations 2008).
5. Affix both CE and UKCA marks to the product data plate.
