# The EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC)
Draft a valid, legally binding EU Declaration of Conformity:

### What is the DoC?
It is a formal, legally binding document signed by the manufacturer (or authorized rep) taking full responsibility for the product's compliance with all relevant EU legislation. It must translate into the language(s) required by the target Member State.

### Mandatory Elements of a DoC
1. **Unique Identification**: Product, batch, type, or serial number.
2. **Name and Address of Manufacturer**: (and Authorized Representative if applicable).
3. **Declaration of Responsibility**: "This declaration of conformity is issued under the sole responsibility of the manufacturer."
4. **Object of the Declaration**: Describe the product allowing traceability (a color image may be included).
5. **Legislation Compliance**: The object described is in conformity with the relevant Union harmonization legislation. *List ALL applicable directives/regulations (e.g., 2014/35/EU, 2014/30/EU).* 
6. **Harmonized Standards**: References to the relevant harmonized standards used (must include the standard number, part, and year of issue, e.g., EN 62368-1:2014) or references to the other technical specifications.
7. **Notified Body Intervention (If applicable)**: Name, number (4-digits), description of intervention, certificate number.
8. **Signature**: Signature, name, function (must be a person empowered to bind the company, usually CEO, Quality Director, or Compliance Manager). Place and Date of issue.

### Multi-Directive DoC
The EU "Blue Guide" states that when a product is subject to multiple directives, the manufacturer should draw up a **single** EU Declaration of Conformity combining all applicable legislation.
