# CE Marking Affixing Rules
Apply the CE mark correctly to products and packaging:

### Graphical Rules for the CE Mark
- **Proportions**: Must consist of the initials "CE" in the exact graphical form specified in Regulation (EC) No 765/2008. The C and E form two semi-circles.
- **Minimum Size**: The mark must be at least **5 mm** high (exceptions exist for very small devices, e.g., some medical devices or micro-machinery).
- **Visibility**: It must be affixed visibly, legibly, and indelibly.
- **Location**: 
  1. Affixed to the product or its data plate.
  2. If the product's nature makes this impossible or not warranted (e.g., explosives, very small implants), it must be affixed to the packaging and accompanying documents.

### Notified Body Number
- If a Notified Body was involved in the *production control phase* (e.g., Modules D, E, F, G, H), their 4-digit identification number must follow the CE mark.
- Example: "CE 0123"
- Note: The NB number is NOT added if the NB was only involved in the design/type examination phase (Module B).

### Other Marks
- You may apply other marks (like UL, CSA, TUV GS) provided they do not impair the visibility, legibility, and meaning of the CE mark.
- You cannot affix marks likely to deceive third parties regarding the meaning/form of the CE mark.
