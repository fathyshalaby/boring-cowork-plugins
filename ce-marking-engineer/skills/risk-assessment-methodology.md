# Risk Assessment Methodology
Conduct and document mandatory risk assessments:

### The Legal Requirement
Almost all CE Directives require a documented Risk Assessment (or Risk Analysis) covering the product's entire lifecycle (design, transport, installation, use, maintenance, dismantling).

### Standard Methodology (e.g., EN ISO 12100 for Machinery)
1. **Determine Limits of the Machinery**: Use (intended use, foreseeable misuse), Space (range of movement), Time (life limit of parts).
2. **Hazard Identification**: Identify all hazards (mechanical, electrical, thermal, noise, radiation, ergonomic).
3. **Risk Estimation**: Determine the severity of possible harm and probability of occurrence.
4. **Risk Evaluation**: Decide if risk reduction is necessary.
5. **Risk Reduction**: Apply the 3-Step Iterative Process.

### The 3-Step Risk Reduction Method (Crucial Concept)
You MUST follow this exact hierarchy to mitigate risks:

1. **Inherently Safe Design**: Eliminate the hazard by design (e.g., reduce voltage to 24V so shock isn't lethal, round sharp edges, eliminate pinch points).
   *If risk remains...*
2. **Safeguarding & Engineering Controls**: Add fixed guards, light curtains, interlocks, two-hand controls.
   *If risk remains...*
3. **Information for Use**: Warning signs, user manuals, training requirements, specify PPE. (Note: PPE and warnings are the LAST resort, not the first solution).

### Documentation
Keep a Risk Register tabulating: Hazard description, Initial Risk Score, Mitigation (Steps 1, 2, 3), Final Residual Risk Score, and reference to the applicable Harmonized Standard clause used for mitigation.
