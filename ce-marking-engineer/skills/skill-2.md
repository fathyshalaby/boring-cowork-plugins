# Directives & Regulations Identification
Determine which EU legal acts apply to your product:

### EU Directives vs. Regulations
- **Directive**: Sets out a goal that all EU countries must achieve. Individual countries must transpose it into national law (which can lead to slight variations in enforcement/interpretation).
- **Regulation**: A binding legislative act applied in its entirety directly across the EU simultaneously (e.g., MDR 2017/745).

### Key CE Marking Directives/Regulations
| Abbreviation | Name | Directive/Regulation Number | Typical Products |
|--------------|------|-----------------------------|------------------|
| **LVD** | Low Voltage Directive | 2014/35/EU | Electrical equipment 50-1000V AC or 75-1500V DC |
| **EMCD** | EMC Directive | 2014/30/EU | Anything with electronic components/motors |
| **RED** | Radio Equipment Directive | 2014/53/EU | Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, RFID, cellular devices |
| **MD** | Machinery Directive | 2006/42/EC | Machines with moving parts, interchangeable equipment |
| **MDR** | Medical Device Regulation | (EU) 2017/745 | Medical devices, software for medical purposes |
| **PPE** | Personal Protective Equipment | (EU) 2016/425 | Safety glasses, helmets, protective clothing |
| **RoHS** | Restriction of Hazardous Subs. | 2011/65/EU | Electrical/electronic equipment (material limits) |

### Products with Multiple Directives
Most modern products fall under multiple directives. E.g., a smart washing machine requires:
- LVD (electrical safety)
- EMCD (electromagnetic compatibility)
- RED (if it has Wi-Fi) - *Note: RED generally supersedes LVD/EMC for the radio elements.*
- MD (moving parts)
- RoHS (hazardous substances restricting)
