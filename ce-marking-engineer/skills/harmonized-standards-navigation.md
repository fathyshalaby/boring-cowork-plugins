# Harmonized Standards Navigation
Use Harmonized Standards to prove conformity:

### What are Harmonized Standards?
- Technical standards developed by recognized European Standards Organizations (CEN, CENELEC, or ETSI) following a mandate from the European Commission.
- They provide technical solutions to meet the "Essential Health and Safety Requirements" (EHSRs) of the Directives.
- Their references are published in the **Official Journal of the European Union (OJEU)**.

### The Principle of "Presumption of Conformity"
- Using Harmonized Standards is technically **voluntary**.
- However, if you apply a standard published in the OJEU and your product passes, you receive the legal "Presumption of Conformity" with the essential requirements covered by that standard.
- If you choose NOT to use a harmonized standard, you must prove (from first theoretical principles) how your product meets the EHSRs. This requires a much thicker Technical File and often a Notified Body assessment.
- **Best Practice**: Always use Harmonized Standards unless impossible.

### Example Standard Structure (EN ISO 12100 - Machinery Safety)
- Type-A standards: Basic safety concepts (e.g., ISO 12100 Risk Assessment).
- Type-B standards: Generic safety standards (e.g., B1 on safety aspects like distance, B2 on safeguards like interlocking devices).
- Type-C standards: Machine safety standards (e.g., EN 415 for packaging machines). Type C standards take precedence over A and B.

### Annex ZA/ZZ
The most important part of a harmonized EN standard for CE marking. It explicitly maps the clauses of the standard to the specific Essential Requirements of the Directive it supports.
