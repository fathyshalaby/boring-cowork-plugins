# Technical File / Technical Documentation
Compile the mandatory Technical File (TF):

### Purpose
The Technical File is the evidence that the product conforms to the relevant directives. It must be kept for **10 years** after the last product is placed on the market (varies by directive, but 10 years is the baseline).

### Required Contents (Typical)
While specific directives vary, a robust TF generally contains:
1. **General Description**: What the product is, models covered, intended use.
2. **Design & Manufacturing Drawings**: Schematics, assembly drawings, PCB layouts, BOM (Bill of Materials), sub-assembly descriptions.
3. **Directives & Standards List**: Explicit list of which harmonized standards were applied in full or in part.
4. **Risk Assessment**: Documented hazard identification and reduction (Skill 5).
5. **Essential Requirements Checklist**: A matrix mapping the directive's requirements to the standards applied or solutions adopted.
6. **Test Reports & Calculations**: EMC test reports, valid LVD test reports, structural calculations, software validation.
7. **User Manual / Instructions**: In the language(s) of the Member State(s) where the product is sold.
8. **Copy of the EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC)**.

### Format and Availability
- The TF does not need to exist as a single physical binder, but the documentation must be compilable and available for presentation to national Market Surveillance Authorities (MSA) within a reasonable time (usually a few days).
- It is usually held by the manufacturer inside the EU or their EU Authorized Representative.
