# Instructions & User Manual Compliance
Author legally compliant User Manuals and Safety Instructions:

### The Manual is Part of the Product
Under CE directives, if the instructions are inadequate or unsafe, the product itself is deemed non-compliant. The manual is the final step in the risk reduction hierarchy.

### Language Requirements
- Instructions must be provided in an official language of the Member State(s) where the product is placed on the market.
- Some directives (like Machinery) require providing the "Original Instructions" (the language of the manufacturer) and a "Translation of the original instructions" for the destination country.
- *Tip: Minimize text and maximize clearly understandable pictograms to reduce translation costs and risks.*

### Essential Manual Contents (e.g., Machinery Directive 1.7.4.2)
1. Manufacturer name and address.
2. Designation of the machinery (matching the data plate and DoC).
3. Copy of the EC Declaration of Conformity (or URL linking to it).
4. General description of the machinery.
5. Normal/intended use, AND reasonably foreseeable misuse.
6. Workstations likely to be occupied by operators.
7. Instructions for safe transport, handling, storage, and installation.
8. Instructions for commissioning and use.
9. Information on residual risks (risks that could not be eliminated by design or guards).
10. Instructions on necessary training and required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).
11. Maintenance, repair, and cleaning instructions (including LOTO procedures).
12. Airborne noise emissions data and vibration data (if applicable).
