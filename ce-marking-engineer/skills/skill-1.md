# CE Marking Fundamentals & Framework
Understand the core principles of the CE Marking framework:

### What is CE Marking?
- "Conformité Européenne" indicates that a product meets EU health, safety, and environmental protection requirements.
- It is a **legal requirement** for placing certain products on the EEA (European Economic Area) market.
- It is a self-declaration by the manufacturer (though high-risk products require third-party verification).
- It is NOT a quality mark or a mark of origin (e.g., "Made in EU").

### Economic Operators & Responsibilities
| Operator | Definition & Key Responsibilities |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| **Manufacturer** | Designs/manufactures product (or has it done under their name/trademark). Bears ultimate responsibility for CE marking, compiling technical file, drawing up DoC. |
| **Authorized Representative** | Established in the EU, mandated by a non-EU manufacturer to act on their behalf for specified tasks. Keep Technical File available for market surveillance. |
| **Importer** | Established in the EU, places a product from outside the EU on the market. Verifies manufacturer has done CE marking, DoC, and keeps technical documentation available. |
| **Distributor** | Any other professional in the supply chain. Verifies CE mark is present and instructions/safety info are provided in the correct language. |

### The 6-Step CE Marking Process
1. Identify applicable Directives/Regulations & Harmonized Standards.
2. Determine product specific requirements.
3. Identify whether an independent conformity assessment (Notified Body) is required.
4. Test the product and check its conformity.
5. Draw up and keep available the Technical Documentation.
6. Affix the CE marking and draw up the EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC).
