# Comprehensive Planning
Develop and implement comprehensive plans:

### Comprehensive Plan Elements
| Element | Content |
|---------|--------|
| Land Use | Future land use map, development policies, growth areas |
| Transportation | Road network, transit, pedestrian/bicycle, parking |
| Housing | Housing needs, affordability, diversity of types |
| Economic Development | Employment centers, commercial areas, workforce |
| Parks & Open Space | Parks system, trails, green infrastructure |
| Utilities & Infrastructure | Water, sewer, stormwater, telecommunications |
| Community Facilities | Schools, libraries, fire/police, government buildings |
| Natural Resources & Environment | Floodplains, wetlands, steep slopes, habitat |
| Historic Preservation | Historic districts, landmarks, design guidelines |

### Planning Process
1. **Community engagement**: Surveys, public meetings, workshops, online input. Ensure diverse and representative participation.
2. **Existing conditions analysis**: Demographics, land use inventory, infrastructure assessment, market analysis.
3. **Vision and goals**: Community-driven vision statement and goal framework.
4. **Plan development**: Policies, strategies, and implementation actions.
5. **Adoption**: Public hearing, planning commission recommendation, governing body adoption.
6. **Implementation**: Capital improvements, ordinance amendments, partnerships.
7. **Monitoring**: Annual report on implementation progress. Full plan update every 10-20 years.
