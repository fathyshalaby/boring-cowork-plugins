# Housing Policy & Affordable Housing
Address housing needs through planning and policy:

### Housing Needs Assessment
- Analyze: housing stock inventory, vacancy rates, price/income ratios, housing cost burden, demographic projections.
- **Cost-burdened**: Spending >30% of gross income on housing. **Severely cost-burdened**: >50%.
- Assess needs by income level: AMI (Area Median Income) brackets — extremely low (<30%), very low (30-50%), low (50-80%), moderate (80-120%).

### Affordable Housing Tools
| Tool | Mechanism |
|------|----------|
| Inclusionary zoning | Require % of affordable units in new developments |
| Density bonus | Allow additional density in exchange for affordable units |
| Accessory dwelling units (ADU) | Allow secondary units on single-family lots |
| Housing trust fund | Dedicated revenue source for affordable housing |
| LIHTC (Low-Income Housing Tax Credit) | Federal tax credits for affordable development |
| Tax increment financing (TIF) | Use increased tax revenue from development to fund affordable housing |
| Land banking | Public acquisition of vacant/abandoned land for future affordable development |
| Missing middle housing | Allow duplexes, triplexes, cottage courts in residential zones |

### Fair Housing Obligations
- Affirmatively further fair housing (AFFH): Proactively address segregation and disparities.
- Protected classes: Race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability.
- Zoning that effectively excludes protected classes may violate the Fair Housing Act.
