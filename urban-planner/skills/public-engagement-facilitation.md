# Public Engagement & Facilitation
Design and facilitate meaningful public engagement:

### IAP2 Spectrum of Public Participation
| Level | Goal | Techniques |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Inform | Provide balanced information | Fact sheets, websites, open houses |
| Consult | Obtain feedback | Surveys, public hearings, comment periods |
| Involve | Work directly with the public | Workshops, focus groups, charrettes |
| Collaborate | Partner in decisions | Advisory committees, consensus-building |
| Empower | Place final decision with public | Referendums, participatory budgeting |

### Equitable Engagement
- Meet people where they are: community centers, places of worship, laundromats, parks.
- Offer childcare, food, and translation services at meetings.
- Multiple formats: in-person, online, phone surveys, paper surveys.
- Partner with community-based organizations to reach underrepresented populations.
- Meeting times: evenings and weekends, not just business hours.

### Design Charrettes
- Intensive, collaborative design workshops (typically 1-5 days).
- Participants include residents, property owners, developers, elected officials, staff.
- Professional facilitators and designers work with the public to create plans and designs in real time.
- Produces tangible outputs: concept plans, design alternatives, illustrative renderings.
