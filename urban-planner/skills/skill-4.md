# Transportation Planning
Plan for multi-modal transportation systems:

### Complete Streets Principles
- Design streets for all users: pedestrians, cyclists, transit riders, and motorists.
- Context-sensitive design: An arterial in a downtown area has different needs than a rural highway.

### Level of Service & Traffic Impact Analysis
| LOS | Description | v/c Ratio | Acceptable? |
|-----|------------|-----------|------------|
| A | Free flow | ≤0.60 | Exceeds target |
| B | Reasonably free flow | 0.61-0.70 | Target for rural/suburban |
| C | Stable flow | 0.71-0.80 | Target for urban |
| D | Approaching unstable | 0.81-0.90 | Acceptable urban |
| E | Unstable flow | 0.91-1.00 | Marginal |
| F | Forced/breakdown flow | >1.00 | Unacceptable |

### Trip Generation
- Use ITE Trip Generation Manual to estimate trips from new development.
- Reduce base trips for: mixed use (internal capture), transit access, TDM measures, urban context.
- Traffic Impact Study (TIS) required when new development generates >100 peak hour trips (threshold varies by jurisdiction).

### Pedestrian & Bicycle Infrastructure
- Sidewalks on both sides of all streets (minimum 5' width, 6-8' in commercial areas).
- Bicycle facilities: sharrows, bike lanes, protected bike lanes, multi-use trails — context dependent.
- ADA compliance: curb ramps at all intersections, accessible pedestrian signals.
