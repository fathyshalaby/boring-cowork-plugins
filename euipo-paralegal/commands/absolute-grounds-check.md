# Command: /euipo:absolute-grounds-check

Screen a proposed trademark against Article 7 absolute grounds for refusal.

## Output Format
```
ABSOLUTE GROUNDS PRE-SCREENING (Art. 7 EUTMR)
Proposed Mark: [Word/Figurative - e.g., "SUPERIOR CLOUD"]
Target Classes: [e.g., 9 (Software), 42 (SaaS)]

1. DISTINCTIVENESS ANALYSIS (Art 7(1)(b) & (c))
- Descriptive Connection: Does the mark immediately relay information about the nature/quality of the goods? 
  [YES. "SUPERIOR" is a laudatory term indicating high quality. "CLOUD" directly describes the technology infrastructure for SaaS.]
- Syntactic/Semantic Rule: Does combining the words create a new, unusual meaning? 
  [NO. The combination follows normal English grammar rules].

2. CUSTOMARY USE & POLICY (Art 7(1)(d), (f), (g))
- Generic/Customary? ["Cloud" is generic for hosting, not for software itself, but highly descriptive].
- Public Policy/Deceptive? [No concerns].

3. RISK ASSESSMENT & RECOMMENDATION
Risk of Refusal: HIGH (Likely objection under 7(1)(b) and (c)).
Recommendation:
  ☐ Abandon and select a more arbitrary mark.
  ☑ Add a visually striking, highly distinctive figurative element (logo) to save the application. The word elements will remain weak, but the composite mark will register.
```\n
