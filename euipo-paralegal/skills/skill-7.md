# Registered Community Designs (RCD)\nManage the EUIPO's system for aesthetic protection:

### What is an RCD?
While a trademark protects a brand identifier (name, logo), a Design protects the *appearance* of the whole or a part of a product resulting from its lines, contours, colors, shape, texture, or materials. (e.g., the shape of a Coca-Cola bottle, the layout of an iOS interface, the design of a chair).

### The Requirements
To be valid, a design must have:
1. **Novelty**: No identical design has been made available to the public anywhere in the world.
2. **Individual Character**: The overall impression it produces on the "informed user" must differ from the overall impression produced by any prior design.

### The Grace Period
Crucially, a designer has a 12-month grace period. They can unveil their product at a trade show, test the market, and still file for an RCD within 12 months without destroying their own novelty.

### Paralegal Workflow for RCDs
- **Multiple Applications**: You can file multiple designs in one application (e.g., 10 different variations of a sneaker) provided they all belong to the same Locarno Classification class, vastly reducing fees.
- **Views**: The application relies entirely on the images (up to 7 views per design: front, back, top, bottom, sides, perspective). The paralegal must ensure drawings are clean, without distracting backgrounds, using dotted lines for parts of the product where protection is NOT claimed. No substantive examination is done by EUIPO upon filing; they just check formalities and publish it in days.\n
