# The EU Trademark (EUTM) System\nMaster the fundamentals of the European Union Trade Mark:

### What is an EUTM?
An EUTM offers intellectual property protection in all 27 Member States of the European Union through a single application to the EUIPO (European Union Intellectual Property Office) based in Alicante, Spain. It is an "all-or-nothing" system: if an application is successfully opposed or rejected based on absolute grounds in just one Member State, the entire EU application falls.

### Key Advantages
- **Cost-Effective**: Much cheaper than filing 27 individual national trademarks.
- **Unified Administration**: Single renewal date, single assignment document, single change of address.
- **Genuine Use**: To maintain an EUTM after its initial 5-year grace period, you must prove "genuine use." A major advantage is that genuine use in just one or two Member States (e.g., only selling in Germany and Austria) is usually sufficient to maintain the mark for the entire EU.

### The Relationship with National Marks
The EUTM coexists with national trademarks. A company might have a national mark in France (INPI) and another company can have the EUTM. However, prior national marks can be used to oppose new EUTMs, and prior EUTMs can oppose new national marks. Paralegals must constantly check databases using tools like TMview to spot these conflicts.\n
