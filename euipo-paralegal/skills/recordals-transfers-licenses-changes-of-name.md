# Recordals (Transfers, Licenses, Changes of Name)\nMaintain the register for corporate life events:

### The Importance of the Register
The EUIPO register must reflect reality. If a company changes its name, merges, or sells its IP, the register must be updated. Failure to do so causes severe issues if that company later needs to file an opposition (they can't prove they own the prior mark).

### 1. Transfers (Assignments)
- When Mark A is sold from Company X to Company Y.
- **Requirement**: Execution of a Deed of Assignment signed by BOTH parties. The paralegal submits this along with the EUIPO Recordal form.
- **Partial Transfers**: You can sell a trademark for *some* goods but keep it for others in the same registration. This splits the EUTM into two separate registrations.

### 2. Licenses & Rights in Rem
- When Company X allows Company Y to use the mark, or when Company X pledges the trademark to a Bank as collateral for a loan.
- Registering a license at EUIPO makes it enforceable against third parties (e.g., if Company X goes bankrupt, the new owner must respect the registered license).

### 3. Changes of Name/Address
- Purely administrative but highly prone to errors. Must ensure whether it's just a change of address/name of the *same legal entity*, or actually a transfer/merger resulting in a *new legal entity* (which requires transfer forms and evidence like extracts from a commercial register).\n
