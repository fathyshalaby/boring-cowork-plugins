# Seniority Claims\nUtilize the EUIPO mechanism to consolidate trademark portfolios:

### The Concept
If a company owns national trademarks in France, Germany, and Spain, and then registers an EUTM covering the whole EU, they effectively have double protection in those three countries. 
A Seniority Claim allows the owner to surrender or let expire the national marks, but "attach" their older filing dates (seniority) to the newer EUTM.

### The Advantage
Massive cost savings on renewal fees. The owner no longer pays local patent offices in France, Germany, and Spain every 10 years, they only pay the EUIPO. YET, if they ever need to sue someone in France, they can rely on the EUTM but claim the older priority date of the original French mark.

### The Triple Identity Rule
To claim seniority, the EUIPO strictly requires "Triple Identity":
1. **Identical Mark**: The national mark and the EUTM must be perfectly identical (a slight logo tweak fails).
2. **Identical Owner**: The registered owner name must match exactly (requires fixing any missing recordals first).
3. **Identical (or narrower) Goods/Services**: The goods claimed for seniority in the EUTM cannot be broader than what the national mark covered.

### Paralegal Task
Seniority can be claimed at the time of filing the EUTM or anytime after registration. Paralegals must download the exact extracts from the national IP offices (often requiring translation of the goods) to prove the triple identity to the EUIPO examiner.\n
