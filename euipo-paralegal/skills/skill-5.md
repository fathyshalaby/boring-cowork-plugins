# Evidence of Genuine Use\nCompile winning proof of use dossiers:

### The Requirement
A trademark isn't a property right you can hoard forever. Under Article 58 EUTMR, an EUTM becomes vulnerable to cancellation (revocation) if not put to "genuine use" in the EU within a continuous period of 5 years.

### What constitutes Genuine Use?
Use must be public, outward-facing commercial exploitation to create or maintain a market share. Token use (shipping one box a year just to keep the mark alive) does not count.

### The Essential Evidence (The "Five factors")
Examiners evaluate evidence based on Place, Time, Extent, and Nature of use for the specific registered goods.
1. **Invoices**: The gold standard. Must show the trademark name, the date (within the relevant 5-year period), the customer address (within the EU), and the volume/price of goods sold.
2. **Catalogs/Brochures**: Showing the mark affixed to the goods.
3. **Website Analytics**: URL traffic data, screenshots from the Wayback Machine proving the e-commerce store was active in specific EU countries.
4. **Advertising Spend**: Invoices for ad campaigns (Google Ads, magazines) featuring the mark.
5. **Affidavits**: Sworn statements from the CEO summarizing the data, but these have low evidentiary value unless backed up by objective primary documents (like invoices).

### Paralegal Best Practice
Do not dump 1,000 unindexed PDF invoices on the EUIPO. Create a clear Table of Annexes, highlighting the dates and the trademark in red on key invoices, categorizing them strictly by Class and specific goods.\n
