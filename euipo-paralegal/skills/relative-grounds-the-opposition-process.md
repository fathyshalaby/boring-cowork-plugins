# Relative Grounds & The Opposition Process\nManage third-party conflicts (Art. 8 EUTMR):

### What are Relative Grounds?
The EUIPO *does not* reject applications just because an identical prior mark exists. It is up to the owners of prior rights to defend their territory by filing an Opposition within 3 months of the new application's publication.

### Grounds for Opposition (Article 8(1))
1. **Identical Mark + Identical Goods (Double Identity)**: Absolute protection. No proof of confusion needed.
2. **Identical/Similar Mark + Identical/Similar Goods**: The opponent must prove a **Likelihood of Confusion** on the part of the relevant public.
3. **Reputation (Article 8(5))**: If the prior mark is famous (e.g., COCA-COLA), they can oppose similar marks even for *dissimilar* goods, if the new mark would take unfair advantage of, or be detrimental to, the distinctive character or repute of the famous mark (dilution/free-riding).

### The Opposition Timeline
1. **Notice of Opposition**: Filed within 3 months of publication. Fee paid.
2. **Cooling-off Period**: 2 months (extendable up to 22 months) where parties try to settle privately (e.g., signing a Co-existence Agreement).
3. **Adversarial Phase**: If no settlement, opponent submits facts/evidence, applicant replies, opponent replies. Months of legal drafting.
4. **Proof of Use**: The applicant's ultimate weapon. If the opponent's mark is older than 5 years, the applicant can demand they prove they've actually used it. If they can't, the opposition is dismissed.\n
