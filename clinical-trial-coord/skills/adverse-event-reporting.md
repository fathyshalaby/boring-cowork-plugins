# Adverse Event Reporting
Report and document adverse events (AEs) and serious adverse events (SAEs) accurately and promptly:

### AE Classification
| Category | Definition | Reporting Timeline |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| AE | Any untoward medical occurrence in a subject, whether or not related to treatment | Document within 24 hours of awareness |
| SAE | Results in death, life-threatening, hospitalization, disability, congenital anomaly, or important medical event | Report to sponsor within 24 hours |
| SUSAR | Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reaction | Sponsor reports to FDA/IRB within 7 days (fatal/life-threatening) or 15 days (other) |

### AE Assessment
For each AE, the investigator must assess:
1. **Severity**: Mild (Grade 1), Moderate (Grade 2), Severe (Grade 3), Life-threatening (Grade 4), Death (Grade 5). Use CTCAE (Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events) grading when applicable.
2. **Causality/Relatedness**: Not related, Unlikely, Possibly, Probably, Definitely related to study treatment.
3. **Expectedness**: Expected (listed in the IB or protocol) vs. Unexpected (not previously observed or documented).
4. **Outcome**: Ongoing, resolved, resolved with sequelae, fatal.
5. **Action taken**: None, dose reduced, drug interrupted, drug discontinued, concomitant medication given.

### SAE Reporting Process
1. Investigator identifies the SAE and assesses causality.
2. Complete the SAE report form (MedWatch 3500A for FDA).
3. Submit to sponsor within 24 hours of awareness (or per protocol-specified timeline).
4. Submit to IRB/IEC per local requirements (typically within 24-48 hours for the site's own subjects, within 10 days for external SUSARs).
5. Follow up until resolution with updated SAE reports.
