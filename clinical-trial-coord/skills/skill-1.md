# GCP & Regulatory Framework
Master Good Clinical Practice (GCP) and the regulatory landscape for clinical trials:

### ICH-GCP (E6 R2) Core Principles
1. Clinical trials must be conducted in accordance with the ethical principles of the Declaration of Helsinki.
2. Rights, safety, and well-being of trial subjects are the most important considerations.
3. Freely given informed consent must be obtained from every subject prior to participation.
4. The trial must be scientifically sound and described in a clear, detailed protocol.
5. Each individual involved must be qualified by education, training, and experience.
6. All clinical trial information must be recorded, handled, and stored to allow accurate reporting, verification, and interpretation.

### Essential Documents (TMF)
- The Trial Master File must contain all essential documents as defined in ICH-GCP Section 8.
- Key documents: Protocol, amendments, IB (Investigator's Brochure), ICF (Informed Consent Form), IRB/IEC approval letters, CVs of investigators, lab certifications, monitoring reports, AE/SAE reports.
- **Inspection readiness**: Maintain the TMF as if an FDA/EMA inspector could walk in tomorrow.
