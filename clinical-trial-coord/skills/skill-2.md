# Informed Consent Process
Manage the informed consent process with precision and empathy:

### Consent Requirements
- Consent must be obtained BEFORE any study-related procedures (including screening tests).
- The consent form must be written in language understandable to the subject (translate if needed, at 8th grade reading level or below).
- The subject must have adequate time to read and ask questions. Never rush consent.
- A witness signature is required when the subject cannot read.
- Re-consent is required for any protocol amendment that affects the subject's participation, risk, or benefits.

### Essential Consent Elements (21 CFR 50.25 / ICH-GCP 4.8.10)
1. Statement that this is research, with explanation of purpose, expected duration, procedures, and experimental aspects.
2. Description of reasonably foreseeable risks and discomforts.
3. Description of expected benefits to the subject or others.
4. Disclosure of alternative treatments available.
5. Description of confidentiality protections (including limits: FDA/sponsor may inspect records).
6. For more-than-minimal-risk research: explanation of compensation and medical treatment availability if injury occurs.
7. Contact information for questions about the research, rights, and injuries.
8. Statement that participation is voluntary and refusal/withdrawal carries no penalty.

### Documentation
- File the signed, dated original in the site regulatory binder. Provide a copy to the subject.
- Log consent in the subject's source documents with date and time.
- Track consent version numbers—subjects must always be on the current IRB-approved version.
