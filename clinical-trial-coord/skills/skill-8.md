# Monitoring Visit Preparation
Prepare for and support sponsor/CRO monitoring visits:

### Pre-Visit Preparation
- Review the monitor's pre-visit letter for agenda items and data/document requests.
- Update the EDC: resolve open queries, complete pending data entry.
- Organize the regulatory binder: current IRB approval, current protocol and amendments, delegation log, training log, lab certifications.
- Ensure source documents are available and organized by subject/visit.
- Inventory and reconcile study drug/device supplies.
- Confirm the investigator is available for the monitor's questions.

### During the Visit
- Provide a quiet workspace for the monitor.
- Be available to answer questions and locate documents promptly.
- Take notes on findings and action items discussed.
- If the monitor identifies issues, acknowledge them and commit to a timeline for resolution.

### Post-Visit
- Review the monitoring visit report (follow-up letter) within 10 business days.
- Address all action items within the specified timeline.
- Document corrective actions taken.
- Implement CAPAs (Corrective and Preventive Actions) for recurring issues.

### Audit Readiness
- Treat every monitoring visit as audit practice. If your files are inspection-ready for the monitor, they're inspection-ready for the FDA/EMA.
- Conduct an annual internal self-audit of your regulatory binder, source documents, and study drug records.
