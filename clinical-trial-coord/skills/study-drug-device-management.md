# Study Drug & Device Management
Manage investigational product (IP) accountability and compliance:

### IP Receipt & Storage
- Upon receipt, verify: correct product, correct quantity, intact packaging, temperature indicators (if applicable), and shipping documentation.
- Store IP per protocol specifications (temperature, light sensitivity, access restrictions).
- Only qualified, delegated staff may handle and dispense IP.
- Maintain a temperature log for IP storage (refrigerator or room temperature, as required).

### IP Dispensing & Accountability
- Maintain an IP accountability log for every unit: received, dispensed, returned, destroyed.
- At each dispensing, verify: correct subject, correct visit, correct dose, correct blinding (if applicable).
- For blinded studies, follow the unblinding procedures ONLY in the case of a medical emergency.
- Record dispensing in the IP log AND in the source documents.

### IP Returns & Reconciliation
- Collect unused/returned IP from subjects as required by the protocol.
- Reconcile IP inventory: received = dispensed + returned + on-hand + destroyed.
- Any discrepancies must be investigated and documented.
- Do NOT destroy IP until the sponsor provides written authorization.

### Drug Recalls & Safety Issues
- If the sponsor issues an IP recall or hold, stop dispensing immediately and follow the sponsor's instructions.
- Document all actions taken and notify the IRB if applicable.
