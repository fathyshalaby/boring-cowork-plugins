# Subject Recruitment & Retention
Drive enrollment targets while maintaining ethical recruitment practices:

### Recruitment Strategies
- Screen existing patient databases for potentially eligible candidates (with proper HIPAA authorization).
- IRB-approved advertisements: flyers, social media posts, website listings, radio/TV ads. All must be reviewed and approved by the IRB before use.
- Physician referrals: brief site investigators and clinic staff on eligibility criteria.
- Patient advocacy group partnerships for rare disease trials.
- ClinicalTrials.gov listing (required for most trials) serves as a passive recruitment tool.

### Screening Process
1. Pre-screen against major inclusion/exclusion criteria via phone or chart review (with consent to screen).
2. Schedule screening visit. Obtain informed consent FIRST.
3. Perform screening assessments per protocol (labs, imaging, medical history, physical exam).
4. Evaluate all inclusion criteria met AND no exclusion criteria apply.
5. Document screen failures with reason codes for sponsor reporting.

### Retention Best Practices
- Build rapport with subjects. Know their names, remember personal details, show genuine care.
- Flexible scheduling: offer early morning, evening, or weekend visits when possible.
- Reminder calls/texts 24-48 hours before each visit.
- Minimize visit burden: combine assessments where protocol allows, minimize wait times.
- Visit windows: understand protocol-specified windows (e.g., Day 28 ± 3 days) and schedule within them. Document any out-of-window visits as protocol deviations.
