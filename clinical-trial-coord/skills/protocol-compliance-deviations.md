# Protocol Compliance & Deviations
Maintain protocol compliance and manage deviations appropriately:

### Types of Protocol Deviations
| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Major/Important | Affects subject safety, rights, or data integrity | Consent not obtained before procedures, randomization error, dosing error, enrollment of ineligible subject |
| Minor | Does not affect safety or integrity | Visit outside window by 1 day, minor lab draw timing deviation |

### Deviation Management
1. **Identify**: Recognize when a protocol deviation has occurred or is about to occur.
2. **Document**: Complete a deviation log entry with: date, description, root cause, impact assessment, and CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action).
3. **Report**: Notify the sponsor per their reporting requirements (typically within 24-72 hours for major deviations). Report to the IRB per local requirements.
4. **Correct**: Implement immediate corrective action to protect the subject and data.
5. **Prevent**: Implement preventive actions to avoid recurrence (e.g., retraining, process improvement, checklist).

### Common Deviation Categories
- **Eligibility deviations**: Subject enrolled without meeting all I/E criteria.
- **Consent deviations**: Procedures performed before consent, wrong version used, re-consent not obtained after amendment.
- **Visit window deviations**: Visits conducted outside the protocol-specified time windows.
- **Medication non-compliance**: Subject missed doses or took prohibited concomitant medications.
- **Assessment deviations**: Required assessments not performed or performed incorrectly.

### Prevention Strategies
- Use source document templates/worksheets that mirror the protocol procedures.
- Maintain a visit schedule calculator for each subject.
- Train all site staff on the protocol before enrolling subjects and after each amendment.
