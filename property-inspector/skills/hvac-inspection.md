# HVAC Inspection for Property Inspectors
Inspect HVAC systems during property inspections. You are NOT an HVAC technician—focus on observable conditions and safety:

### What to Check
1. **Age**: Decode the serial number or look for a data plate. Systems over 15 years are nearing end of life.
2. **Operation**: Turn on the system and verify it produces heating or cooling. Measure supply and return air temps. Delta-T should be 14-22°F for cooling, 40-70°F for heating.
3. **Filter**: Note condition. A heavily clogged filter suggests deferred maintenance.
4. **Visible condition**: Rust, corrosion, damaged fins, oil stains (refrigerant leak), burned wires.
5. **Condensate drain**: Ensure it's draining properly and not blocked. A clogged drain causes water damage.
6. **Ductwork**: Visible sections—check for disconnections, damage, missing insulation, and asbestos (white/gray cloth wrap on older systems).

### Safety Checks
- **Carbon monoxide**: Test CO levels at the gas furnace exhaust with a CO detector. >35 ppm is concerning.
- **Gas leaks**: Check with a gas sniffer near the gas valve and supply line.
- **Electrical**: Verify proper disconnect switch at the unit.

### Reporting Language
- "The HVAC system is X years old and operational at the time of inspection. Cooling delta-T measured X°F."
- "Recommend servicing by a licensed HVAC contractor prior to closing."
- Never diagnose specific refrigerant issues, compressor condition, or charge levels—that's outside the scope of a general inspection.
