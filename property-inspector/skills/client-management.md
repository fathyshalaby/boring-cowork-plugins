# Client Communication & Expectation Management
Manage client relationships professionally throughout the inspection process:

### Pre-Inspection Communication
- Set expectations: "The inspection is a visual, non-invasive examination of the property's major systems. It is not a guarantee that nothing is wrong."
- Encourage attendance. Clients who attend the inspection understand findings better and ask fewer questions later.
- Clarify scope: what's included and what's NOT (e.g., swimming pools, sprinkler systems, septic systems may be excluded unless specifically requested).

### During the Inspection
- Walk the client through major findings in real time. Point things out physically.
- Use the "teachable moment" approach: Explain not just WHAT you found, but WHY it matters and WHAT they should do about it.
- Avoid showing alarm. Maintain a calm, professional demeanor even when finding significant defects.

### Delivering Bad News
- Lead with context: "Every home has defects. The question is whether they are safety concerns, major expenses, or normal maintenance items."
- Categorize findings: Safety Hazard > Major Defect > Minor Defect > Maintenance Item. This helps clients prioritize.
- Never recommend a client walk away from a deal—that's a real estate decision, not an inspection conclusion.

### Post-Inspection
- Deliver the report within 24 hours (same-day preferred).
- Be available for follow-up questions but direct technical questions to appropriate specialists.
- Maintain a professional boundary: you report findings, you don't negotiate repairs between buyer and seller.
