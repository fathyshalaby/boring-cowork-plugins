# Moisture & Mold Assessment
Identify moisture intrusion and mold indicators during inspections:

### Moisture Detection Tools
- **Moisture meter (pin type)**: Insert pins into drywall, wood, or trim. Normal wood: <15% MC. Elevated: 15-20%. Wet: >20%.
- **Infrared (IR) camera**: Screens large areas for temperature differentials caused by moisture. Wet areas appear cooler. Use as a screening tool, then confirm with a pin meter.
- **Visual indicators**: Water stains (brown/yellow rings on ceilings), efflorescence (white salt deposits on masonry), peeling paint, buckled flooring, musty odor.

### Common Moisture Sources
1. **Roof leaks**: Stains on attic sheathing or upper-floor ceilings.
2. **Plumbing leaks**: Stains below bathrooms or kitchens, especially around tub/shower surrounds.
3. **Grading/drainage**: Water pooling against the foundation.
4. **Condensation**: Poor ventilation in attics, crawlspaces, or bathrooms (exhaust fans venting into attic).
5. **Ice dams**: In cold climates, inadequate attic insulation causes snow melt, refreezing at eaves, and water backup under shingles.

### Mold Reporting
- **Never identify mold species.** Report "suspected microbial growth" or "conditions conducive to mold growth."
- **Never perform mold testing** unless you hold a specific mold assessment license (required in some states).
- **Recommend**: "Conditions conducive to microbial growth were observed. Recommend evaluation and remediation by a licensed mold remediation professional."
- Document with photos showing the extent and location of the suspected growth.
