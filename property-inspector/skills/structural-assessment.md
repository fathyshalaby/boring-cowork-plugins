# Structural Assessment Fundamentals
Identify structural concerns versus cosmetic issues:

### Foundation Cracks Classification
| Type | Width | Direction | Severity | Action |
|------|-------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Hairline | <1/16" | Any | Cosmetic | Monitor |
| Minor | 1/16"–1/4" | Vertical/Diagonal | Low concern | Seal, monitor |
| Moderate | 1/4"–1/2" | Any | Moderate | Engineer evaluation |
| Major | >1/2" | Any | High concern | Engineer evaluation ASAP |
| Horizontal | Any width | Horizontal | **Serious** | Immediate engineer referral |
| Stair-step | Any | Stair pattern in brick/block | **Serious** | Differential settlement—engineer |

### Signs of Structural Movement
- Doors and windows that stick or won't close properly.
- Visible floor slope (use a marble or level—>1/2" over 20 feet is concerning).
- Drywall cracks at corners of doors/windows (stress points).
- Separated crown molding or trim at wall/ceiling junctions.
- Bowing or leaning walls (measure with a plumb bob or level).

### When to Refer to a Structural Engineer
**Always refer when**: Horizontal cracks, active water intrusion through foundation, visible bowing of walls, floor slope >1" over 20 feet, or when the client asks "is this structural?"—your answer should be to recommend evaluation, not to determine structural adequacy.
