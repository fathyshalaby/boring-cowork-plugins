# Electrical System Inspection
Inspect residential electrical systems for safety and code compliance:

### Panel Inspection
- **Service size**: 100A minimum for small homes, 200A standard for modern homes. Note the main breaker amperage.
- **Panel brand concerns**: Federal Pacific (Stab-Lok) and Zinsco panels are known fire hazards—always flag these for replacement.
- **Double-tapped breakers**: Two wires on one breaker terminal is a defect (unless the breaker is rated for double-tap).
- **Wire gauge**: Verify proper wire gauge for breaker size. 15A = 14 AWG, 20A = 12 AWG, 30A = 10 AWG.
- **Aluminum wiring**: Homes built 1965–1973 may have aluminum branch circuit wiring. This requires COPALUM or AlumiConn connectors at all terminations. Always flag.

### GFCI & AFCI Protection
- **GFCI required**: Kitchens (within 6ft of sink), bathrooms, garages, exteriors, unfinished basements, laundry areas (NEC).
- **AFCI required**: Bedrooms (NEC 2002+), living areas, hallways, closets (NEC 2014+).
- **Test every GFCI**: Press TEST, confirm outlet goes dead, press RESET. Document non-functional GFCIs.

### Common Defects
- Open grounds (3-prong outlets without ground wire—common in pre-1960 homes).
- Reversed polarity (hot and neutral swapped—use a plug tester).
- Missing junction box covers (exposed wire connections = fire hazard).
- Improper exterior connections (no weatherproof covers on outdoor outlets).
