# Professional Report Writing Standards
Write inspection reports that are clear, defensible, and actionable:

### Report Structure
Every report should follow a consistent structure:
1. **Cover Page**: Property address, inspection date, client name, inspector credentials.
2. **Summary Page**: Major findings categorized as Safety Hazards, Major Defects, and Minor Defects. This is what most clients read FIRST.
3. **System-by-System Details**: Each system (Roof, Electrical, Plumbing, etc.) gets its own section with findings, photos, and recommendations.
4. **Appendix**: Full photo gallery, maintenance recommendations, and inspector qualifications.

### Writing Style
- **State facts, not opinions.** "The roof shingles exhibit curling and granule loss consistent with end-of-life wear" NOT "The roof is bad."
- **Quantify whenever possible.** "3/8-inch horizontal crack in the east foundation wall" NOT "crack in the foundation."
- **Use the SOR format**: Situation → Observation → Recommendation. "The attic bathroom exhaust fan terminates into the attic space (Observation). This can cause moisture buildup and mold growth (Situation). Recommend extending the duct to terminate at the exterior (Recommendation)."
- **Photograph EVERYTHING.** Every defect gets at least one photo. Use arrows and annotations.
- **Avoid alarmist language.** "Recommend further evaluation by a licensed structural engineer" is better than "The foundation is failing."
- **Disclaimer**: Always include that this is a visual, non-invasive inspection and does not guarantee the absence of hidden defects.
