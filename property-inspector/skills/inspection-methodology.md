# Systematic Property Inspection Methodology
Follow a consistent, top-to-bottom inspection sequence for every property:

### Exterior Inspection Sequence
1. **Roof**: Walk or drone-inspect. Check for missing/damaged shingles, flashing failure at penetrations (chimneys, vents, skylights), gutter condition, and proper drainage slope. Note approximate remaining life: asphalt shingles last 20-25 years, metal 40-60 years, tile 50+ years.
2. **Siding & Exterior Walls**: Look for cracks, rot, peeling paint, moisture intrusion signs (staining, efflorescence on brick, bubbling). Probe suspected rot with an awl.
3. **Foundation**: Walk the perimeter. Check for visible cracks (hairline = cosmetic; >1/4" or horizontal = structural concern), settlement, and grading (soil should slope AWAY from foundation at 6" per 10 feet).
4. **Windows & Doors**: Check for broken seals (fogging between panes), rot in frames, proper weatherstripping, and functional hardware.
5. **Grading & Drainage**: Ensure gutters direct water at least 4-6 feet from the foundation. Look for standing water, erosion, or signs of poor drainage.

### Interior Inspection Sequence
1. **Attic**: Insulation type and depth (R-38 minimum in most climate zones), ventilation (soffit + ridge vents), signs of leaks (water stains on sheathing), and proper bathroom exhaust vent termination (must exit to exterior, NOT into attic).
2. **Electrical**: Panel condition, wire type (copper vs. aluminum—aluminum requires special attention), GFCI protection in wet areas, AFCI in bedrooms (NEC 2014+), proper grounding.
3. **Plumbing**: Water heater age and condition, supply pipe material, drain function, water pressure (40-80 PSI normal), and visible leaks.
4. **HVAC**: Age, condition, last service date, filter condition, and operation test.
5. **Each Room**: Walls, ceilings, floors, windows, outlets, and fixtures systematically.
