# Roof Inspection & Assessment
Inspect and assess residential roofing systems comprehensively:

### Roof Covering Types & Life Expectancy
| Material | Life (years) | Key Inspection Points |
|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| 3-Tab Asphalt | 15–20 | Curling, granule loss, cracking |
| Architectural Asphalt | 25–30 | Seal strip failure, wind damage |
| Metal (Standing Seam) | 40–60 | Fastener corrosion, panel separation |
| Clay/Concrete Tile | 50+ | Cracked/broken tiles, underlayment failure |
| Wood Shake | 20–30 | Splitting, moss/mold, fire rating |
| Slate | 75–100+ | Delamination, broken tiles, flashing failure |

### Critical Areas to Inspect
1. **Flashing**: Around chimneys, valleys, wall-to-roof junctions, vent pipes, and skylights. Flashing failure = #1 cause of roof leaks.
2. **Valleys**: Open valleys should have metal flashing. Closed valleys hide debris buildup.
3. **Penetrations**: Every roof penetration (vents, pipes, satellite mounts) is a potential leak point.
4. **Gutters**: Properly attached, draining freely, with downspout extensions directing water away.
5. **Ventilation**: Adequate attic ventilation prevents ice dams (cold climates) and heat buildup (warm climates). NFA ratio: 1 sq ft of vent per 150 sq ft of attic floor.

### Estimating Remaining Roof Life
- Note the installation date (check permit records or manufacturer date code on shingles).
- Factor current condition: minor wear vs. significant deterioration.
- Provide a range: "Based on the observed condition and approximate age, this roof has an estimated 3–7 years of remaining functional life."
- **Never guarantee roof life.** Use qualifiers like "approximate" and "based on visible conditions."
