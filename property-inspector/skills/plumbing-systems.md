# Plumbing System Inspection
Inspect residential plumbing for leaks, material concerns, and functional issues:

### Supply Pipe Materials
| Material | Era | Concerns | Life Expectancy |
|----------|-----|----------|----------------|
| Copper | 1950s–present | Pinhole leaks in acidic water areas | 50–70 years |
| Galvanized Steel | Pre-1960 | Internal corrosion, flow restriction | 40–50 years (many past due) |
| CPVC | 1980s–present | Brittle with age, fails at fittings | 25–40 years |
| PEX | 2000s–present | Best modern option, flexible | 50+ years |
| Polybutylene (PB) | 1978–1995 | **Known failure-prone**—always flag | Replace immediately |
| Lead | Pre-1930 | **Health hazard** | Replace immediately |

### Drain/Waste/Vent (DWV)
- **Cast iron**: Common pre-1975. Prone to internal rust and deterioration. Inspect with a camera if accessible.
- **ABS/PVC**: Standard modern. Check glue joints for leaks.
- **Orangeburg**: Tar-paper pipe (1940s–1970s). Collapses under weight. Always flag for sewer scope.

### Water Heater Inspection
- **Age**: Check serial number for manufacture date. Tank heaters last 8–12 years.
- **TPR valve**: Temperature-Pressure Relief valve must be present AND piped to within 6" of floor or exterior.
- **Corrosion**: Rust at fittings, on the tank body, or water stains below the unit indicate leaks.
- **Flue (gas units)**: Check for proper draft. Backdrafting is a CO hazard—test with a smoke pencil.
