# Information and Consultation Rights
Understand the core legal framework governing Works Council rights:

### The EU Directive Framework (Directive 2002/14/EC)
The EU establishes a general framework for informing and consulting employees. Implementation varies heavily by Member State (e.g., Betriebsrat in Germany, CSE in France, Ondernemingsraad in Netherlands).

### Definitions
- **Information**: Transmission of data by the employer to the employees' representatives to enable them to acquaint themselves with the subject matter and examine it.
- **Consultation**: The exchange of views and establishment of dialogue between the employees' representatives and the employer.

### When is Information/Consultation Required?
1. **Recent and probable development of activities/economic situation**: (Often quarterly or annual updates on finances, strategy, sales).
2. **Situation, structure, and probable development of employment**: Anticipatory measures if employment is under threat (hiring plans, use of temporary workers).
3. **Decisions likely to lead to substantial changes in work organization or contractual relations**: 
   - Restructuring operations / M&A (Mergers and Acquisitions).
   - Collective redundancies.
   - Transfer of undertakings (TUPE).
   - Introduction of new technologies or monitoring systems.
   - Changes to working hours, shifts, or compensation structures.

### Golden Rule of Consultation
Consultation must take place **in good time**, *before* the decision is finalized or irreversible, allowing the Works Council's opinion to genuinely influence the outcome. Presenting a "fait accompli" (a done deal) is a severe compliance violation (e.g., 'Délit d'entrave' in France).
