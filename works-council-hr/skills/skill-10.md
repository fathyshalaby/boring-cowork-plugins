# Managing Difficult Relations and Deadlocks
Handle conflicts, escalation, and conciliation processes:

### The Obligation of Trustful Cooperation (Vertrauensvolle Zusammenarbeit)
In many jurisdictions, both sides are legally bound to cooperate in good faith and avoid unnecessary disruption (the peace obligation). Treating the WC as an adversary rather than a partner is the fastest route to crippling delays.

### Handling Deadlocks
When negotiations over co-determined matters completely fail:
1. **Conciliation Board (Einigungsstelle - Germany)**: An internal arbitration panel composed of an equal number of employer and employee reps, plus an impartial chair (often a labor court judge). The decision of the board is binding on both parties and replaces the agreement. It is slow and extremely expensive (employer pays the judge and WC lawyers). It should be avoided.
2. **Labor Court (Arbeitsgericht)**: For legal disputes (e.g., the WC claims you skipped consultation; management claims the WC is blocking a measure outside their co-determination rights).

### Best Practices for Healthy Relations
- **No surprises**: Informal "heads-up" conversations before formal memos are sent build immense trust.
- **Respect timelines**: Do not demand overnight decisions on 100-page restructuring documents. Building enough time into the business rhythm for proper consultation prevents friction.
- **Consistency**: Centralize labor relations strategy. Do not let local line managers circumvent the WC, as it destroys trust at the corporate level.
