# The French CSE (Comité Social et Économique)
Manage relations with the French CSE framework:

### What is the CSE?
Since 2020, the CSE replaces the former fragmented bodies (DP, CE, CHSCT) into a single representative body in companies with at least 11 employees.

### Mandatory Recurring Consultations (Companies >50 employees)
The employer must consult the CSE on three major topics annually (unless a collective agreement modifies periodicity to max every 3 years):
1. **Strategic orientation of the enterprise**: Economic direction, training plans, GPEC (forward-looking employment management).
2. **Economic and financial situation**: Turnover, profits, investments.
3. **Social policy, working conditions, and employment**: Headcount evolution, health and safety, gender equality, working hours.

### BDESE (Database)
The employer must maintain a BDESE (Base de Données Économiques, Sociales et Environnementales), an electronic database gathering all information necessary for these consultations. It must be accessible to CSE members. Failing to update it is a major compliance risk.

### The Expert's Role
The CSE has the legal right to hire an external financial or technical expert (e.g., an accounting firm) to help them understand complex information during major consultations (e.g., restructuring, PSE). The employer typically pays 80%-100% of these expert fees. Anticipate delays (2-3 months) when experts are appointed.
