# Collective Redundancies (Restructuring)
Manage consultation obligations during collective dismissals:

### Collective Redundancies Directive (98/59/EC)
Triggers specific obligations when redundancies reach certain thresholds within a specific period (e.g., 30 days or 90 days), varying slightly by member state (e.g., 10 employees in establishments with 20-99 staff; 10% in 100-299; 30 in 300+).

### The Consultation Process
Consultation must begin *in good time* and with a view to reaching an agreement on:
1. Avoiding collective redundancies altogether.
2. Reducing the number of workers affected.
3. Mitigating the consequences (via outplacement, retraining, severance packages - the "Social Plan").

### Required Information Package
Management must supply representatives with all relevant information in writing, including:
- Reasons for the projected redundancies (the business case).
- Number and categories of workers to be made redundant.
- Number and categories of workers normally employed.
- Period over which the redundancies are to be effected.
- Proposed criteria for selecting workers for redundancy (e.g., LIFO, skills matrix, social criteria).
- Method for calculating redundancy payments (beyond statutory minimums).

### Competent Public Authority
Management must notify the national competent authority (e.g., labor ministry or employment agency) of the projected redundancies in writing. Dismissals cannot take effect until a "cooling off" period (usually 30 days) after notification has expired.
