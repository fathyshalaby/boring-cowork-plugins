# Handling Transfers of Undertakings (TUPE)
Manage Works Council obligations during M&A and TUPE transfers:

### The TUPE Directive (2001/23/EC)
Acronym based on the UK implementation, but refers to the EU Acquired Rights Directive. When an economic entity transfers and retains its identity, employees automatically transfer to the new employer on their existing terms and conditions.

### Information and Consultation Requirements
Both the transferor (seller) and transferee (buyer) must inform the representatives of their respective employees affected by the transfer *in good time* before the transfer.

### Required Information
1. The fact that the transfer is taking place, the date, and the reasons for it.
2. The legal, economic, and social implications of the transfer for the employees.
3. Any "measures envisaged" in relation to the employees (e.g., reorganizations, harmonizing benefits, relocations).

### The Consultation Trigger
If either the buyer or seller envisages measures in relation to the employees, they must *consult* the employee representatives in good time with a view to reaching an agreement.

### Protection Against Dismissal
Employees cannot be dismissed solely *because* of the transfer. Dismissals post-transfer are only valid for ETO reasons (Economic, Technical, or Organisational reasons entailing changes in the workforce). Harmonizing terms and conditions downward post-transfer is highly restricted across Europe.
