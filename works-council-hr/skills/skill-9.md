# Privacy & Data Protection at Work (GDPR vs WC)
Navigate the intersection of Works Council rights and employee privacy:

### The Conflict
- **GDPR**: Mandates data minimization, purpose limitation, and strong employee privacy.
- **Works Council**: Often demands extensive data sets (salary lists, demographic data, individual bonus details) to fulfill their mandate of monitoring fair treatment and legal compliance.

### Resolving the Conflict
- Works Councils are NOT third parties; they are part of the controller (the company). However, they must independently comply with data protection principles.
- **The Necessity Principle**: The employer must provide the WC with data *necessary* to perform their tasks. You cannot refuse data merely by crying "GDPR" if the WC has a legitimate legal basis to review it.
- **Anonymization vs. Pseudonymization**: When providing salary grids or diversity statistics to the WC, aggregate or anonymize the data whenever possible so individuals cannot be identified, especially in smaller departments.
- **IT System Deployments**: A major battleground. Implementing software globally (like Workday, Salesforce, or Microsoft 365) requires extensive negotiation to prove the system complies with local labor laws regarding employee monitoring. You must prepare a detailed "IT System Profile" for the WC describing exactly what data is processed, who sees it, and when it is deleted.
