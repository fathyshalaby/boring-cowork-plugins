# Negotiating Works Agreements & Company Agreements
Draft and negotiate binding agreements with employee representatives:

### Nature of Works Agreements (e.g., Betriebsvereinbarung - BV)
A Works Agreement is a binding contract between the employer and the Works Council. In countries like Germany, it has a normative effect—it acts like a law directly affecting employment contracts, superseding individual employment contracts if the BV is more favorable, and sometimes even if less favorable.

### Typical Topics for Agreements
- IT systems and Data Protection (email, internet usage, monitoring).
- Working Time models (flextime, remote work, shift schedules).
- Evaluation and performance review systems.
- Bonus target architectures.
- Corporate policies (code of conduct, travel policy).

### The Negotiation Strategy
1. **Preparation**: Define minimum acceptable outcomes (BATNA). Anticipate WC concerns (privacy, workload, job security).
2. **The Draft**: Always aim to provide the first draft. Structure it legally but clearly.
3. **Information symmetry**: Provide clear answers to "Why is this being introduced?" and "What data is collected?"
4. **Safeguards**: Works Councils will demand limits on employer rights. For IT systems, compromise by guaranteeing that data collected will NOT be used for individual performance management or disciplinary action (the classic "no behavior control" clause).
5. **Termination Clauses**: Ensure the agreement defines notice periods for termination and what happens after termination (Nachwirkung – after-effects).
