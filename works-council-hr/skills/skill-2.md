# Co-Determination (The German Model)
Navigate strict Co-Determination rights (Mitbestimmungsrecht), particularly referencing Germany:

### What is Co-determination?
The strongest form of employee involvement. The employer *cannot* implement a decision without the explicit agreement of the Works Council. If agreement fails, the matter goes to a conciliation board (Einigungsstelle) whose decision is binding.

### Matters Subject to Co-Determination (e.g., German BetrVG §87)
These typically involve social matters that affect the collective workforce, not pure economic/business strategy decisions:

1. **Working Time**: Commencement and termination of daily working hours, distribution of working hours across the week, introduction of shift work, overtime arrangements, short-time work.
2. **Remuneration Structures**: Principles of pay, new compensation models, performance-related pay, bonuses (the *method* of calculation, not the absolute amount).
3. **Leave**: Principles for establishing holiday schedules.
4. **Surveillance/Monitoring**: Introduction of technical devices designed to monitor behavior or performance (e.g., GPS tracking, email monitoring, productivity software like Microsoft Viva/Teams analytics). *This is extremely heavily litigated.*
5. **Workplace Health & Safety**: Regulations on the prevention of accidents and occupational diseases.
6. **Order in the Workplace**: Dress codes, smoking policies, gate controls.

### Strategy for Co-Determination
- Draft a "Works Agreement" (Betriebsvereinbarung) defining the rules.
- Do not deploy software features (like status tracking in chat apps) that monitor employees without a Works Agreement in place for countries with these rights.
