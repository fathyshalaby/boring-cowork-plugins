# European Works Councils (EWC)
Navigate the complexities of transnational representation via EWCs:

### What is an EWC? (Directive 2009/38/EC)
A standing body providing information and consultation to employees of multinational companies operating in Europe on transnational issues.

### Applicability
Companies with at least 1,000 employees within the EU/EEA, and at least 150 employees in each of at least two Member States.

### Transnational Competence
The EWC is ONLY competent for matters that are "transnational" — meaning matters concerning the entire European undertaking, or at least two establishments situated in different Member States. Purely local issues (e.g., closing one plant in Spain without affecting other countries) remain the competence of local Works Councils.

### EWC vs. Local Councils
- The EWC does not replace national representative bodies; it sits above them.
- Typically involves an annual plenary meeting with central management and a "Select Committee" for ongoing dialogue and urgent issues.
- **Critical Process**: When a transnational restructuring occurs, management must inform and consult the EWC *and* the local Works Councils. Often, local consultation cannot conclude until EWC consultation is complete (though they can run concurrently in some agreements).

### Operating Agreements
EWCs operate based on a negotiated EWC Agreement defining the number of delegates, meeting frequency, budget for experts, and exact scope of information/consultation.
