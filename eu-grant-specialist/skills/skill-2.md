# The Funding & Tenders Portal
Master the primary tool for EU direct grants:

### Portal Navigation & Setup
1. **EU Login**: Create your personal account (required for all actions).
2. **PIC (Participant Identification Code)**: A 9-digit number identifying your organization. Search to see if your organization already has one before creating a new one.
3. **LEAR (Legal Entity Appointed Representative)**: The person authorized to manage the organization's legal/financial data and administrative roles in the portal. Designation requires original paper documents sent to Brussels.
4. **Roles**: Assign roles carefully (Coordinator Contact, Participant Contact, Task Manager, Financial Signatory/FSIGN, Legal Signatory/LSIGN). ONLY the FSIGN can sign financial statements; ONLY LSIGN signs grants.

### Finding Opportunities
- Search the "Search funding & tenders" section using keywords, specific programmes, or CPV codes.
- **Call for Proposals (Call)**: The overarching theme/budget.
- **Topic**: The specific problem to address within the call. You apply to a Topic.
- Read the **Topic Conditions and Documents** carefully. This includes the Work Programme, specific eligibility rules, and budget limits.

### Proposal Submission System
- Part A: Administrative forms generated by the portal (contacts, budgets, ethics questionnaires).
- Part B: The technical narrative (usually a PDF uploaded). Follow strict page limits (e.g., exactly 45 pages for Horizon Europe RIA). Pages over the limit will be watermarked and ignored by evaluators.
