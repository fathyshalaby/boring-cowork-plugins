# Ethics, Open Science & Gender Requirements
Comply with cross-cutting EU policy requirements:

### Ethics Requirements
- All activities must comply with ethical principles and relevant national/EU/international legislation.
- Common issues: Dual use (military/civilian), human participants, use of animals, AI systems, data protection (GDPR).
- Provide the Ethics Self-Assessment in the proposal. The EC conducts an Ethics Review resulting in Ethics Requirements that become contractual obligations (often requiring deliverables like "Ethics Advisor first report" or "Copies of ethical approvals").

### Open Science Practices
- **Mandatory**: Open access to peer-reviewed scientific publications. You must retain sufficient intellectual property rights to offer immediate (no embargo), free online access (deposit in trusted repository).
- **FAIR Data**: Research data must be Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable. Data must be made open by default, following the "as open as possible, as closed as necessary" principle.
- **Data Management Plan (DMP)**: A mandatory deliverable (usually Due M6) detailing what data will be generated, how it will be curated, and how it will be shared.

### Gender Equality Plan (GEP)
- Mandatory eligibility criterion for public bodies, research organizations, and higher education institutions established in MS/AC.
- Must be a public document, approved by top management, containing dedicated resources, data collection/monitoring, and training on gender bias.
- Must also integrate the gender dimension (biological sex and/or socio-cultural gender analysis) into the research and innovation content itself.
