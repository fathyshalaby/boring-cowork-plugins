# Proposal Structure (Excellence, Impact, Implementation)
Master the standard tripartite evaluating criteria (especially for Horizon Europe):

### 1. Excellence (What?)
*Evaluates the quality, ambition, and methodology of the proposed work.* 
- **Objectives**: SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Must align perfectly with the Call Topic.
- **State of the Art**: Demonstrate deep knowledge of current solutions and explain how your project moves beyond them.
- **Methodology**: Detail the concepts, models, and assumptions. Address Open Science practices and gender dimension in research/content.

### 2. Impact (Why?)
*Evaluates the difference the project will make.* 
- **Pathways to Impact**: How will project results lead to outcomes (short/medium term) and wider impacts (long term)? Target the specific impacts listed in the destination/topic description.
- **Measures to maximise impact**: 
  - **Dissemination**: Sharing results with peers/scientific community.
  - **Exploitation**: Using results for commercial purposes or policymaking.
  - **Communication**: Engaging the general public.
- **Scale & Significance**: Quantify whenever possible. Who benefits and by how much?

### 3. Quality & Efficiency of Implementation (How?)
*Evaluates the work plan and the consortium.* 
- **Work Plan**: Broken down into Work Packages (WPs). Needs logical flow, clear timing (Gantt chart), Deliverables (Tangible outputs), and Milestones (Decision/control points).
- **Risk Management**: Identify critical risks (technical, operational) and propose credible mitigation measures.
- **Consortium as a whole**: Explain why this specific group of partners is the *best* team for the job. Show complementarity and lack of redundancy.
