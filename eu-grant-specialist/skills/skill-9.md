# Audits & Compliance
Prepare for and manage EC financial audits (Ex-post audits):

### The Audit Environment
- The EC (or its external auditing firms) can audit a beneficiary up to **2 years after the payment of the balance**.
- The European Court of Auditors (ECA) and European Anti-Fraud Office (OLAF) can also conduct investigations.
- Goal of audit: Verify that costs declared are eligible, actually incurred, and properly recorded.

### Most Common Audit Errors
1. **Personnel Costs**: Incorrect productive hours calculation, lack of reliable time-sheets, claiming time spent on proposal writing (ineligible), charging commercial daily rates instead of actual salary costs.
2. **Subcontracting without best value**: Failing to demonstrate that subcontractors were selected transparently evaluating price/quality (no multiple quotes retained).
3. **Travel costs outside project scope**: Lacking proof of connection to project tasks (agenda, mission reports, proof of attendance).
4. **VAT**: Claiming deductible VAT as an eligible cost (VAT is only eligible if the organization cannot recover it under national law).

### Audit Preparedness Strategy
- **Keep separate project accounts**: Use analytical accounting codes specific to the grant.
- **Time-recording systems**: Must track hours per WP, signed monthly by employee and supervisor. Implement robust electronic timesheet systems.
- **Record retention**: Keep originals (or certified electronic copies) stored safely for 5 years after final payment.
