# Budgeting & Cost Eligibility
Develop compliant budgets according to EU financial rules:

### Basic Principles of Eligible Costs
To be reimbursable, costs must be:
- Actually incurred by the beneficiary.
- Incurred during the project duration.
- Declared in the estimated budget.
- Incurred in connection with the action and necessary for its implementation.
- Identifiable and verifiable (recorded in statutory accounts).
- Reasonable and justified (sound financial management).

### Cost Categories
1. **A. Personnel Costs**: Based on actual days worked × daily rate (calculated per EC rules, often based on calendar year divided by 215 days). Requires time-sheets or monthly declarations.
2. **B. Subcontracting**: Must be justified in Annex 1. Awarded on best value for money. No indirect costs applied to this category.
3. **C. Purchase Costs**: 
   - Travel & Subsistence.
   - Equipment (depreciation costs only, unless specifically allowed otherwise).
   - Other goods, works, and services (consumables, publications, certs).
4. **D. Other Cost Categories**: Specific to the call (e.g., Financial support to third parties / cascading grants).
5. **E. Indirect Costs (Overheads)**: Generally calculated as a flat rate of **25%** of direct eligible costs (excluding subcontracting and certain specific costs).

### Funding Rates
- RIA (Research & Innovation Action): 100% for all entities.
- IA (Innovation Action): 100% for non-profit entities, 70% for profit entities (SMEs and large enterprises).
- CSA (Coordination & Support Action): 100%.
