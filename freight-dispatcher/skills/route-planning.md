# Route Planning & Optimization
Optimize routes to reduce transit time, deadhead, and compliance risk:

### Route Planning Factors
1. **Total miles vs. practical miles**: Use PC*Miler or Rand McNally for truck-legal routing. Google Maps is NOT sufficient (it routes on car-restricted roads).
2. **Truck restrictions**: Low bridges, weight-restricted roads, no-truck zones. Always confirm route allows the equipment type.
3. **Fuel stops**: Plan fuel stops at truck stops with competitive diesel pricing. Use apps like GasBuddy or fleet fuel cards (Comdata, EFS).
4. **Rest areas & parking**: Truck parking shortage is real. After 5 PM on the East Coast, finding parking is extremely difficult. Plan driver's 10-hour break location in advance.

### Weather & Seasonal Considerations
- **Winter**: I-80 through Wyoming and I-90 through Montana/South Dakota are frequently closed. Chain laws apply.
- **Summer**: Arizona, Nevada, and Southern California have tire blowout risk at extreme temperatures.
- **Hurricane Season** (June–November): Gulf Coast and Eastern Seaboard. Monitor NOAA and adjust routes.

### Multi-Stop Optimization
- Sequence stops geographically to minimize backtracking.
- Account for appointment windows at each stop. A missed appointment = potential rollover to next day.
- Charge shipper for each additional stop: typically $50–$150 per extra stop.
