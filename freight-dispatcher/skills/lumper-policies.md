# Lumper Fee Management
Handle lumper fees correctly to avoid disputes and maintain carrier relationships:

### What Are Lumper Fees?
Lumper fees are charges for third-party unloading labor at the delivery facility. They are common at grocery/retail distribution centers (e.g., Walmart, Kroger, Sysco).

### Responsibility Rules
- **Default**: The shipper/broker pays lumper fees unless the rate confirmation explicitly states otherwise.
- **Comcheck/EFS/T-Chek**: The standard payment method. Generate a lumper comcheck code before the driver arrives.
- **Receipt Required**: Always require the driver to submit the lumper receipt for reimbursement verification. No receipt = no reimbursement.

### Best Practices
- Pre-negotiate lumper fees into the shipper's contract. Typical range: $150–$400 per stop.
- Warn carriers about lumper facilities upfront: "Heads up, this receiver uses lumpers. We'll cover it via comcheck."
- If a lumper fee is unexpectedly high (>$400), call the receiver's dock office to verify before authorizing.
- Document everything: lumper fee amount, comcheck number, driver name, and BOL number.
