# Cross-Border (US-Canada-Mexico) Operations
Navigate the complexities of cross-border freight movements:

### US-Canada Crossings
- **FAST/PARS**: Pre-arrival processing to expedite customs clearance. Carrier must be FAST-approved.
- **Customs Broker**: Required on both sides. Shipper provides commercial invoice and packing list.
- **PARS Number**: Assigned by customs broker, transmitted to CBSA before arrival at the border.
- **Cabotage**: A Canadian carrier CANNOT pick up and deliver within the US (and vice versa). The load must cross the border.
- **Common Crossings**: Ambassador Bridge (Detroit-Windsor), Blue Water Bridge (Port Huron-Sarnia), Peace Bridge (Buffalo-Fort Erie), Pacific Highway (Blaine-Surrey).

### US-Mexico Crossings
- **Drayage Model**: Mexico-domiciled carriers typically transfer freight to a US carrier at the border.
- **C-TPAT**: Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism. Preferred carriers get expedited processing.
- **Border Wait Times**: Check CBP wait times. Laredo, El Paso, and Otay Mesa can have 4+ hour delays.

### Documentation Essentials
- Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading, NAFTA/USMCA Certificate of Origin (for duty-free treatment).
- Ensure HS (Harmonized System) codes are correct. Wrong codes = customs holds and fines.
