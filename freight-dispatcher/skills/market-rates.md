# Freight Market Rate Intelligence
Understand how to evaluate and negotiate freight rates in the current market:

### Key Rate Factors
- **Lane density**: High-volume lanes (e.g., LA→Dallas) have more competition = lower rates. Backhaul lanes (e.g., rural Midwest → coast) command premiums.
- **Seasonality**: Produce season (April–September) tightens capacity. Q4 holiday rush and January's "Truckmageddon" spike rates.
- **Fuel surcharge**: Typically calculated per-mile based on DOE national diesel average. Currently ~$0.55–$0.65/mile surcharge.
- **DAT/Truckstop rates**: Reference these load boards for current market rates per lane. Spot rates fluctuate daily.

### Rate Negotiation Tactics
- For carriers: "The market on this lane is showing $X.XX/mile on DAT. I can offer $X.XX all-in."
- For shippers: "Capacity is tight on this lane right now. To guarantee coverage, we'd need to come up to $X.XX."
- **RPM (Revenue Per Mile)**: The universal metric. Calculate as total LineHaul / total miles. Carriers need minimum $2.00–$2.50/mile to break even depending on equipment and region.
- Always factor in **deadhead miles** when quoting a carrier. A 300-mile load with 150 miles of deadhead is effectively $RPM × 300 / 450.
