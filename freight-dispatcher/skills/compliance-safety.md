# Compliance & Safety Regulations
Ensure every dispatched load meets federal and state safety requirements:

### FMCSA Carrier Vetting
Before dispatching a carrier, verify:
1. **MC Authority**: Active and in good standing (check SAFER or Highway).
2. **Insurance**: Minimum $750K cargo + $1M auto liability. Get a fresh certificate of insurance (COI).
3. **Safety Rating**: Check CSA scores. Avoid carriers with "Conditional" or "Unsatisfactory" ratings.
4. **Operating Authority Age**: New authorities (<6 months) are higher risk for double-brokering.

### Double-Brokering Prevention
- Red flags: Carrier wants to change pickup driver last minute, MC number doesn't match truck at pickup, carrier won't provide driver's cell phone.
- Always call the carrier's dispatch number on FMCSA records, not the number they called you from.
- Use **carrier identity verification**: Ask for a callback number, verify MC, and confirm equipment details.

### Cargo Claims Process
1. Document damage at delivery with **photos and driver signature on the BOL exception**.
2. File a written claim within 9 months of delivery (Carmack Amendment).
3. Carrier has 30 days to acknowledge, 120 days to resolve.
4. Liability: Carriers are liable for full actual loss unless a released value rate was agreed upon.
