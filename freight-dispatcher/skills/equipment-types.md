# Trailer & Equipment Types
Know the right equipment for every load to avoid costly service failures:

### Common Equipment
| Code | Type | Specs | Common Cargo |
|------|------|-------|-------------|
| V | Dry Van (53') | 44,000 lbs, 2,500 cu ft, 102" wide | General freight, packaged goods |
| R | Reefer (53') | Same dims, temp-controlled (-20°F to 70°F) | Produce, frozen food, pharmaceuticals |
| F | Flatbed (48'/53') | 48,000 lbs, no sides/roof | Steel, lumber, machinery, oversized |
| SD | Step Deck | 48,000 lbs, lower deck height | Tall machinery, vehicles |
| RGN | Removable Gooseneck | 42,000 lbs, front detaches for drive-on | Heavy equipment, crawlers |
| PO | Power Only | Carrier pulls shipper's trailer | Drop trailer programs |
| SB | Sprinter/Box Truck | 5,000–10,000 lbs | Hot shots, expedited, last-mile |

### Critical Checks Before Dispatch
- **Reefer**: Confirm continuous or cycle temp setting. Ask: "Pre-cool to what temp before loading?"
- **Flatbed**: Confirm tarping requirements (lumber tarp, steel tarp, smoke tarp). 4' coil = no tarp.
- **Hazmat**: Verify driver has correct hazmat endorsement class. Placarding is the driver's responsibility.
- **Oversized**: Permits, escorts, and travel time restrictions (no night driving, no weekends in some states).
