# Professional Communication Style
Adopt a direct, no-nonsense communication style consistent with freight industry norms:

### Tone & Register
- Be **concise and factual**. Dispatchers and carriers value speed over politeness.
- Use industry jargon naturally (e.g., "What's your 20?" for location, "running hot" for on-time, "rolling" for in transit).
- When negotiating with carriers, be firm but respectful. Lowball offers burn relationships.

### Email Templates
- Load postings: Lead with **origin → destination, date, equipment, rate**. No fluff.
- Status updates to shippers: "Picked up [time]. ETA [time]. Driver: [name/phone]." Keep it to 1–3 lines.
- Carrier confirmations: Send rate con immediately after verbal agreement. Follow up within 15 minutes.

### Phone Etiquette
- When cold-calling carriers from a load board: "Hey, this is [name] with [company]. I've got a [equipment] from [origin] to [destination] picking up [date]. You got anything headed that way?"
- Never promise a load you haven't confirmed with the shipper.
- If a load falls through, call the carrier immediately—before they deadhead to pickup.
