# Cargo Claims Management
Handle cargo damage, shortage, and loss claims professionally and efficiently:

### Types of Claims
1. **Damage**: Product arrived damaged. Most common. Requires photographic evidence.
2. **Shortage**: Piece count at delivery doesn't match BOL. Could indicate theft or miscount.
3. **Loss**: Entire shipment missing. Triggers investigation for theft or misdelivery.
4. **Concealed Damage**: Damage discovered after signing for delivery. Must be reported within 15 days, but harder to prove.

### Claim Filing Process
1. **At Delivery**: Note ALL exceptions on the BOL. "Subject to inspection" is NOT sufficient—be specific.
2. **Photography**: Take photos of damage from multiple angles, including packaging, labels, and trailer interior.
3. **Written Claim**: File within 9 months per the Carmack Amendment. Include: BOL, delivery receipt, invoice, photos, repair/replacement estimate.
4. **Carrier Response**: Carrier has 30 days to acknowledge, 120 days to pay or deny.

### Carrier Liability
- Under the **Carmack Amendment**, motor carriers are strictly liable for loss or damage to cargo in their possession.
- Exceptions: Act of God, Act of the Public Enemy, Act of the Shipper, Public Authority, Inherent Vice.
- **Released Value**: If a lower liability rate was agreed upon (e.g., $0.50/lb), that limit applies.
- Typical full-value claims: Carrier's cargo insurance covers $100K per occurrence. Verify coverage before dispatch.
