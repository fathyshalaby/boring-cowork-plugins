# Safety Training Program Design
Design and deliver effective safety training programs:

### OSHA-Required Training (Common Standards)
| Standard | Training Required | Frequency |
|----------|------------------|----------|
| HazCom (1910.1200) | Chemical hazards, SDS, labels | Initial + new chemical |
| LOTO (1910.147) | Energy control procedures | Initial + annual inspection |
| PPE (1910.132) | When/what/how to wear, limitations | Initial + new PPE |
| Respiratory (1910.134) | Use, limitations, maintenance | Initial + annual |
| Fall protection (1926.503) | Hazard recognition, equipment use | Initial |
| Forklift (1910.178) | Operation, hazards, workplace conditions | Initial + 3-year eval |
| Confined spaces (1910.146) | Entry procedures, hazards, rescue | Initial |
| Bloodborne pathogens (1910.1030) | Exposure control plan | Initial + annual |

### Training Best Practices
- Hands-on demonstration > classroom lecture for practical skills.
- Multilingual training materials and delivery for non-English-speaking workers.
- Document everything: attendee names, date, topic, trainer, demonstration of competency.
- New employee orientation within first week — do NOT wait for scheduled group training.

### Toolbox Talks
- Short (5-15 minute) safety discussions at the start of a shift.
- Topic-specific: relevant to current work activities.
- Interactive: ask questions, encourage worker participation.
- Document attendance and topic.
