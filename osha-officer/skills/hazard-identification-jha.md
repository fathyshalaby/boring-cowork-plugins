# Hazard Identification & JHA
Identify workplace hazards and conduct Job Hazard Analyses:

### Hazard Categories
- **Safety hazards**: Falls, struck-by, caught-in/between, electrocution (the "Fatal Four" in construction account for ~60% of worker deaths).
- **Health hazards**: Chemical exposure, noise, heat/cold stress, biological agents, ergonomic stressors.
- **Environmental hazards**: Weather, confined spaces, trenching, elevated work.

### Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) Process
1. Select the job to analyze (prioritize: high injury rates, new operations, near-miss history).
2. Break the job into sequential steps.
3. For each step, identify potential hazards.
4. For each hazard, determine controls using the Hierarchy of Controls.
5. Document in the JHA form. Train workers on the JHA.

### Hierarchy of Controls (Most to Least Effective)
1. **Elimination**: Remove the hazard entirely.
2. **Substitution**: Replace with less hazardous material/process.
3. **Engineering controls**: Physical modifications (guards, ventilation, barriers).
4. **Administrative controls**: Policies, procedures, training, rotation.
5. **PPE**: Last resort — personal protective equipment.

### Workplace Inspections
- Conduct regular workplace inspections (daily walk-throughs, weekly formal, monthly comprehensive).
- Use standardized inspection checklists by area/department.
- Document findings, assign corrective actions with deadlines, and verify completion.
