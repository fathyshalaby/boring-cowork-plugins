# Fall Protection Programs
Implement comprehensive fall protection per OSHA standards:

### Fall Protection Triggers
| Industry | Height Trigger |
|----------|---------------|
| Construction (1926.501) | 6 feet |
| General Industry (1910.28) | 4 feet |
| Shipyard (1915.159) | 5 feet |

### Fall Protection Systems
- **Guardrails**: Top rail at 42" (±3"), midrail at 21", capable of withstanding 200 lbs force.
- **Safety nets**: Installed as close as practicable below the working surface, not more than 30 feet.
- **Personal Fall Arrest Systems (PFAS)**: Full body harness + connecting device (lanyard or SRL) + anchor point. Anchor must support 5,000 lbs per attached worker or be designed by a qualified person.
- **Positioning systems**: Used for rebar tying, steel erection. Must limit free fall to 2 feet.
- **Warning line systems**: Construction only, for roofing work. Lines set 6 feet from the edge.

### Fall Protection Plan
- Written plan required when conventional fall protection is infeasible (construction only, 1926.502(k)).
- Must identify the specific work activities, explain why conventional methods are infeasible, and describe alternative measures.
- Developed by a qualified person, site-specific.

### Rescue Planning
- **Every PFAS requires a rescue plan.** A worker suspended in a harness for more than 6-15 minutes can develop suspension trauma (orthostatic intolerance).
- Rescue options: self-rescue, assisted rescue by co-workers, professional rescue.
- Practice rescue procedures and have equipment readily available.
