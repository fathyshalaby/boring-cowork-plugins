# Incident Investigation & Reporting
Investigate workplace incidents and meet OSHA reporting requirements:

### OSHA Reporting Requirements
| Event | Reporting Requirement |
|-------|----------------------|
| Fatality | Report to OSHA within **8 hours** |
| Inpatient hospitalization | Report to OSHA within **24 hours** |
| Amputation | Report to OSHA within **24 hours** |
| Loss of an eye | Report to OSHA within **24 hours** |
| All recordable injuries/illnesses | Record on OSHA 300 Log within 7 calendar days |

### Investigation Methodology
1. **Secure the scene**: Prevent additional injuries. Preserve evidence.
2. **Provide medical attention**: Priority is always the injured worker.
3. **Notify management**: Immediately. Determine OSHA reporting obligation.
4. **Document the scene**: Photos, measurements, witness identification.
5. **Interview witnesses**: As soon as possible while memories are fresh. Use open-ended questions.
6. **Determine root cause**: Use the 5 Whys, fishbone diagram, or fault tree analysis. Go beyond the immediate cause to systemic failures.
7. **Corrective actions**: Address root causes, not just symptoms. Assign owners and deadlines.
8. **Follow-up**: Verify corrective actions are implemented and effective.

### OSHA 300 Log & Recordkeeping
- Maintain OSHA 300 Log of all recordable injuries/illnesses.
- Post OSHA 300A Summary from February 1 through April 30 each year.
- Electronic submission of 300A data required for establishments with ≥100 employees in certain industries.
- Retain records for 5 years.
