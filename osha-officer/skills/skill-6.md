# Hazard Communication (GHS/HazCom)
Manage the Hazard Communication Standard (HCS 2012, GHS-aligned):

### HazCom Program Requirements
1. **Written program**: How the employer will implement HazCom. Must address labels, SDS, and training.
2. **Chemical inventory**: List of all hazardous chemicals in the workplace.
3. **Safety Data Sheets (SDS)**: 16-section standardized format. Must be readily accessible to employees during their shift.
4. **Labels**: GHS-compliant labels with product identifier, signal word, hazard statements, pictograms, precautionary statements, and supplier info.
5. **Training**: Initial and when new hazards are introduced. Employees must understand: hazards of chemicals they work with, how to read labels and SDS, and protective measures.

### GHS Pictograms
| Pictogram | Hazard |
|-----------|--------|
| Flame | Flammable, self-reactive |
| Skull & Crossbones | Acute toxicity (severe) |
| Exclamation Mark | Irritant, narcotic, acute toxicity (less severe) |
| Health Hazard | Carcinogen, mutagenicity, reproductive toxicity |
| Corrosion | Corrosive to metals, skin, eyes |
| Gas Cylinder | Gases under pressure |
| Exploding Bomb | Explosives, self-reactive |
| Flame over Circle | Oxidizers |
| Environment | Aquatic toxicity |
