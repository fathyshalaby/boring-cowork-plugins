# Emergency Action Plans
Develop and maintain emergency action plans:

### OSHA Requirements (1910.38)
- Written plan required if your facility has >10 employees (10 or fewer may communicate orally).
- Required elements: emergency escape procedures and routes, procedures for employees who remain to operate critical equipment, procedures for accounting for all employees after evacuation, rescue and medical duties, preferred means of reporting emergencies, person(s) to contact for plan information.

### Emergency Types to Address
| Emergency | Key Planning Elements |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Fire | Alarm system, evacuation routes, assembly points, fire extinguisher training |
| Severe weather | Shelter areas, warning system, communication plan |
| Active shooter | Run-Hide-Fight protocol, lockdown procedures, communication |
| Chemical spill | Spill response team, SDS access, PPE for responders, evacuation zones |
| Medical emergency | First aid/CPR/AED trained responders, first aid supplies location |
| Natural disaster | Earthquake, flood, tornado procedures specific to your region |

### Drill Requirements
- Fire drills: Annually minimum (more frequent in high-risk environments).
- Document all drills: date, time, evacuation time, issues identified.
- Account for all employees at the assembly point.
- Review and update the plan at least annually or when conditions change.
