# Patent Drawings & Specifications
Prepare patent specifications and drawings:

### Specification Sections
1. **Title**: Concise and descriptive.
2. **Cross-reference to related applications**: Priority claims, continuations, divisionals.
3. **Field of the invention**: Technical area.
4. **Background**: Problems with existing solutions. Do NOT admit prior art unnecessarily.
5. **Summary**: Brief description of the invention.
6. **Brief description of drawings**: Reference each figure.
7. **Detailed description**: Best mode of carrying out the invention. Enough detail for enablement and written description.
8. **Claims**: Define the scope of protection.
9. **Abstract**: 150 words max. For search purposes.

### Drawing Requirements (37 CFR 1.84)
- **Black and white line drawings** (standard). Color or photos require a petition.
- Figures must be numbered sequentially (FIG. 1, FIG. 2, etc.).
- Reference numerals on drawings must correspond to the specification.
- Drawing must show every feature recited in the claims.
- Standard sheet sizes: 8.5" × 11" or A4.

### Enablement & Written Description
- **Enablement**: Would a person of ordinary skill be able to make and use the full scope of the claimed invention without undue experimentation?
- **Written Description**: Does the specification convey that the inventor was in possession of the claimed invention at the time of filing?
- Wands factors for undue experimentation: breadth of claims, nature of invention, state of the art, amount of direction/guidance, existence of examples, predictability, quantity of experimentation needed.
