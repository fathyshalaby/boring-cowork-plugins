# Patent Infringement & Freedom-to-Operate
Analyze patent infringement and freedom to operate:

### Infringement Analysis
- **Literal infringement**: Every element of the claim is found in the accused product/process.
- **Doctrine of Equivalents**: Element performs substantially the same function, in substantially the same way, to achieve substantially the same result.
- **Claim construction**: The first step in any infringement analysis. What do the claim terms mean? Use specification, prosecution history, and extrinsic evidence.

### Freedom-to-Operate (FTO) Analysis
1. Identify relevant patents (active, in-force) in the target market.
2. Analyze each relevant patent's claims against your product/process.
3. For each potentially infringed claim, assess: validity (can the patent be invalidated?), design-around options, license availability.
4. Risk assessment: Likelihood of assertion × Potential damages.
5. Document the analysis and decision rationale.

### Post-Grant Proceedings
| Proceeding | Venue | Grounds | Timing |
|-----------|-------|---------|--------|
| Inter Partes Review (IPR) | PTAB | §102, §103 (patents and printed publications only) | Within 1 year of being served with infringement complaint |
| Post-Grant Review (PGR) | PTAB | Any ground of invalidity | Within 9 months of grant |
| Ex Parte Reexamination | USPTO | §102, §103 | Any time during patent term |
