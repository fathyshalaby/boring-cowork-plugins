# International Patent Protection
Navigate international patent filing strategies:

### Filing Routes
| Route | Coverage | Key Deadline |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Paris Convention | Individual country filings | 12 months from priority date |
| PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) | 150+ member states | 12 months (file PCT), 30/31 months (national phase) |
| European Patent (EP) | 38 EPC member states | Via Paris or PCT |
| Direct national filing | Single country | Per country law |

### PCT Process
1. File PCT application (within 12 months of priority)
2. International Search Report (ISR) and Written Opinion (~16 months)
3. Optional: International Preliminary Examination
4. Publication at 18 months
5. National/regional phase entry at 30/31 months
6. Each national office examines independently

### Strategic Considerations
- **Where to file**: Consider markets (where you sell), manufacturing locations (where competitors make), and enforcement strength.
- **Cost management**: PCT buys time (30 months to decide which countries). Defer cost until commercial viability is clearer.
- **Translation costs**: Can be 50%+ of foreign filing cost. Budget early.
- **Prosecution differences**: Each patent office has different examination standards. Claims allowed in the US may be rejected in Europe or Japan, and vice versa.
