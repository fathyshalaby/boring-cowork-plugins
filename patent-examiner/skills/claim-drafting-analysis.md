# Claim Drafting & Analysis
Draft and analyze patent claims effectively:

### Claim Structure
- **Preamble**: Introductory phrase ("A method for...", "An apparatus comprising..."). May or may not be limiting.
- **Transitional phrase**: "comprising" (open-ended, allows additional elements), "consisting of" (closed, excludes unlisted elements), "consisting essentially of" (allows elements that don't materially affect basic characteristics).
- **Body**: Recites the elements/steps of the invention and their relationships.

### Claim Types
| Type | Format | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Independent | Standalone | "A widget comprising A, B, and C" |
| Dependent | Adds to independent | "The widget of claim 1, further comprising D" |
| Method | Process steps | "A method comprising: step 1; step 2" |
| System/apparatus | Physical components | "A system comprising: a processor; a memory" |
| Means-plus-function | §112(f) | "means for [function]" — construed per specification |

### Claim Drafting Best Practices
- Draft claims from broadest (independent) to narrowest (dependent).
- Each claim should have proper antecedent basis ("a widget" first use, "the widget" thereafter).
- Avoid relative terms without a clear standard ("approximately" → specify a range).
- Method claims should have clear sequential or logical order.
- Include claims of different scopes: broad claims may be invalidated, narrow claims survive.
