# Patent Law Fundamentals
Understand the legal framework governing patents:

### Patentability Requirements (35 USC)
| Requirement | Statute | Key Question |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| Patentable Subject Matter | §101 | Is it a process, machine, manufacture, or composition of matter? |
| Utility | §101 | Is it useful? (Low bar — specific, substantial, credible utility) |
| Novelty | §102 | Is it new? (Not anticipated by prior art) |
| Non-Obviousness | §103 | Would it be obvious to a person of ordinary skill in the art? |
| Enablement | §112 | Does the specification teach how to make and use the invention? |
| Written Description | §112 | Does the specification demonstrate possession of the invention? |
| Definiteness | §112 | Are the claims clear and definite? |

### Patent Types
- **Utility patent**: Protects functional aspects. 20 years from filing date.
- **Design patent**: Protects ornamental appearance. 15 years from grant date.
- **Plant patent**: Protects new varieties of asexually reproduced plants. 20 years from filing.

### Key Legal Standards
- **§102 Prior Art**: Anything patented, published, or in public use before the effective filing date. AIA (post-March 16, 2013) = first-inventor-to-file system.
- **§103 Obviousness**: Graham v. John Deere factors: (1) scope/content of prior art, (2) differences between claims and prior art, (3) level of ordinary skill, (4) secondary considerations (commercial success, long-felt need, failure of others).
- **Alice/Mayo test for §101**: (1) Is the claim directed to an abstract idea, law of nature, or natural phenomenon? (2) If yes, does it recite an inventive concept beyond the judicial exception?
