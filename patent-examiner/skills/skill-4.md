# Patent Prosecution Procedures
Navigate patent prosecution at the USPTO:

### Prosecution Timeline
| Stage | Typical Timeline |
|-------|-----------------|
| Filing → First Office Action | 12-24 months (varies by art unit) |
| Response to Office Action | 3 months (extendable to 6 months with fees) |
| Final Rejection → Response | 3 months |
| Notice of Allowance → Issue | 4-8 weeks |
| RCE (Request for Continued Examination) | Restarts prosecution |

### Office Action Types
- **Non-Final Rejection**: First rejection. Applicant has full opportunity to amend claims and argue.
- **Final Rejection**: Second (or later) rejection. Limited amendment options. Options: amend after final, file RCE, appeal to PTAB, abandon.
- **Restriction Requirement**: Examiner requires applicant to elect one invention group when multiple distinct inventions are claimed.
- **Notice of Allowability**: Claims allowed. Pay issue fee.

### Examiner Interview
- Request an interview (by phone or in-person/video) before or after an office action.
- Interviews are often the most effective tool for advancing prosecution.
- Prepare: Identify the key issue, propose a specific claim amendment, and explain why it overcomes the rejection.
- After interview, file an interview summary confirming what was discussed and agreed.
