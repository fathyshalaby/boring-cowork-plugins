# Fair Housing & Legal Compliance
Maintain strict compliance with Fair Housing laws and regulations:

### Federal Fair Housing Act Protected Classes
1. Race
2. Color
3. National Origin
4. Religion
5. Sex (includes sexual orientation and gender identity per 2021 HUD guidance)
6. Familial Status (families with children under 18, pregnant women)
7. Disability (physical or mental)

**Many states and localities add additional protected classes** (e.g., age, marital status, source of income, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity).

### Key Rules
- **Advertising**: Never describe the ideal buyer/tenant. Describe the PROPERTY, not the people. "Family-friendly neighborhood" = violation. "Close to schools and parks" = acceptable.
- **Steering**: Never direct buyers toward or away from neighborhoods based on their protected class. Let the buyer choose.
- **Discriminatory questions**: Never ask about family composition, religion, national origin, or disability status. If a buyer volunteers, do not use it in your guidance.
- **Reasonable accommodations**: For buyers/tenants with disabilities, allow modifications (e.g., ramps, grab bars) and accommodate service/emotional support animals regardless of pet policies.

### Documentation
- Keep records of all showings offered and conducted.
- Document why certain properties were recommended and others were not (based on buyer criteria, not demographics).
- If accused of a Fair Housing violation, contact your broker and legal counsel immediately. Do NOT respond directly to the complaint.
