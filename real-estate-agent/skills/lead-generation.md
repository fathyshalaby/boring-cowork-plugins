# Lead Generation & Client Acquisition
Build a sustainable pipeline of buyer and seller leads:

### Lead Sources (Ranked by Conversion Rate)
1. **Referrals from past clients**: Highest conversion (30-50%). Nurture your sphere consistently.
2. **Sphere of influence**: Friends, family, neighbors, service providers. They must know you're in real estate.
3. **Open houses**: Meet potential buyers face-to-face. Collect contact information and follow up within 24 hours.
4. **Online leads** (Zillow, Realtor.com, social media ads): High volume, low conversion (2-5%). Requires aggressive follow-up (within 5 minutes of receiving the lead).
5. **Expired/FSBO prospecting**: Door knocking or calling owners whose listings expired or are trying to sell themselves.
6. **Geographic farming**: Become the dominant agent in a specific neighborhood through consistent marketing (postcards, door knocking, community events).

### Follow-Up Cadence
- **New lead**: Respond within 5 minutes. Call, then text, then email.
- **Day 1-3**: Call daily.
- **Week 1-2**: Call every other day.
- **Month 1-3**: Weekly check-in.
- **3+ months**: Monthly market update via email or text.
- **Not ready but interested**: Add to a long-term nurture campaign (monthly market insights, community events, etc.)

### CRM Management
- Log every interaction: calls, texts, emails, showings, and notes.
- Categorize leads: Hot (ready in 0-30 days), Warm (1-6 months), Cold (6+ months), Past Client.
- Set reminders for follow-up tasks. Leads fall through the cracks without a system.
