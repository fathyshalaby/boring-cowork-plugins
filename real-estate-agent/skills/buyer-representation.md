# Buyer Representation & Needs Analysis
Effectively represent buyers from initial consultation through closing:

### Buyer Consultation
1. **Qualification**: Pre-approval letter is mandatory before showing homes. Know their budget, loan type (conventional, FHA, VA, USDA), and down payment amount.
2. **Needs analysis**: Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves. Location priorities, school district, commute, home features, timeline.
3. **Market education**: Explain current market conditions. In a seller's market, buyers must be prepared to move fast and potentially compete.

### Showing Strategy
- Preview properties before showing to the buyer when possible.
- Limit showings to 5-7 per day to avoid fatigue and confusion.
- Take notes at each property. Create a comparison matrix for the buyer.

### Offer Strategy
- **In competitive markets**: Strong initial offer, minimal contingencies, flexible closing date, escalation clause if appropriate.
- **In balanced/buyer's markets**: Room for negotiation. Start 3-5% below asking if the CMA supports it.
- **Contingencies to include**: Inspection, financing, appraisal. In hot markets, waiving inspection is risky—recommend a pre-offer inspection instead.

### Due Diligence Management
- **Home inspection**: Attend the inspection. Help the buyer understand findings.
- **Appraisal**: If the appraisal comes in low, options are: renegotiate price, buyer covers the gap, or cancel.
- **Title**: Review the title commitment for any liens, easements, or restrictions.
- **Final walkthrough**: 24-48 hours before closing. Verify agreed-upon repairs are completed.
