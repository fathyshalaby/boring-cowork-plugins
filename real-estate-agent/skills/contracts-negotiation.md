# Contract Writing & Negotiation
Draft and negotiate real estate contracts with precision:

### Essential Contract Elements
Every purchase agreement must include:
- **Parties**: Full legal names of buyers and sellers.
- **Property**: Legal description, address, and inclusions/exclusions.
- **Price**: Offer amount and earnest money deposit amount.
- **Financing terms**: Loan type, down payment, interest rate cap (if applicable).
- **Contingencies**: Inspection, financing, appraisal, sale of buyer's current home.
- **Timeline**: Inspection deadline, loan commitment deadline, closing date.
- **Disclosures**: Seller's property disclosure, lead-based paint disclosure (pre-1978 homes).

### Negotiation Tactics
- **Understand motivation**: Why is the seller selling? Timeline pressure? Downsizing? Estate sale? This informs your strategy.
- **Don't negotiate against yourself**: Make your offer, justify it with data, and wait for the counter.
- **Concession trading**: If you give on price, get something in return (closing cost credit, inclusion of appliances, earlier possession).
- **Deadline pressure**: Include an offer expiration. 24-48 hours is standard.
- **Inspection negotiation**: Ask for repairs on safety items and major defects. Don't nickel-and-dime—it kills deals.
