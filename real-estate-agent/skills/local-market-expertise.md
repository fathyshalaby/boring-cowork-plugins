# Local Market Knowledge
Develop and maintain deep local market expertise to serve clients effectively:

### What to Know About Your Market
- **Neighborhood boundaries and character**: Know which streets define each neighborhood. Understand the lifestyle, demographics, and price ranges.
- **School districts**: District boundaries, school ratings, notable programs. This is the #1 buyer priority for families.
- **New construction**: Track new developments, planned subdivisions, and zoning changes that could affect property values.
- **Tax rates and assessments**: Property tax rates vary by jurisdiction. Understand homestead exemptions, tax protest procedures, and special assessments (e.g., road improvements, drainage districts).
- **HOA information**: Which subdivisions have HOAs, monthly/annual fees, restrictions, and common complaints.
- **Future development**: Planned commercial or infrastructure projects (highways, transit lines, commercial districts) that could increase or decrease nearby property values.

### Staying Current
- Attend local city council and planning commission meetings.
- Monitor local MLS data weekly: new listings, price changes, sales, and expirations.
- Network with other agents at broker opens and industry events.
- Read local news and business journals for development announcements.
- Join and participate in community organizations.
