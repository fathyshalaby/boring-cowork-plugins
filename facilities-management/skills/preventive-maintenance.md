# Preventive Maintenance Program Design
Design and manage comprehensive preventive maintenance (PM) programs for commercial facilities:

### PM Schedule Framework
Organize all building assets into a tiered maintenance schedule:

**Daily Checks** (performed by on-site staff):
- Life safety systems: emergency lighting, fire exits clear, AED accessibility
- Restroom supplies and cleanliness checks
- Common area lighting operational
- HVAC comfort spot-checks (occupant complaints)

**Weekly**:
- Elevator inspection (visual, operation test)
- Parking lot and grounds walkthrough
- Loading dock equipment inspection
- Generator readiness check (battery voltage, fuel level)

**Monthly**:
- HVAC filter replacement/inspection
- Fire extinguisher visual inspection (pin, pressure gauge)
- Plumbing fixture check (leaks, running toilets)
- Pest control vendor visit
- Emergency generator load test (30 minutes under load)

**Quarterly**:
- Fire alarm system testing (with monitoring company)
- HVAC seasonal changeover (heating ↔ cooling)
- Roof inspection (drains, membrane, flashing)
- Backflow preventer testing (certified tester required)

**Annually**:
- Fire suppression system inspection (sprinkler, hood suppression)
- Elevator full-load safety test (certified elevator company)
- Comprehensive roof inspection
- Electrical switchgear thermographic survey
- Air quality testing (if required by lease)

### CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System)
- All PM tasks should be tracked in a CMMS (e.g., Limble, Fiix, or UpKeep).
- Each asset gets a unique ID, location, and linked PM schedule.
- Track: work orders completed, time spent, parts used, and cost per asset.
- Use CMMS data to identify assets with rising maintenance costs (candidates for replacement).
