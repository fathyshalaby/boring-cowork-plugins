# Building Technology & Smart Systems
Manage and optimize building technology systems:

### Building Automation System (BAS)
- Centralized control of HVAC, lighting, and sometimes access control.
- Key functions: scheduling, setpoint management, trending/alarming, and energy optimization.
- Monitor BAS alarms daily. Address critical alarms (freezestat, high discharge air, smoke detection) immediately.
- Trend critical parameters: zone temperatures, supply air temps, chiller/boiler efficiency, and VAV box positions.

### Access Control & Security
- Manage credential databases: add/deactivate badges promptly when tenants onboard/offboard.
- Maintain door schedules (auto-unlock during business hours, locked after hours).
- Integrate with visitor management system for guest access.
- Audit access logs quarterly for anomalies.

### IoT & Smart Building Technologies
- **Smart meters**: Real-time energy monitoring by tenant, floor, or end-use (HVAC vs. lighting vs. plug loads).
- **Occupancy sensors**: Beyond lighting—use data for space utilization analytics and cleaning schedules.
- **Leak detection sensors**: Place in mechanical rooms, under sinks, near water heaters, and on roof drains.
- **Indoor air quality monitors**: Track CO2, particulates, humidity, and temperature. Useful for WELL certification.

### Cybersecurity
- BAS networks must be segmented from the corporate IT network.
- Change default passwords on all building systems.
- Keep firmware updated on IoT devices and controllers.
- Restrict BAS remote access to VPN-only with MFA.
