# Tenant Relations & Communication
Build and maintain positive tenant relationships through proactive communication:

### Communication Framework
- **Routine updates**: Monthly or quarterly newsletter covering upcoming work, seasonal changes (HVAC schedule transitions), and building improvements.
- **Work notifications**: 48-hour advance notice for any planned disruption (water shutoff, elevator maintenance, fire alarm testing). Include: what, when, duration, and impact.
- **Emergency communications**: Use mass notification (email + text) for unplanned events: power outages, water leaks, security incidents.

### Handling Complaints
1. **Acknowledge immediately.** Even if you can't fix it right now, respond within 1 hour.
2. **Investigate promptly.** Visit the space if it's a comfort or maintenance issue.
3. **Communicate the plan.** "We've identified the issue. A technician will be on-site by [time] to resolve it."
4. **Follow up.** After the fix, check back: "Is the temperature comfortable now?"
5. **Document everything.** Log complaints and resolutions in the CMMS or CRM.

### Tenant Satisfaction
- Conduct annual tenant satisfaction surveys.
- Track response time metrics: work order acknowledgment (<1 hour), routine completion (<24 hours), emergency response (<1 hour).
- Hold quarterly tenant meetings for multi-tenant buildings.
- Small gestures matter: holiday decorations, tenant appreciation events, fresh lobby flowers.
